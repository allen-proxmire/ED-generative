## C1 — §14: reframe the BTFR bet (1.4), it is oversold as a weapon

- **Where:** §14 predictions; the "best long-shot, highest payoff" framing of BTFR slope-4 + zero-scatter.
- **Current:** BTFR slope-4 / zero-scatter billed as a top argument-ending bet ("the single cleanest number to miss").
- **Change:** reframe to **consilience + ΛCDM-tension, not an ED-vs-MOND discriminator.** Slope-4 (SPARC V_flat: **3.85 ± 0.09**, Lelli+2019 — consistent, central ~1.7σ below 4) and near-zero intrinsic scatter (**~6%**, consistent with 0) are **shared with MOND**; they discriminate ED+MOND from ΛCDM (which needs ≥0.15 dex intrinsic scatter), not ED from MOND. The **one ED-distinctive claim is `a₀ = cH₀/(2π)`** (MOND fits a₀; ED derives it): predicted **1.04–1.14×10⁻¹⁰** (H₀ 67–74) vs RAR **g† = 1.20 ±0.24(sys)** — consistent, ~10%, but a **postdiction**. The genuine *forward* weapon is **`a₀ tracks H₀`** (the SCBU covariation, 4.8) — MOND's a₀ is fixed, ED's rides the Hubble rate.
- **Why:** done honestly, BTFR is a strong consilience ED passes, not a discriminator against MOND; billing it as a headline weapon overstates it.
- **Backing:** this-session SPARC analysis (Lelli+2016 ApJL 816 L14; Lelli+2019 MNRAS 484 3267; McGaugh+2016 RAR g†). Not yet written as a corpus note.
