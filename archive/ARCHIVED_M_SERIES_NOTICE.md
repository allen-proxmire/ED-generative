## Why archived

External review (Claude B, 2026-05-13) identified that the M-series did not achieve its stated goal of "forcing the substrate primitives from a deeper layer." Specifically:

- **M-1 Route E (Coecke-Kissinger)** invokes a categorical framework that already presupposes complex Hilbert space — circular.
- **M-1 Route A.2 (Frobenius classification)** contains a textually explicit invocation of Paper #1's conclusion as input — circular by the paper's own §3.1 acyclicity claim.
- **All M-papers** rely on the meta-substrate's C*4 empirical-recovery commitment as the shared hidden assumption. Removing C*4 collapses every "forcing route" across all M-papers. The "convergent routes" pattern is one commitment refracted through multiple vocabularies, not over-determined forcing.

The displaced-postulate critique is not eliminated by the M-series — it is *displaced one level up* into the meta-substrate framework. That level is the same kind of structural commitment as the original primitive level, just relocated.

## The pivot

The ED program is reframed as an **axiomatic substrate ontology**:

> *"Here are 13 primitives. Here is what they generate across QM kinematics, gravitational dynamics, soft-matter rheology, black-hole architecture, multiplicity walls, and kernel hierarchies. The empirical case rests on cross-domain reach, not on derivation of the primitives themselves."*

This is the same genre as classical Newtonian mechanics (three laws, posited), special relativity (two postulates, posited), Schrödinger (equation, posited), and the operational-reconstruction tradition (Hardy 2001, CDP 2011, Masanes-Müller 2011, Coecke-Kissinger 2017). None of those frameworks "derived" their axioms; they were judged downstream on what the axioms generate.

The M-series was a band-aid on a non-wound. ED does not need it. The 13 primitives are the starting point; the downstream reach across ~40 orders of magnitude is the empirical case.
