## How to Avoid Circularity

**Rule.** Each M-k primitive-forcing paper must invoke only:

1. Background mathematics (Frobenius classification, Stone's theorem, Shannon-Khinchin axioms, Coecke-Kissinger framework, etc.).
2. The meta-substrate framework $\{C^*\}$ (Meta-Paper M0 §5).
3. **Already-closed** M-j papers with $j < k$.

**Rule.** Downstream Forcing Paper #N's §3.0 may cite M-k once M-k has closed. The citation simply notes "P-primitive now forced upstream by M-k" — it does NOT use M-k's machinery in Paper #N's proofs.

**Check.** When updating Paper #N's §3.0 after M-k closes, the §3.0 text changes but the §§4–10 proofs do not. No circularity introduced — the proofs were valid given the primitive as input; they remain valid given the primitive as upstream-forced.
