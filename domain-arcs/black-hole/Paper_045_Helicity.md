## §3 The Helicity Audit

### 3.1 Schwarzschild: helicity preserved

For non-rotating black holes, the acoustic-metric background has spherical symmetry. V5 cross-chain correlations preserve helicity by P-Helicity-Channel (no helicity-channel coupling in spherically-symmetric substrate).

BHPT scattering off Schwarzschild then preserves helicity (D-via-I).

### 3.2 Kerr: helicity-amplification via superradiance

For rotating black holes, the substrate carries the Kerr-twist axisymmetric rotation (Paper_046). By P-Kerr-Helicity-Coupling, V5 admits helicity-channel coupling proportional to the rotational parameter $a/M$.

Standard superradiance machinery (I-Helicity) applied to this coupling produces helicity-amplification in the frequency / azimuthal-quantum-number regime
$$\omega < m \Omega_H ,$$
where $\Omega_H$ is the horizon angular velocity and $m$ is the azimuthal quantum number.

This is **D-via-I**.

### 3.3 Substrate origin of superradiance

In standard BHPT, superradiance is a curved-spacetime phenomenon arising from the negative-energy modes inside the ergoregion. In the substrate audit, superradiance is a V5 helicity-channel coupling effect — substrate-level structurally distinct but producing the same observational signatures.
