## §3 The Four-Modification Ledger

### 3.1 Mod-1: Horizon = decoupling surface

Paper_039: information does not "fall through" the horizon in the standard sense. Substrate cells inside the horizon become causally decoupled from outside, but they remain in the substrate Hilbert space. The horizon is a partition, not a destruction.

### 3.2 Mod-2: Substrate-corrected Hawking spectrum

Paper_047: the Hawking spectrum at $T_H = \kappa/(2\pi)$ is the leading-order substrate result, with corrections at $(\ell_P/M)^2$ from V5 cutoff (Paper_040). The radiation is **not exactly thermal** at substrate level; the substrate correlations are subleading but present.

### 3.3 Mod-3: V5 Page curve

Paper_050: entanglement entropy of the radiation follows the Page curve via V5 entanglement-bandwidth modulation. Substrate-level entanglement-tracking is consistent with unitarity on the full substrate Hilbert space.

### 3.4 Mod-4: Remnant endpoint

Paper_041: evaporation does not complete; a Planck-mass remnant remains. The final-state Hilbert space retains an information-carrying remnant subspace, preventing the "mixed final state from pure initial state" structure.

### 3.5 Composition: paradox not generated

The standard paradox-generating combination requires all three of: (a) exactly thermal Hawking, (b) complete evaporation, (c) global unitarity violation. Mods 2 + 4 individually break (a) and (b); Mod-3 supplies the unitarity-tracking mechanism. The combination is **not assembled**; the paradox is **not generated**.

This is a **structural** result (A→position), not a constructive resolution of the semiclassical paradox.
