## 3. Scalar-Tensor Acoustic-Metric Covariantization

### 3.1 Substrate-graph acoustic metric

By P-AcousticMetric (§2), substrate-graph kinematic-flow content + cumulative-strain content produces a substrate-level metric structure $g^{\alpha\beta}_{\mathrm{substrate}}$. Under DCGT continuum limit (Paper_073), this coarse-grains to a continuum Lorentzian metric $g^{\alpha\beta}(x)$ with signature $(-+++)$.

**Substrate-level origin of acoustic metric:** the substrate-graph cumulative-strain content (Paper_026) + V1-mediated kinematic propagation produces an emergent metric structure. *(A→analogy, used for exposition only: this structure is **structurally analogous** to the acoustic metric in fluid-mechanics analog-gravity contexts (Unruh 1981, etc.); the analogy is illustrative, not a derivation.)* The substrate-level commitment via P-AcousticMetric is that this emergent metric covers all kinematic content needed for substrate-level gravity in covariantized form.

**This is the substrate-level analog of curved-spacetime geometry, emerging from substrate-graph content.** Not derived from substrate primitives alone; substrate-level commitment via P-AcousticMetric.

### 3.2 Substrate gravitational scalar $\Phi$

The substrate-level gravitational potential $\Phi(R)$ from Paper_026 cumulative-strain reading is generalized to a covariantized scalar field $\Phi(x)$ propagating on the acoustic-metric background:

$$
\Phi_{\mathrm{flat}}(R) = -GM/R \quad \longrightarrow \quad \Phi(x) \text{ on } g_{\mu\nu}(x).
$$

The flat-spacetime Newton (Paper_027) is the weak-field limit of the acoustic-metric covariantized $\Phi$.

### 3.3 Covariantized scalar-tensor field equation

By P-Scalar-Tensor-Form + P-MOND-Interpolation-Covariantization (§2), covariantizing the substrate gravity field equation in the acoustic-metric arena gives:

$$
\boxed{\nabla_\mu\!\left[\mu\!\left(\frac{\sqrt{g^{\alpha\beta}\nabla_\alpha\Phi\,\nabla_\beta\Phi}}{a_0}\right)\nabla^\mu\Phi\right] = 4\pi G\,T}

where:
- $\nabla_\mu$: acoustic-metric covariant derivative.

**Substrate-level structural form derived; numerical content inherited from Papers_027/029.**

### 3.4 Structural cost — deep-MOND superluminality + (C1)–(C3) conditions

**(a) Deep-MOND superluminality:** in the deep-MOND asymptotic regime ($|\nabla\Phi|/a_0 \ll 1$), the substrate-level field equation admits scalar-field modes with superluminal phase velocity. **This is a structural cost** of the scalar-tensor acoustic-metric framework — avoidance routes would violate substrate-level "no-new-primitives" guardrail or break BTFR slope-4.

**(b) OS-positivity + ghost-free + gradient-stable conditions (C1)–(C3):** under specific conditions on the MOND interpolation $\mu(x)$ (positivity in deep-MOND limit; positive-derivative; convexity constraints), the substrate-level scalar-tensor field equation is OS-positive (no negative-norm states), ghost-free (no negative-kinetic-energy modes), and gradient-stable (no exponential mode-growth). **(C1)–(C3) are conditions on $\mu(x)$ that must be checked against empirical MOND interpolation data.**

**Verdict:** conditional-positive — scalar-tensor acoustic-metric covariantization is structurally viable under (C1)–(C3); deep-MOND superluminality is structural cost without simple avoidance.
