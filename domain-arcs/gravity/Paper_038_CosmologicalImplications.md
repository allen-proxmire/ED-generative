## §3 The Three Cosmological Implications

### 3.1 CO-1: Cosmic decoupling surface ↔ Hubble horizon

The cosmic decoupling surface $R_H = c/H_0$ (Paper_028) is structurally the same scale as the Hubble horizon, up to a factor of $2\pi$ inherited from $a_0 = c H_0/(2\pi)$ (Paper_029). The decoupling surface marks the substrate-level boundary beyond which dressed-object propagation decouples from local physics; the Hubble horizon marks the FRW-level boundary beyond which causal influence diminishes.

The numerical coincidence (up to $2\pi$) is **structurally FORCED** by the composition of Papers_028 + 029. This is **D-via-I**.

### 3.2 CO-2: BTFR slope-4 universality with zero intrinsic scatter

By Paper_031 (inherited), substrate gravity predicts BTFR slope-4 $v^4 = G M a_0$ with **zero intrinsic scatter** in the deep-MOND asymptotic. At cosmological scales, this implies that **all** galaxies in the deep-MOND-asymptotic regime should lie on the BTFR with no population-level scatter.

This is a **strong empirical prediction** (CO-2) inherited from Paper_031. Detection of intrinsic scatter (beyond observational error) would refute Paper_031 and propagate here.

### 3.3 CO-3: $a_0$ cosmologically locked to $H_0$

By Paper_029 + Paper_037, $a_0$ is structurally tied to $H_0$ at the substrate level and continuum-invariant. This implies that **changes in $H_0$** (e.g., redshift-evolution, Hubble tension) would propagate to changes in $a_0$. Specifically, if the local-universe $H_0$ differs from the early-universe $H_0$, then $a_0$ should differ correspondingly.

This is **D** (composition of I-029 + I-037) and supplies a structural empirical-prediction channel: $a_0$ at high redshift should differ from local $a_0$ by the same factor as $H_0$.
