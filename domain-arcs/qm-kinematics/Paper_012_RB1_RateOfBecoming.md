## §3 The Postulate Stated

### 3.1 P-RB-1 in full

Let $\Psi$ be a pre-individuation amplitude at substrate location $x$. The local rate of becoming is
$$\dot{\theta}(x) = \frac{\partial \arg \Psi(x, \tau)}{\partial \tau} ,$$
where $\tau$ parameterizes substrate evolution (P13 time homogeneity supplies the $\tau$-translation symmetry).

**(a) Atomic superposition.** Relative phase rate of $c_1 |E_1\rangle + c_2 |E_2\rangle$ coarse-grains to $(E_2 - E_1)/\hbar$.

**(b) Free photon.** V1-kernel retarded propagation rate is substrate $c$; phase rate $\omega = c|k|$.

### 3.2 P-Substrate-c-Constancy

Substrate $c$ is constant under all admissible coarse-graining variations:
- Slow light in dispersive medium — dressed-object.
- Refractive index $n \neq 1$ — dressed-object.
- Gravitational time dilation — acoustic-metric coarse-graining (ED-Phys-10).
- Cosmological redshift — FRW dressed-object effect.

## §4 Coarse-Grainings In Detail

### 4.1 The $E/\hbar$ coarse-graining

For an energy eigenstate $|E\rangle$, $\Psi \sim e^{-i E \tau / \hbar}$, phase rate $E/\hbar$. Bohr-frequency rule $\nu = (E_2 - E_1)/h$ as relative rate. **Inherited** from QM-emergence Phase-1; P-RB-1 declares the substrate coarse-graining.

### 4.2 The substrate-$c$ coarse-graining

Free-photon mode in vacuum: V1-kernel propagation gives substrate $c$. In media with $n > 1$, the dressed-photon propagates slower; substrate $c$ unchanged.
