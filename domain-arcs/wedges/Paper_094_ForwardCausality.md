## Abstract

Forward causality at substrate level follows from a three-primitive composition: (i) **P04 bandwidth-additive structure** supplies the budget constraint that substrate commitments consume; (ii) **P11 commitment-irreversibility** makes consumed budget unrecoverable; (iii) **V1/V5 retardation** (Papers_089/090) propagates information forward only. The composition forces the substrate-primitive structure to be **forward-causal** at the deepest level — not as a derived consequence of dynamics, but as a structural property of the primitive set. Phenomenological forward-causality (Lorentz covariance, signal-propagation, thermodynamic second-law) inherits from this substrate-primitive structure.

## §3 The Argument

### 3.1 Budget-irreversibility synthesis

P04 says substrate bandwidth is non-negative additive — a budget. P11 says commitments are irreversible — once consumed, budget is gone.

Together, P04 + P11 produce a **monotonic** substrate process: substrate commitments accumulate forward in time, never backward.

### 3.2 V1 + V5 retardation

V1 retarded kernel (Paper_089) and V5 forward-memory (Paper_090) implement this monotonicity at the rule-type level: kernels propagate information forward only.

### 3.3 Primitive-level forward causality

By P-Primitive-Forward-Causality, this is the substrate-level commitment: forward-causality is **not derived** from substrate dynamics; it is the structural property of the primitive set itself.

This is structurally distinct from forward-causality being **emergent** at coarse-grained scales — it is **primitive** at substrate level.

### 3.4 Phenomenological inheritance

By P-Causality-Propagation, phenomenological forward-causality (Lorentz light-cone, retarded electromagnetic potentials, thermodynamic second-law, etc.) **inherits** from the substrate-primitive structure under DCGT coarse-graining.

Standard physics's various "arrows of time" (radiative, thermodynamic, cosmological) all trace back, at substrate level, to the same primitive-forward-causality.

## §4 Falsification Criteria

- **F1:** Substrate evidence of backward-in-time information transmission at primitive level — refutes P-Primitive-Forward-Causality.
- **F2:** Phenomenological arrow not derivable from substrate primitive forward-causality — refutes P-Causality-Propagation (or requires extension).
- **F3:** P04 + P11 composition failing to produce monotonic substrate process — refutes the substrate framework.
