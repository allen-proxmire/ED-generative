# Here Is the Knife
### Every Way Event Density Could Be Wrong

*Allen Proxmire*

*Series: Event Density, Part 11, the last. Companion papers: `Predictions_and_Falsifiers`, `22_Ways_to_Kill_Event_Density`, `Paper_056_ClassA_Wall`.*

---

For ten parts I have handed you reasons. Why the electron turns twice, why there are three forces, why a black hole is warm, why galaxies spin the way they do. If a person wanted to be suspicious of all of it, and a person should, the suspicion has a name. Anyone can build a story that explains what we already see. The test of a theory is not how much it explains. It is whether it can *die*.

A crank theory explains everything and forbids nothing. You can never catch it, because it was never standing anywhere you could aim. A real theory is the opposite. It walks out into the open, points at specific things that have not been measured yet, and says: if it comes out *this* way, I am wrong, and you should throw me out. So this final part is not another reason. It is the knife. Every way I know of to kill Event Density, laid on the table, most of them checkable against data that already exists or experiments already running.

Try to kill it. I mean that.

## The sharpest one first

There is a number in gravity called alpha-two. It measures whether a preferred cosmic frame leaks into how gravity behaves, whether a body's gravity depends a little on how it is moving through the universe. Most theories that single out a cosmic "now," and Event Density does single one out, predict this number is small but nonzero, and that has killed a long line of them.

Event Density predicts alpha-two is not small. It is *exactly zero*. Not approximately, not below a bound, zero, as a structural consequence of how sparse the substrate's commitment is. And that is the cleanest kill switch in the whole program, because "exactly zero" is the easiest thing in the world to disprove. Lunar laser ranging, pulsar timing, the precise clocks physics already runs, need only find alpha-two to be *anything other than zero*, at any level, and Event Density is finished. One nonzero digit and it is over. I do not get to negotiate. That is what handing you the knife looks like.

## The ones you can check today

Some kills need no new machine. The data is already sitting in a catalog.

Galaxies obey a relation between how much ordinary matter they hold and how fast their outer edges rotate. Event Density predicts that relation has a slope of exactly four, with no intrinsic scatter, in the low-acceleration limit. Not "about four." Four. There is a public catalog of galaxies, called SPARC, where anyone can check this right now. If the slope is not four, or if there is real scatter the theory forbids, that is a kill, available this afternoon.

Same for the acceleration scale where galaxies start misbehaving, the one Event Density builds from the cosmic expansion rate rather than fitting to galaxies. Wide binary stars, two stars orbiting each other far apart, drift into exactly that low-acceleration regime, and the European Gaia mission has measured them by the thousands. If those wide pairs obey plain Newtonian gravity with no anomaly at that scale, the derivation is wrong. The measurement is being fought over as I write this, which is exactly where a live prediction should be.

## The forbidding predictions, which are the strong ones

Now the kind I care about most, because they are the kind nothing else makes. A theory earns real credit not when it reproduces what we know, but when it *forbids* something other theories happily allow. Here are two.

There should be no stable fundamental force larger than the strong force. In the standard mathematics of forces, there is nothing special about the sizes one, two, and three that we see, electromagnetism, the weak, the strong. Bigger ones are allowed on paper, and physicists write them down freely. Event Density says the stable ones stop at three, because of a counting argument about how many channels can stay distinct at once. Find one stable fundamental force bigger than the strong force, a stable large gauge sector, and this whole line is dead. Standard physics permits it. Event Density bars it. That gap is the prediction.

And there should be no parity-violating version of electromagnetism. The weak force is famously left-handed, it tells left from right. Electromagnetism is not, and in Event Density it *cannot* be, because the single-channel structure that carries it is blind to handedness by construction. General gauge theory allows a handed electromagnetic-type force. Event Density forbids it. Discover one, and again, over. These are the predictions that would mean something if they hold, because they are not ED agreeing with the textbook. They are ED sticking its neck out where the textbook stays silent.

## The ones the lab is coming for

Some kills are five or ten years out, but the machines are being built now.

The clearest is the quantum-to-classical wall. Quantum mechanics says a molecule can be in two places at once, and experimenters demonstrate it by sending ever-larger molecules through an interferometer and watching them interfere with themselves. The molecules keep getting heavier, tens of thousands of atomic mass units and climbing. Event Density predicts that this does not continue forever, and, more importantly, that it stops in a *specific way*: a sharp wall, not a gentle fade, and a wall that sits at the same place regardless of the platform's fine details. I want to be honest about the tiers here, because it is easy to oversell. The *sharp, architecture-independent wall* is the real structural prediction. The specific mass, somewhere around 140 to 250 thousand atomic mass units, is an *extrapolation* from today's data, not a derivation, and if the wall shows up at fifty thousand or five hundred thousand instead, the framework re-anchors around the same shape. What it cannot survive is the *wrong shape*: clean interference sailing past that regime with no wall at all, or a soft gradual fade instead of a sharp edge. And here is why it matters: ordinary environmental decoherence, the standard explanation for why big things look classical, does not predict a sharp architecture-independent wall. That specific shape is Event Density's, and the interferometry groups are climbing straight toward it.

There is a second one down there too. A black hole's radiation should not be exactly thermal, exactly featureless; Event Density predicts a faint, sign-specific deviation. We cannot poke a real black hole, but we can build analogs, sound horizons in flowing fluids and cold-atom clouds, that carry the same mathematics. If those analog horizons come out perfectly thermal, with none of the predicted deviation, that is a kill, and those experiments run in labs today.

## The honest parts, including where it already limps

A finale owes you the uncomfortable items, not just the brave ones.

Some of these predictions have already been *checked, and passed*. Light bends by exactly the factor of two general relativity requires; gravitational waves travel at exactly the speed of light, confirmed when we saw light and waves arrive together from a neutron-star merger. I do not list these as victories. I list them so the bar is clear: if any of them had come out wrong, the theory would already be dead, and it isn't. That is the floor, not the ceiling.

One of the predictions rests on an *open premise*, and I will not hide it behind the others. The argument that space has three dimensions because the arrow needs to lock its order into a knot, which I made back in part five, depends on the substrate actually holding its order by that kind of knotting, and that specific step is still under active test in my own simulations, and it has not closed. It might not. If it fails, the three-dimensions argument loses its foundation, and I would have to say so out loud. I am saying so now.

And Event Density already carries *walls it cannot pass*, which is the strangest kind of honesty a theory can offer. It cannot build a soliton, a wave that holds its shape forever, from its bottom rule; we tested it, and the substrate disperses the wave instead. It provably cannot reach the deep structure of the prime numbers with finite memory. A theory that maps its own failures, that can point at the places it does not reach, is a theory that has coordinates. Cranks have no coastline. This one drew its own.

## What this whole thing is, in the end

So let me close the series where an honest theory has to close: not on how much it explains, but on how easily it dies.

Event Density is not offered to you as proven. It is offered as *killable*. Nearly everything in this list is checkable against data that exists or experiments already running, and any single one of them coming back the wrong way takes the whole structure down, cleanly and visibly. Exactly-zero must stay zero. The slope must be four. No force may be larger than the strong one, no electromagnetism may be handed, the quantum wall must be sharp and it must be there. Miss any of those, and I do not get to save it with an epicycle. That is the deal a real theory signs, and a crank theory never will.

The whole series has been one primitive, the arrow of time laid at the foundation, wearing a different face in every part. The final face is this one: a theory brave enough to be wrong, that hands you the reasons in ten parts and the knife in the eleventh. I would rather have built something that can die than something you can only admire. If it lives, it will be because the universe kept coming back with the answers it was told, in advance, it needed. And if it dies, it will die the way a real idea should, on a number, in the open, where everyone can see.

Here is the knife. I hope you use it.
