# Cost and Benefit
### What each Event Density move asks you to give up, and what it gives back

This is the Harmon story circle run in miniature, over and over. A character leaves the ordinary world, crosses a threshold, pays a price in the unknown, and returns transformed, carrying the reward home. Every Event Density move has that shape: you **give up** a comfortable belief — the *cost*, the Take — and you **come back** with a resolution — the *benefit*, the reward. The table below is the ledger of those trades.

They sort under ED's two roots — **the arrow** (time is the act of commitment) and **the finite relational substrate** (a grained, related world with no true infinities). The single biggest cost, the macro-Take the whole book turns on, is the last one in Root 2: giving up the smooth continuum.

---

## Root 1 — The Arrow: time is the act of commitment

| | The cost — what you give up | The benefit — what you get back |
|---|---|---|
| **Time** | the timeless block universe; time as a mere coordinate, reality as fundamentally reversible | time is real and fundamental — one arrow does every job at once: becoming, causality, measurement, gravity's direction |
| **Measurement** | the tidy all-unitary picture, smooth reversible evolution as the whole story | the measurement problem dissolves — it is just commitment; no observer, no collapse bolted on |
| **Entanglement** | two separate particles sitting at two separate places | no spooky action, no nonlocality paradox — one shared, pre-spatial, still-uncommitted state |
| **The quantum→classical line** | "mass alone (or the environment alone) decides what turns classical" | a sharp threshold you can compute and test — internal complexity crossing `M_crit` (the 140–250 kDa bet) |
| **Gravity** | the dream of quantizing the metric; gravity as a fundamental force | gravity is emergent geometry — the arrow made spatial; it unifies with time and measurement, nothing to quantize |
| **The wavefunction** | the wavefunction as what reality fundamentally *is* | it is emergent bookkeeping — the paradox never arises at the bottom layer |

## Root 2 — The finite relational substrate: a grained, related world, no true infinities

| | The cost — what you give up | The benefit — what you get back |
|---|---|---|
| **Dimensions** | 3D as a brute given, or one landscape choice among many | 3+1 forced and explained — one fewer free parameter, and it falls out from several independent roads |
| **Dark matter** | one clean universal answer (a single dark particle, everywhere) | galaxies without a particle (MOND) **and** a committed relic for clusters and the CMB — matching how the data actually splits |
| **Singularities** | the smooth continuum itself — the manifold *(the macro-Take)* | no infinities — singularities and the Big Bang become finite substrate events; Penrose's cold cyclic endpoints kept |
| **Information** | the fundamental bit; infinite precision | information as relational contrast, grounded in "two before one"; the bit is its shadow |

---

Read down the middle column and you have the price of admission; read down the right and you have what ED pays you for it. The whole framework is that trade made a dozen times: hand over an assumption you mistook for a fact, and get back a mystery that dissolves or an infinity that was never there.

*Honest footing: most of the rewards are standing structural results of the framework; the `M_crit` / 140–250 kDa boundary is a pre-registered, not-yet-tested prediction (ED still shares ordinary environmental decoherence — the distinctive claim is that the irreversible commitment fires at an internal-complexity threshold); and the singularity line is substrate regularization plus resonance with CCC, not a refutation of the singularity theorems, which are airtight in the continuum ED says is only the shadow. The right-hand column is a reward the framework earns, not one it asserts.*
