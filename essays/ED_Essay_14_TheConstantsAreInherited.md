# The Constants Are Inherited
### Units first, numbers later — a walk through what the universe brought with it

Every physics textbook keeps a table of fundamental constants — the settings of reality, the numbers you have to feed in by hand before the equations will run. We treat them as a single flat list, dialed in once at the beginning. Event Density says they are not one list but a *family with two ages*, and the line between the two ages is sharp. It is the line between a **unit** and a **number**.

## The line

Some constants are **dimensionful** — the speed of light is a length divided by a time, Planck's constant is an action, the Planck length is a length. Their numerical value depends entirely on whether you measure in meters or miles, seconds or years. Choose your units well (physicists call them *natural units*) and every one of them becomes simply **1**. They carry no information about *which* universe you live in. They are scaffolding — a clock, a ruler, an action-scale.

Other constants are **dimensionless** — pure numbers, the same for any observer in any units. The fine-structure constant, ≈ 1/137, that sets how strongly light couples to charge. The ratio of the proton's mass to the electron's, ≈ 1836. These are the *real* physics — the numbers a distant civilization would write down and agree with us on, letter for letter. They are what make our universe *this* one and not some other.

And here is ED's claim, the whole essay in a sentence: **the units are there at the beginning; the numbers are inherited late.**

## The three the universe was born with

Look at a single event — one tick of the arrow, one participation committing. It already carries exactly three scales.

**c — the rate of becoming.** How fast a commitment at one place reaches the next. It is the conversion between the tick and the hop, the speed of the arrow itself. A length over a time — a unit. Present the instant one event can touch another.

**ħ — the grain of memory.** How much happens in one commitment. It sets the phase a quantum system carries, and the phase is exactly what the system *remembers* — the record of where it has been (interference is memory of both paths; decoherence is forgetting). ħ is the smallest unit of that record, the pixel of remembering. An action — a unit. Present in the first tick.

**ℓ_P — the grain of space.** The size of one hop, the resolution of the substrate. A length — a unit. Present wherever there is a hop at all.

A clock, an action, a ruler: **c, ħ, ℓ_P.** The going, the keeping, the room. In natural units, all three equal 1. So the universe opens with units fully in hand and *not a single characterful number to its name* — nothing yet to argue about.

## G — the first thing to fall out, and only a shadow

Give the universe those three and one more constant appears immediately, for free: Newton's *G*, which is just `c³ℓ_P²/ħ` — the three units, rearranged. It is the first constant to *emerge*, and also the least fundamental one on the whole list: it adds no new information, it appears nowhere outside gravity (it is absent from the entire Standard Model), and it is only an exchange rate between two things that are themselves emergent — mass and curvature. Gravity's own constant is not the first *thing*. It is the first *shadow*. (That story has its own essay — *The First Shadow*.)

## The rest — the inheritance

Everything with genuine personality comes later, and comes from somewhere:

- **The particle masses and the couplings** — including α ≈ 1/137 and the mass ratios — belong to the matter sector, and matter is *built*: bound, self-sustaining structure that takes accumulation. No matter yet, no masses yet.
- **The galactic acceleration a₀** needs a whole grown cosmos with a horizon to define it — it is `cH₀/(2π)`, and it is so late that it is *still changing* as the universe expands.
- **The cosmological constant Λ** is a saturation floor of that same late, stretched-out cosmos.

These are the numbers we build careers measuring, and every one of them is a latecomer — grown into as the universe fills with matter and stretches out room.

## What "inherited" honestly means

Here is the part I want to be straight about, because it is where a lesser theory would overreach. **ED does not derive these numbers from nothing.** It does not hand you 1/137 out of pure logic. Some physicists dream of a final theory that would — ED is not claiming to be that, and it would be a cheat to pretend otherwise. ED *inherits* its constants, the same as every theory does.

What ED offers instead is more modest, and I think more honest: it tells you the **order** and the **kind**. It shows you which constants are mere units, present from the first tick and carrying no real information (c, ħ, ℓ_P), and which are the universe's earned personality, accumulated only once there is matter and a cosmos to hold them (the masses, the couplings, a₀, Λ). And where it *can* show a constant is downstream, it does — G from the units, a₀ from the horizon — trimming the list of things that have to be assumed. The irreducible dimensionless core it hands you as given, and says so plainly.

## The point

So the constants are not one flat row of dials set at the dawn of time. They are a family with two ages: the bare units the universe was *born* with, and the characterful numbers it *grew into*. And the thing we habitually call fundamental — the deep, the exotic, the given — turns out to be mostly the second kind. Not the birth. The inheritance.
