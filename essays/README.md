# Event Density — Essays

Public-facing explainer essays for the Event Density (ED) program. Each essay is a plain-language, cold-reader-accessible companion to one or more corpus papers: the paper carries the derivation and the audit, the essay carries the story. Essays are for the video series (NotebookLM feeds on the clean `.md`), for outreach, and as standalone reads. They are not derivations, and they never claim more than their source papers do.

## The Essay Standard

Every essay in this folder is held to the same discipline the papers are, translated for a general reader. An essay is in good standing when it meets all seven:

1. **Anchored.** It names its companion paper(s) up front, and every substantive claim traces to one. No claim in an essay that isn't backed by a paper in the corpus.
2. **Honestly tiered.** It reflects the source's verdict tier and never upgrades it. A *structural* result is not narrated as a *derivation*; an *account* is not narrated as a *proof*; a *measured* negative is reported as a negative. Speculation is marked as speculation, in the essay's own voice.
3. **No overclaim.** It never says ED "derives X from nothing" or "forces X" unconditionally. The thirteen primitives are carried, not proven, and the essay says so where it matters. Where a number appears, it distinguishes **form-forced** (the substrate fixes the law's shape / a scale relation) from **value-inherited** (the raw number is measured, e.g. H₀, ℓ_P).
4. **Killable.** It states at least one way the claim could be wrong: a falsifier, a control case, a measurement that would end it. A story you can kill is worth more than a fact you just accept.
5. **Grounded in the substrate.** Jargon is translated to plain language per step. It does not let an emergent shadow (a "field," a "metric," "temperature") quietly become a primitive. It tests ED's real claims, not a hand-built stand-in.
6. **In-voice and clean.** Warm, first-person, specific, philosophical where earned. Em-dashes minimized (commas / periods / colons / parentheses instead). No Wolfram / Ruliad references in public-facing material. Safe lineage comparisons only ('t Hooft, causal sets, Penrose CCC where apt).
7. **Standalone.** A reader who has seen none of the papers can follow it, and leaves knowing what ED claims, at what confidence, and where the honest edges are.

## Evaluation

`EVALUATION.md` records each essay's status against the standard: **keep** (in good standing), **amend** (keep the essay, fix specific items), or **flag** (a substantive accuracy or tiering problem to resolve before it ships). The evaluation is re-run when essays are added or the corpus shifts under them.
