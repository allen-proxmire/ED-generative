# ED-10 — Round 2: Horizon Dynamics and Causal Classification

**Causal-structure dynamics analysis. Not a GR derivation, not a corpus edit, not a new primitive.**
Extends ED-10 Round 1 (the structural unification `b→0 = A2 cut = A1 capacity-0 = metric degeneration = V5 horizon`) to the *dynamics* of formation, permanence, and leakage under the admissible commitment-driven bandwidth rule.
**Framing:** the corpus already has Hawking-via-V5 (040/047) and BH entropy; this round *unifies* those with the A1/A2/bandwidth-dynamics results. Qualitative throughout — quantitative rates are Round 3, and horizon *placement* still needs the EFE keystone.

## 1. Formation dynamics

The admissible rule (Round 2) drains the commitment-reserve band as commitments accumulate, with `Γ_commit ~ b_int/reserve`. As reserve drains, `Γ_commit` **rises** — a **positive feedback**. Against it runs **adjacency-band influx** (P05 transport of bandwidth from neighbors). Formation is therefore a **local threshold instability**, a reaction–diffusion competition between a local commitment *sink* and a diffusive *influx*:

- **Rapid formation:** local commitment density above threshold → drain outpaces influx → the positive feedback takes over → **accelerating runaway collapse** to `b=0` (gravitational-collapse-like).
- **Slow/marginal:** near threshold, drain ≈ influx → slow, possibly stalled formation (a proto-horizon).
- **Non-formation:** below threshold → influx sustains `b>0` → no horizon (sub-critical, like sub-Chandrasekhar mass).

So horizon formation is **local + threshold-based**, an instability — not a smooth global process. A horizon nucleates wherever local commitment density crosses the runaway threshold.

## 2. Stability and permanence

Once an edge reaches `b=0` it is **decoupled** (inadmissible) → no front crosses → no P05 influx can reach it → it **cannot be replenished**. Combined with the monotone drain (P11: commitment-reserve consumed irreversibly), a fully-collapsed surface is **frozen — classically permanent.** No admissible `F` allows recovery of a *fully-formed* (`b=0`) horizon: Round 2 ruled out replenishing forms, and even an influx-based recovery is blocked because the surface has isolated itself. **Partial** horizons (`b` small but `>0`) remain influx-connected → **recoverable** (sub-critical proto-horizons). So permanence is sharp: `b=0` → frozen; `b>0` → reversible. Classical permanence (P11 + self-isolation) is then distinct from the slow V5 leak (§3).

## 3. V5 finite-memory leakage — the Hawking analogue

A `b=0` surface blocks classical V1 propagation (Round 1). But V5 mediates cross-chain correlations with **finite memory `τ_V5 ~ ℓ_P/c`** — the corpus's Hawking-cutoff mechanism (040/047). As the surface forms, cross-chain-correlated event pairs straddle it; the finite memory lets a correlation "leak" across over time `~τ_V5` — the ED form of horizon pair-production.

- **Symmetry tracks causal type.** For a **one-way (BH) horizon**, the V1 cone makes the leak **asymmetric** → net outward flux = **Hawking radiation**, and the surface slowly **evaporates**. For a **two-way (cosmological) horizon**, the leak is **symmetric** → a thermal "temperature" with **no net flux** (de Sitter-like), and the surface does **not** evaporate (it is expansion-supported, not mass-supported).
- **Rate.** Leakage is **rate-limited by `τ_V5`** (the cutoff) and modulated by the steepness of `b→0` at the surface (a **surface-gravity analogue**). Quantification is Round 3.
- **Compatibility with A1 — the key reconciliation.** A1's **capacity = 0** was measured for the **classical V1 channel** across a structural cut. V5 leakage is a **separate, finite-memory (quantum) channel**, "beneath" the classical cut. So they coexist with no contradiction: **the horizon is classically opaque (A1 capacity 0) and quantum-mechanically slowly radiating (V5 leak)** — exactly the black-hole picture (classically nothing escapes; Hawking radiation leaks quantum-mechanically). ED reproduces the classical/quantum opacity split structurally: **A1 = classical opacity, V5 = quantum leakage.**
- **Effect on type.** Leakage does not change the *instantaneous* causal type; over evaporation timescales it **dissolves** a one-way (mass-supported) horizon, while leaving a two-way (expansion-supported) horizon intact.

## 4. Causal-type classification (refining Round 1)

Round 1 had two types; the dynamics add a third (partial):

| Collapse | V1 cone | V5 leakage | A1 signature | Causal type |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **two-way** (reciprocal `b=0`) | blocked both directions | symmetric, thermal, **no net flux** | capacity 0 **both ways** | cosmological/domain horizon = **determinability boundary** |
| **one-way** (directional `b=0`) | blocked outward, open inward | **asymmetric, net outward** (Hawking) | capacity 0 **outward only** | **black-hole horizon** (evaporates) |
| **partial** (`b` small, `>0`) | redshifted but **crossable** | proto/marginal | capacity **small, nonzero** | **semi-permeable / proto-horizon** (recoverable) |

The V1 retarded cone supplies the directionality that splits two-way from one-way; the partial class is the *forming/sub-critical* surface where `b>0` so A1's *exact* zero does not yet hold.

## 5. Determinability boundaries vs causal horizons

- **Coincidence.** A two-way (reciprocal) horizon **is** an A1 determinability boundary — capacity 0 both ways. So **A1 determinability boundaries = the two-way subclass of causal horizons.**
- **Causal without (symmetric) determinability.** A one-way (BH) horizon is a causal horizon but **not** an A1 *symmetric*-capacity-0 boundary; it is a **directional** determinability boundary (the interior is undeterminable from outside, but not vice versa). So the general notion is *determinability = causal horizon*, with A1 having measured the symmetric instance.
- **The observational-vs-transmission distinction.** A1's finding — capacity 0 (no transmission) yet correlation persists via **common cause (shared past)** — is exactly the horizon-with-shared-origin structure: regions disconnected *now* but correlated through a common earlier contact. For a two-way (cosmological) horizon this is the **CMB/horizon-problem** structure; for a one-way (BH) horizon it is the interior–exterior shared-origin (a complementarity-flavored statement). So "observational, not transmission" is *the* signature distinguishing a horizon (no causal transmission) from a mere channel (transmission) — and the common-cause residue is what every horizon-with-a-shared-past carries.

## 6. Verdict

**Under the admissible commitment-driven bandwidth rule, horizon dynamics are coherent and reproduce the qualitative black-hole/cosmological phenomenology — without full EFE.** Horizons **form** by a local threshold instability (positive feedback `Γ_commit~b_int/reserve` vs adjacency influx; runaway collapse above threshold, sub-critical below); are **permanent** once fully formed (`b=0`: monotone drain + self-isolation from influx, P11); and **leak** slowly via V5 finite memory — asymmetric/evaporating for one-way (Hawking), symmetric/stable for two-way (de Sitter-like). A1's classical capacity-0 **coexists** with V5 quantum leakage (the classical/quantum opacity split). Determinability boundaries are the two-way subclass; one-way horizons are directional. This unifies the corpus's existing V5 horizons + Hawking (040/047) with the A1/A2/bandwidth-dynamics program into one dynamical picture. **What still needs the EFE keystone:** all *quantitative* statements — the Hawking *rate/temperature*, the evaporation *time*, the area law, and the horizon *placement* (radius from mass). Qualitative dynamics: yes without EFE; numbers: Round 3 + Phase-3 GR.
