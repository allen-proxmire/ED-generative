## 1. What decides the count: the gauge group

The propagating degrees of freedom of a gravity theory are fixed by its gauge symmetry. **General Relativity** has full 4D **diffeomorphism invariance**: of the metric's 10 components, 4 are coordinate gauge and 4 are non-propagating constraints (lapse + shift, fixed by the Hamiltonian and momentum constraints), leaving **2 propagating** — the transverse-traceless (TT) tensor modes, the two polarizations of a gravitational wave. A theory with **less** gauge symmetry removes fewer components and therefore propagates **more** modes. So the count is decided by: *how much gauge symmetry does ED's gravitational sector have?*

## 2. ED's gravitational field

The field is the per-edge bandwidth `b_ij`. It is a **symmetric** rank-2 tensor (P02 reciprocity: `b_ij = b_ji`, shared edge record), so in the continuum it is a symmetric rank-2 tensor field — the same 10-component object as the metric, with `g_ij ~ b_ij⁻¹`, lapse `N² ~ b` (trace/scale, R8), and shift `g_0i` from directed flux (R4 §2). On component-count alone, `b_ij` carries exactly the metric's content and no manifest extra field — which is why R9 left the door open to pure Einstein. The count is not decided by components; it is decided by **gauge**, and that is where ED differs.

## 3. The decisive structure: ED's arrow is in the *law*, not the boundary conditions

Here is the fact that settles it. **GR's arrow of time lives in the boundary conditions** — the field equations are time-symmetric and fully diffeomorphism-invariant; a solution acquires an arrow only from its initial/thermodynamic conditions. **ED's arrow lives in the law itself**: P11 (irreversible commitment) and P13 (the homogeneous substrate tick) make the time-direction a **primitive of the dynamics**. The substrate singles out a **preferred foliation** — the slicing by ticks, oriented by the commitment arrow — at the level of the rules, not the solution.

A preferred foliation baked into the law **breaks full 4D diffeomorphism invariance down to foliation-preserving diffeomorphisms** (spatial diffeos on each slice + time reparametrization). That is *exactly* the gauge group of **khronometric gravity** — the hypersurface-orthogonal Einstein-aether theory, equivalently the low-energy limit of **Hořava gravity** — in which a scalar field (the "khronon" `T`, whose gradient defines the preferred time) supplements the metric.

## 4. The count: 2 tensor + 1 scalar — khronometric, not GR

With foliation-preserving rather than full diffeomorphism gauge, the standard counting gives **2 tensor (TT) modes + 1 scalar mode** = **3 propagating degrees of freedom**. The extra scalar is the **khronon** — the propagating preferred-time field — and in ED it is precisely the substrate's arrow/foliation made dynamical. So:

> **ED's gravity is khronometric (Hořava-class): Einstein's tensor sector plus a preferred-foliation scalar — not pure General Relativity.**

This **resolves the R9 residual.** Lovelock's "built solely from the metric, no extra field" condition **fails**: the khronon is the extra field. So Lovelock does not force pure Einstein; it places ED in the **scalar-tensor family, specifically the khronometric subclass**, with the scalar fixed in origin (the arrow) rather than added by hand. The mode count is decided — and decided by P11/P13, the most ED-specific structure there is.

## 5. Reconciliation with Rounds 7–8 (and why they still stand)

Khronometric gravity **shares GR's static, spherically-symmetric weak-field metric** — the Schwarzschild form — so the R7/R8 results (factor-of-2 light bending, redshift, the `g_00 g_rr ~ −1` relation) are *unchanged*: ED is **observationally GR-like in every regime those tests probe.** The difference between ED-khronometric and pure GR shows up elsewhere: **preferred-frame effects** (PPN parameters `α₁, α₂`), a possibly **different gravitational-wave sector** (the scalar mode; tensor speed), and **Lorentz violation** at high energy. So Rounds 7–8 are not contradicted — they are the sector where khronometric and Einstein agree. Round 10 locates the sector where they *differ*.

## 6. The deep consistency — the arrow's fingerprint, again

This is the payoff, and it ties the GR arc to the whole program. ED's gravity is khronometric **for the same reason** ED cannot cast a time-symmetric PDE shadow (CoarseGrain arc) and the same reason its arrow is primitive rather than emergent: **the arrow is in the law.** Pure GR — with full diffeomorphism invariance and no preferred time — is precisely the **over-symmetric "forgetful shadow"** that would have to *erase* ED's arrow to exist. So it is not a defect that ED misses pure GR; it would be a *contradiction* if it hit it. **ED gravity should be khronometric** — Einstein's tensor physics carrying the indelible mark of the primitive arrow — and that is exactly what the mode count returns. The same structural fact (arrow-in-the-law) that forbade the time-symmetric continuum forbids full diffeomorphism invariance; both are the arrow refusing to be averaged away.

## 7. Falsifiability — distinctive, tight, live

Being khronometric rather than pure-GR makes ED **more** falsifiable, not less:

- **GW170817** (tensor gravitational-wave speed = light speed to ~10⁻¹⁵) tightly constrains the khronometric tensor sector — a hard bound ED's `b`-dynamics must satisfy.
- **Preferred-frame PPN** (`α₁, α₂`) must lie within solar-system / pulsar bounds.
- **The scalar (breathing) GW polarization** is a concrete, observable signature distinguishing ED from GR.
- **Universal horizons.** Khronometric black holes possess *universal horizons* (trapping even the instantaneously-propagating khronon), a structure beyond GR's Killing horizons — and ED's horizon is exactly the `b → 0` locus (R4 §6: `b→0` = metric degeneration = A2 cut = V5 horizon). **ED's `b→0` horizons are candidate khronometric universal horizons** — a third deep unification of the same locus.
