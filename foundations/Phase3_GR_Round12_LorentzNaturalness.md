## 1. A self-correction of Round 11

R11 called ED's cosmic-frame alignment a "natural suppressor" of the preferred-frame parameters. That was imprecise, and honesty requires fixing it: the preferred frame being the **CMB rest frame** makes ED's preferred frame *observationally well-defined* and makes the PPN bounds *applicable as derived* — but it does **not** make the theory parameters `α₁, α₂` small. Those are intrinsic couplings; their smallness is a *separate* requirement. So the cosmic-frame point settles *which* frame, not *how small* the coupling. The smallness is the real problem, and it is the **Lorentz-naturalness problem** — the frontier where every theory of this class lives.

## 2. The constraint that actually kills Lorentz-violating gravity

The PPN parameters (`α₁ ≲ 10⁻⁴`, `α₂ ≲ 10⁻⁷`) are *not* the tightest constraint on preferred-frame physics. The lethal bounds are in the **matter sector**: differential Lorentz violation between species (photon vs electron cones, vacuum Čerenkov, clock-comparison, time-of-flight) is constrained to **`10⁻²⁰` and beyond.** And in any theory where gravity violates Lorentz invariance, that violation **percolates** into the matter sector through loops — generically at order one, *unless* protected. This percolation is what makes Hořava / Einstein-aether theories fragile: keeping matter-sector LV below `10⁻²⁰` while gravity-sector LV is `O(1)` is a severe, radiatively-unstable fine-tuning. **This, not the PPN bounds, is the usual executioner.**

## 3. ED's structural escape — universal, not differential, violation

Here ED's defining feature — *matter and geometry are the same substrate* — turns the executioner aside, and it does so **independent of `F`.** The deadly bounds are on **differential** LV: *different* species having *different* cones. ED has only one transport mechanism (P05 adjacency) and one maximal speed (the bandwidth-limit speed). Every propagating thing — light, electrons, gravitational waves, the khronon — is a substrate process through the *same* P05 transport, saturating the *same* cone (R11). So ED's Lorentz violation is **universal**: one deformed cone shared by all sectors. And universal LV is, at leading order, **removable by a single coordinate/field rescaling** — it is *unobservable*. The `10⁻²⁰` matter-sector bounds constrain *differential* LV, which ED does not produce at leading order.

> **ED's unification protects it.** Generic Hořava/Einstein-aether has the aether as a *separate* field, so matter and gravity can acquire *different* cones → differential LV → the `10⁻²⁰` executioner. ED's matter and gravity descend from the *same* bandwidth substrate → *one* cone → *universal* LV → the worst bounds do not bite. The very feature that makes ED radical (geometry and matter from one substrate) is what makes its Lorentz violation safe.

This is a genuine, `F`-independent structural advantage, and it is the strongest viability result the arc produces — stronger than the PPN discussion, because it disarms the *tightest* constraint.

## 4. What remains `F`-dependent — the honest residual

Universal LV is leading-order. Two residuals survive, and they need `F`:

- **Higher-order differential LV.** Different species couple to the substrate slightly differently beyond leading order, generating *suppressed* differential effects. Whether the suppression beats `10⁻²⁰` depends on the operator dimensions in `F` (the naturalness question, now at sub-leading order).
- **Preferred-frame gravity `α₁, α₂`.** These are pure-gravity preferred-frame effects (weaker bounds, `10⁻⁴/10⁻⁷`), set by the khronon-gravity coupling in the linearized `F`. The single-cone structure forces `c₁+c₃≈0` (R11) and may force further `cᵢ` relations toward a viable corner — but confirming that needs the explicit `F`.

So the residual viability question is real but **demoted**: from "survive the `10⁻²⁰` executioner" (structurally handled) to "compute the sub-leading and pure-gravity coefficients and check the weaker bounds" — a model-building computation, not a survival crisis.

## 5. The terminus — where derivation ends and model-building begins

R12 marks a principled boundary, and it is the right place to close the derivation arc:

- **Rounds 1–11 were forced or structural** — the metric, covariance, conservation = geodesics, the weak-field Schwarzschild metric and classical tests, the Lovelock-forced field-equation form, the khronometric class, `c_T=c`, and now **universal (safe) Lorentz violation**. None of these chose `F`'s coefficients; all followed from primitives + corpus + general theorems.
- **Beyond R12 is model-building** — pinning `F`'s coefficients within the admissible family, extracting `α₁, α₂` and the sub-leading differential LV, confronting the weaker bounds. This is conditional phenomenology (its outputs depend on the `F`-choice), epistemically distinct from everything before it.

The foundational program is therefore **complete**: ED supports an Einstein-class gravity of the **khronometric** type, GW-clean by construction, with Lorentz violation that is **universal and hence safe from the lethal differential bounds** — all derived, with the deviation from textbook GR located at the arrow (P11). What is left is to *build the model* (fix `F`) and compute the weaker, `F`-dependent numbers — genuine physics, but a different mode of work.
