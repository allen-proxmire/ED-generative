## §1 Problem restatement

The R4 constructive derivation (per `Memo_ED_CoherenceFunctional_SingleTemplate_Construct` §3) closed at **Outcome C — partial closure**: coherence-functional minimization on the Q-C3-2 admissible-fluctuation set under uniform-Ψ no-slack boundary conditions yields a **continuously-degenerate minimum manifold** (P09 phase symmetry pre-admission), but a **unique attractor given first-arrival IC** (V5 phase-coherence + capacity penalty post-admission). The asymmetry between matter and antimatter is generated by framework-neutral first-arrival selection from the degenerate manifold, not by asymmetric admission cost between two pre-admitted classes.

Cos_01's M3 unconditional closure (per Paper_ED_Inflation Preamble item 5 + §2 substrate-research-support block) operates via an analogous conditional structure: the Q-C3 cascade closes the biconditional uniform-Ψ ↔ saturation at substrate-state level (Q-C3-1a + Q-C3-1b + Q-C3-2 + Q-C3-2b), but the substrate-graph realizability of the uniform-Ψ state itself is supplied at IC-INHERITED level **framework-neutrally** via Q-C3-3 — either via Paper_ED_CCC §3.7 cyclic framing or R3 single-aeon framing, with the choice between them outside scope per `Memo_ED_CCC_Realizability_SubConstruct`. Cos_01 is M3 unconditional in the current corpus state despite this conditional IC-supply structure; the IC-INHERITED axis is what carries the conditional content per Paper_095 §3.2.1.

**The audit question:** Is R4's first-arrival IC-supply structurally identical to Cos_01's Q-C3-3 IC-supply — at the level relevant to Paper_095 §3.2.1's IC-INHERITED sub-label designation — such that Baryogenesis can inherit M3 status via the same conditional-IC mechanism?

If yes (Outcome A): Baryogenesis M2 → M3 upgrade is corpus-consistent and the follow-up upgrade memo can proceed.

If no or partial: the upgrade either fails (Baryogenesis stays M2 with reframed OPEN) or requires additional substrate-graph work to bridge the gap.

**Properties extracted:**

- **(P1) Role.** Q-C3-3 supplies the *substrate-graph realizability* of the uniform-Ψ saturation state. It does not derive uniform-Ψ from primitives; it asserts that uniform-Ψ is realized as an IC condition, with the realization-mechanism supplied externally.
- **(P2) Framework neutrality.** Either of two distinct framings supply the IC — cyclic-CCC (pre-aeon inheritance) or R3 single-aeon (structural-IC assumption). The choice between them is outside Cos_01's scope. This makes the IC-supply *framework-neutral* in the strict sense: Cos_01's M3 verdict does not depend on which framing supplies the IC.
- **(P3) External-non-postulate status.** Q-C3-3 IC supply does not declare a new paper-specific postulate. It inherits IC content from an upstream framework (either CCC §3.7 or R3 single-aeon), both of which are corpus-external to Cos_01.
- **(P4) Verdict-tier role.** Q-C3-3 is the IC-INHERITED axis of Cos_01's three-axis M3 verdict (form-IDENTIFIED + value-INHERITED + IC-INHERITED). The IC-INHERITED axis carries the conditional content; the verdict tier remains M3 because the IC supply is at framework-neutral inheritance status, not at substrate-graph-derivation status.
- **(P5) Precedent for M3 compatibility.** Cos_01 is **M3 unconditional** in the current corpus state. This establishes that conditional-IC supply via Q-C3-3 framework-neutrality *is corpus-accepted as M3-compatible*. The same precedent applies to Cos_05 (M3 unconditional via the same Q-C3 + IC inheritance chain) and Paper_038_5 (M3 retroactive via Route A4 substrate-parameter-INHERITED closure inheriting the analogous conditional structure).

**The substrate-state level vs. fluctuation level distinction.** Q-C3-3 specifically addresses realizability of the leading-order substrate state (uniform Ψ). It does not address fluctuation-selection on top of that state — that content sits in Q-C3-2 (admissible fluctuation set) and is treated as not load-bearing for Cos_01's leading-order phenomenology. **Properties extracted:**

## §4 Equivalence test

Four tests applied to determine whether R4 IC-supply ≡ Q-C3-3 IC-supply at the level relevant to Paper_095 §3.2.1 IC-INHERITED designation.

### Test 1 — Structural role (does IC merely select among admissible templates?)

**Q-C3-3:** Selects the uniform-Ψ realization from among possible substrate-state initial conditions (cyclic-CCC vs R3 single-aeon). The realization is an "admissible template" in the sense that both framings are corpus-admissible IC suppliers; Q-C3-3 picks one (framework-neutrally).

**R4 first-arrival:** Selects $\chi^*$ from the continuously-degenerate minimum manifold $S^1$. The selection is among "admissible templates" in the sense that every $\chi_C \in S^1$ is equally a candidate template pre-admission; first-arrival picks one (framework-neutrally).

**Equivalence:** ✓ Both IC mechanisms select among an *otherwise underdetermined* set of admissible templates. The structural role is identical.

### Test 2 — Verdict-tier role (does IC-supply appear in FORM/VALUE/IC grammar?)

**Q-C3-3:** Carries the IC-INHERITED axis of Cos_01's three-axis M3 verdict per Paper_095 §3.2.1. Sub-label *IC-INHERITED* applied at Cos_01 audit rows treating uniform-Ψ realizability supply.

**R4 first-arrival:** Under the proposed upgrade, would carry the IC-INHERITED axis of Baryogenesis's three-axis M3 verdict. Sub-label *IC-INHERITED* would apply at the Baryogenesis audit row treating first-arrival chirality selection.

**Equivalence:** ✓ Both occupy the IC-INHERITED axis of the Paper_095 §3.2.1 three-axis verdict notation. The verdict-tier role is identical.

### Test 3 — Inheritance role (is IC-supply external, framework-neutral, non-postulate?)

**Q-C3-3:** External (inherits from CCC §3.7 or R3 single-aeon, both corpus-external to Cos_01). Framework-neutral (either route admissible; choice outside scope). Non-postulate (no new paper-specific postulate declared in Cos_01).

**R4 first-arrival:** External (inherits from the *same* CCC §3.7 or R3 single-aeon machinery via `Memo_ED_CCC_Realizability_SubConstruct`). Framework-neutral (either route admissible; choice outside scope). Non-postulate (under the proposed upgrade, no new paper-specific postulate declared in Baryogenesis — P-BinaryAdmission is *reinterpreted*, not replaced by a new postulate).

**Equivalence:** ✓ Both IC suppliers are external, framework-neutral, and non-postulate. The inheritance role is identical.

### Test 4 — Precedent role (does Cos_01 treat Q-C3-3 as M3-compatible?)

**Cos_01 status:** M3 unconditional in the current corpus state. The Q-C3-3 IC-supply mechanism is accepted as compatible with the M3 verdict tier — the verdict is not downgraded to M2 because of the conditional-IC-supply structure.

**Corroborating precedents:**
- **Cos_05:** M3 unconditional via the same Cos_01 + Q-C3 + IC inheritance chain. Confirms Q-C3-3 IC-supply is M3-compatible across multiple papers, not specific to Cos_01.
- **Paper_038_5:** M3 retroactive via Route A4 substrate-parameter-INHERITED closure inheriting the same conditional-IC pattern. Confirms the broader conditional-IC + framework-neutrality structure is corpus-accepted at M3 status.

**Implication for R4:** if Q-C3-3 IC-supply is M3-compatible in Cos_01 / Cos_05 / Paper_038_5, then a structurally-identical IC-supply mechanism in Baryogenesis should also be M3-compatible. The precedent is consistent and load-bearing.

**Equivalence:** ✓ Cos_01 + Cos_05 + Paper_038_5 collectively establish the precedent that conditional-IC supply via framework-neutral CCC vs R3 single-aeon machinery is M3-compatible. R4 inherits the same precedent.

### Test result summary

All four tests pass. R4 first-arrival IC-supply is structurally identical to Q-C3-3 IC-supply at the level relevant to Paper_095 §3.2.1 IC-INHERITED designation.

**Layer-difference nuance from (P5)/(R5).** Q-C3-3 operates at the substrate-state level (uniform-Ψ background); R4 operates at the fluctuation-selection level (first-arrival $\chi^*$ on top of the uniform-Ψ background). This is a real difference in *what gets IC-supplied*, but it does not change the *structural role* of the IC supply mechanism. For Paper_095 §3.2.1's IC-INHERITED sub-label — which designates "inheritance of initial-condition / boundary-state content from an upstream substrate-state specification, framework-neutral per `Memo_ED_CCC_Realizability_SubConstruct`" — both qualify under the same designation. The layer difference should be noted for transparency in the upgrade memo (Baryogenesis IC-INHERITED row should specify "fluctuation-selection level: first-arrival $\chi^*$" alongside the framework-neutral citation) but does not load-bear on the M3 verdict.

## §5 Outcome classification

**Outcome A — Equivalent (R4 IC-supply ≡ Q-C3-3 IC-supply at structural-role level relevant to IC-INHERITED designation).**

**CONFIRMED.** All four equivalence tests pass. R4 first-arrival IC-supply and Q-C3-3 IC-supply share identical structural role (select among admissible templates), identical verdict-tier role (IC-INHERITED axis of three-axis verdict per Paper_095 §3.2.1), identical inheritance role (external + framework-neutral + non-postulate via `Memo_ED_CCC_Realizability_SubConstruct`), and identical corpus precedent (Cos_01 M3 unconditional + Cos_05 M3 unconditional + Paper_038_5 M3 retroactive all use the conditional-IC + framework-neutrality pattern at M3 status). The layer difference (substrate-state vs. fluctuation-selection) is real but does not load-bear on the M3 verdict tier; it should be noted in the upgrade memo for transparency but does not block the upgrade.

**Verdict consequence:** the Baryogenesis M2 → M3 upgrade is **corpus-consistent**. The follow-up upgrade memo can proceed without additional bridging work.

**Outcome B — Not equivalent (R4 IC-supply fails one or more equivalence tests).**

**NOT REACHED.** No equivalence test failed.

**Outcome C — Partial equivalence (layer-difference is load-bearing such that R4 IC-supply requires additional bridging work for M3 compatibility).**

**NOT REACHED.** The layer-difference exists but is not load-bearing for the IC-INHERITED designation per Paper_095 §3.2.1. The designation operates at the structural-role level (external + framework-neutral + non-postulate inheritance from upstream substrate-state specification), which is identical across both layers. Were the layer-difference load-bearing, Paper_095 §3.2.1 would need to differentiate substrate-state-level IC-INHERITED from fluctuation-selection-level IC-INHERITED — but the current sub-label specification does not make this distinction, and no corpus paper has flagged the absence of such a distinction as an issue.
