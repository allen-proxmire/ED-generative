## Foundations

- ED postulates 13 primitives (P01–P13) — discrete micro-events, participation, channels, bandwidth, polarity, commitment, and a handful more — as the substrate beneath spacetime and quantum mechanics. They are **postulated, not derived**; the case for them is what they generate downstream.
- The ontology is relational, not object-first: a chain's identity, spin, and even its existence as "one thing" only make sense in relation to what it's tethered to. **"Two is more fundamental than one."**
- **Commitment (P11) is primitive, not derived.** The arrow of time is not a statistical illusion emerging from reversible microlaws — it is a foundational fact about the substrate. Reversibility, where it shows up in physics, is the coarse-grained approximation.
- The governing pattern across almost every downstream result: **form is FORCED, value is INHERITED.** The substrate fixes the *structure* of a law; it does not fix free numerical constants, masses, or counts.
- ED is not a Theory of Everything and does not claim to be. It names explicitly, in every paper, what it does not explain.
