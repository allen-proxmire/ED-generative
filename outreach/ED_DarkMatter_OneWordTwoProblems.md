# Dark Matter Is One Word for Two Problems

### The Event Density position, in plain terms

**Allen Proxmire — July 2026**

Two different observations wear the same name.

The first is galaxies. Stars at the edge of a spiral orbit faster than the visible matter can hold them. Something is missing, and it tracks the ordinary matter with a precision, one acceleration scale and one tight relation, that has been measured for forty years.

The second is the early universe. The pattern of the cosmic microwave background, and the mass of galaxy clusters, require something that pulls gravitationally but does not slosh with the ordinary matter and light, something already settled into place before the first atoms formed, at about five times the density of ordinary matter.

For fifty years these have been treated as one thing: a single new particle, cold and invisible, that happens to solve both. The assumption is economical, it fits the numbers, and it is the whole reason "dark matter" is spoken of as a particle. But it is an assumption. The word bundled two problems together, and then quietly claimed one substance solves both.

Event Density pries them apart, because in ED they have two different answers.

**The galactic problem is not a particle.** In ED, gravity read at the level of the substrate has two pieces: the familiar one (Newton) and an interference term between a local mass and the cosmic horizon. That interference term *is* MOND. It reproduces the rotation curves, the tight relation, and the single acceleration scale, with nothing added and no particle anywhere. There is no missing matter in a galaxy. There is a piece of gravity that was being missed.

**The cosmological problem is a particle.** The early-universe component the CMB and clusters demand is, in ED, a real relic: a neutral remnant from the same early process that made ordinary matter, uncharged so it ignores light, with enough mass to settle and dilute like matter rather than radiation. It gravitates, it clusters, it is genuinely there. It is dark matter in the honest sense of the phrase.

So the ED position, said precisely:

> There is no universal dark-matter particle doing every job. The galactic effect is a feature of gravity (MOND), with no particle. The cosmological effect is a real neutral relic. One word was covering two different things.

This is worth stating carefully, because the reflex objection to any MOND theory is "but the CMB proves dark matter exists." Event Density agrees. The CMB does show a real dark component, and ED gives it a real one. What ED denies is only that the *same* particle is what holds galaxies together. Conceding the first is what earns the second a hearing.

And the split is sharply falsifiable. If the galactic effect were a cold particle, its fingerprints would show up inside galaxies too. ED predicts they do not, and it predicts the cosmological relic is *warm*, not the cold particle the standard picture assumes. A direct detection of a cold dark-matter particle, or cold-particle signatures in galactic dynamics, would break the ED account.

One honest limit. The relic is ED's *candidate* for the cosmological component. It follows natively from the framework, but its mass is inherited rather than derived, and a full fit to the CMB spectrum has not yet been computed. ED does not claim to have proven the relic. It claims to have separated the two problems, solved the galactic one, and given the cosmological one a native candidate with a falsifiable signature.
