### 6. Why space has three dimensions

Ever wonder why space has three dimensions, not two, not eleven?

(conceptual, not an equation): a closed loop can hold another closed loop in exactly one dimension count

In 2D, two loops can't even cross. In 4D, any two loops slide free of each other untouched. Only in 3D can one loop actually hold another.

The universe needed a way to hold its own history in place. Three dimensions was the only room with a lock.

Probability was never a rule bolted onto reality afterward. It's just how much of it is there.
