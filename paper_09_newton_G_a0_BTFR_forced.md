## 8. Exclusion Arguments

### 8.1 A1 — Non-inverse-square Newtonian law

$G$ universality follows from the substrate-derivation $G = c^3 \ell_P^2 / \hbar$ — every quantity on the right-hand side is a universal substrate constant. ### 8.3 A3 — No transition acceleration

A pure-Newton substrate (no $a_0$) would require either no cosmic decoupling surface (excluded by C6) or no dipole-mode coupling under acceleration (excluded by the substrate's anisotropic-projection mechanism in §7.2). ### 8.5 A5 — Non-multiplicative combination rule

Additive combination $a = a_N + a_0$ would produce $v^2 = a R = (a_N + a_0) R$, giving rotation curves that grow as $\sqrt{R}$ rather than asymptoting to $v_\mathrm{flat}$. The slope cannot be other than 4 without violating T19 (linear-in-$M$ Newton), T20 (cosmic-scale $a_0$), or the ED Combination Rule (multiplicative combination).

Volume-scaling channel counts ($N \propto R^3$) would produce a $1/R^3$ gravitational potential and a $1/R^4$ acceleration — strongly contradicted by every solar-system gravitational test. ### A.1 The dipole-mode mechanism for $a_0$ — explicit derivation

An accelerating chain has a privileged axis along the acceleration direction $\hat{a}$. The cosmic decoupling surface at $R_H = c/H_0$, viewed from the accelerating chain's frame, decomposes into spherical harmonics:
$$
\Phi_\mathrm{cosmic}(\theta, \phi) = \sum_{l=0}^\infty \sum_{m=-l}^{l} c_{lm}\,Y_{lm}(\theta, \phi),
$$
with the polar axis along $\hat{a}$. The $l = 0$ (monopole) component contributes only an overall additive constant to the chain's stability landscape — it does not produce a coupling between chain motion and cosmic content. The $l = 1$ (dipole) component is the leading anisotropic mode coupling to the chain's adjacency structure under acceleration.

The $(l = 1, m = 0)$ spherical harmonic is
$$
Y_{1,0}(\theta, \phi) = \sqrt{\frac{3}{4\pi}}\,\cos\theta,
$$
with angular structure independent of $\phi$. However, the **azimuthal period** of the angular projection — the period of $e^{i\phi}$ around the chain's acceleration axis — is exactly $2\pi$. This $2\pi$ enters the dipole-mode coupling to the chain's adjacency structure as a structural factor.

The dipole-mediated effective rate of cosmic-content fluctuations impinging on the chain is
$$
\gamma_\mathrm{cosmic}^\mathrm{eff} = \frac{H_0}{2\pi},
$$
with $H_0$ the cosmic rate and $2\pi$ the geometric period factor. The chain's experienced rate under acceleration $a$ is $\gamma_\mathrm{chain} = a/c$. The transition acceleration $a_0$ is defined as the value of $a$ matching these rates:
$$
\frac{a_0}{c} = \frac{H_0}{2\pi} \implies a_0 = \frac{c H_0}{2\pi}.
$$

The factor of $2\pi$ is therefore *geometric* (azimuthal period of the leading anisotropic spherical-harmonic mode) and *not phenomenological*. This is the structural distinction between the substrate-derived $a_0$ and MOND's empirical fit parameter.
