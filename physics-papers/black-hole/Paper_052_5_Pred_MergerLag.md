## §3 Derivation

### 3.1 Merger events at substrate level

A merger event (compact-binary, galactic, or cluster) at substrate level involves:
- Two substrate-level configurations approaching each other.
- Substrate-level participation content reorganizing across the merger zone.
- V5 cross-chain content shifting from one configuration to another.
- Post-merger substrate relaxation as the merged configuration settles.

The substrate-level dynamics are richer than the standard relativistic two-body description because the substrate has finite-memory (V5) + finite-width (V1) kernel content.

### 3.2 V5 finite-memory contribution to lag

The V5 cross-chain kernel has finite memory $\tau_{V5}$ (Paper_090). During a merger, V5 must redistribute cross-chain bandwidth content from the pre-merger two-configuration state to the post-merger one-configuration state.

This redistribution does **not** happen instantaneously. The substrate-level reorganization timescale is $\sim \tau_{V5}$, producing a characteristic lag in the substrate-level merger completion relative to the standard-GR prediction.

Quantitatively: if the merger event has a characteristic merger-zone size $L_{\mathrm{merger}}$, the V5-redistribution lag is approximately
$$\tau_{\mathrm{lag, V5}} \;\sim\; \min(\tau_{V5}, L_{\mathrm{merger}}/c) ,$$
depending on whether V5 memory or signal-propagation time dominates.

### 3.3 Mobility-gradient contribution to lag

By Paper_EDSC_AcousticCurvature, acoustic-metric curvature concentrates on substrate mobility-gradient scales. In a merger event, the merger zone exhibits extreme mobility-gradients (sharp transitions between high-mobility and low-mobility substrate regions during merger).

The curvature-induced dynamics modify effective propagation through the merger zone. Specifically: signals traversing high-curvature regions experience effective time-dilation relative to flat-region propagation. The integrated time-dilation over the merger event contributes a lag:
$$\tau_{\mathrm{lag, mobility}} \;\sim\; \int_{\mathrm{merger\,zone}} \frac{|\nabla\mu(x)|^2}{\mu(x)^2} \cdot \frac{dt}{c^2} ,$$
where the integral is over the merger zone's substrate spacetime extent.

### 3.4 Kernel-cascade contribution

Merger events span multiple substrate scales (Paper_091 kernel cascade). The substrate-level reorganization at each scale (UV V1 → transition → IR V5 → cosmological boundary) has its own characteristic timescale. The full merger-lag is the cumulative integral over the cascade:
$$\tau_{\mathrm{lag, cascade}} \;\sim\; \int_{\ell_{V1}}^{R_H} \frac{d\ell}{c} \cdot f_{\mathrm{cascade}}(\ell) ,$$
with $f_{\mathrm{cascade}}(\ell)$ a substrate-derived cascade-weighting function.

For most empirically-relevant mergers (stellar-mass binaries to galaxy-cluster mergers), the dominant contribution is at the V5-relaxation scale; cosmological-scale contributions are sub-dominant.
