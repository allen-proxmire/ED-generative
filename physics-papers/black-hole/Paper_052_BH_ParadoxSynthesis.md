## 3. The Synthesis Argument

### 3.1 The standard paradox

The BH information paradox arises from three statements:

**(1) Hawking radiation is purely thermal.** Standard semiclassical derivation (Hawking 1974, 1975) gives the radiation a thermal Planck spectrum at $T_H = \kappa/(2\pi)$, with no information about the matter that formed the hole.

**(2) BH evaporation is complete.** Naive extrapolation of Hawking's result has evaporation continuing until $M \to 0$, with the BH disappearing.

**(3) Quantum mechanics is unitary.** Time evolution preserves information; pure states evolve to pure states.

The three together produce contradiction: matter (pure state, carrying information) goes into BH; BH evaporates completely (matter is gone); radiation carries no information (purely thermal); information has been destroyed (non-unitary).

### 3.2 ED refines (1): Hawking radiation is not purely thermal

By Paper_047 §6 under P-V5-Even, the substrate-level Hawking spectrum has form:

$$
n_H^{\mathrm{ED}}(\omega) = n_H^{\mathrm{Planck}}(\omega)\,\left[1 - c_1(\omega/\omega_c)^2 + \mathcal{O}((\omega/\omega_c)^4)\right]
$$

The $(\omega/\omega_c)^2$ non-thermal corrections are sign-definite, mass-dependent, and **provide structural room for information-bearing content** (Paper_047 §6.3 — soft claim, structural availability not encoding-map derivation).

**Statement (1) refined:** Hawking radiation is purely thermal *at leading order ($\omega \ll \omega_c$)*; non-thermal corrections at $(\omega/\omega_c)^2$ leave room for information-bearing content.

### 3.3 ED refines (2): evaporation is not complete

By Paper_039 §6, evaporation halts at a **stable Planck-mass remnant** $M_\star = M_P = \hbar/(c\,\ell_P)$ (exact at leading order; order-unity coefficient $c_\star \approx 1$ inherited). The substrate-level mechanism: V5 finite-memory cutoff $\omega_c = c/\ell_P$ structurally prevents emission of modes above $\omega_c$; at $M = M_\star$, dominant emission frequency $\omega_H \to \omega_c$ and evaporation halts.

**Statement (2) refined:** evaporation is not complete; a stable Planck-mass remnant persists carrying substrate-level content.

### 3.4 ED preserves (3) at substrate level

**Substrate-level unitarity** is preserved via:

- **P04 (bandwidth additivity):** total substrate bandwidth content is conserved throughout BH formation and evaporation.

- **Re-routing (P-Re-routing, Paper_050):** post-Page-time, accumulated entanglement re-routes through alternative substrate-graph channels (radiation↔radiation + radiation↔remnant) — substrate-level information returns to outgoing radiation.
