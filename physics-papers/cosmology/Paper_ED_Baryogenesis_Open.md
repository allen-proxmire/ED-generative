### 3.2 Chain-arrow chirality $\chi_C \in S^1$

Per Memo_ED_ChainArrowChirality §3.3, every chain $C$ carries a P09-valued chirality at each of its commitment events:

$$\chi_C(e_n) := \pi_C(e_n) - \pi_K(\ell(e_n)) \pmod{2\pi},$$

where $\pi_C(e_n)$ is the P09 phase of the chain-arrow $\sigma_C(e_n) = (e_n \to e_{n+1})$ and $\pi_K(\ell(e_n))$ is the P09 phase of the kernel-arrow at the chain's locus. The chirality is a substrate-graph quantity (D-via-I from P09 + P11 + Paper_089 + Paper_093 composition).

Per Memo_ED_ChainArrowChirality §3.5, in the post-SCBU regime ($\Delta\pi_K = 0$ from §3.1(ii); $\Delta\pi_C^{P05} = 0$ from §3.1(i) homogeneity), $\chi_C$ is **conserved along the chain**: each chain carries a fixed $\chi_C$ value through its commitment sequence.

In the absence of an admission filter, $\chi_C \in S^1$ is continuous and chains can in principle carry any phase value.

### 3.3 P-BinaryAdmission

P-BinaryAdmission (§2.3) restricts admissible chirality values in the post-SCBU ignition regime to $\chi_C \in \{0, \pi\}$. Under this postulate:

- **Aligned chirality** ($\chi_C = 0$): chain-arrow co-aligned with globally-coherent kernel-arrow. Admitted.
- **Anti-aligned chirality** ($\chi_C = \pi$): chain-arrow phase-shifted by $\pi$ relative to kernel-arrow. Admitted.
- **Intermediate chirality** ($\chi_C \in S^1 \setminus \{0, \pi\}$): fails Paper_072 individuation; not admitted.

P-BinaryAdmission is the single load-bearing paper-specific postulate of this paper. Its substrate-graph derivation is OPEN per `Memo_ED_BinaryChirality` (R1 fails by Paper_089 T18; R2 fails by lack of chain-typing parity; R3 fails by category mismatch between scale-collapse and orientation-collapse).

The honest reading: the framework requires a chirality binary that the substrate does not currently natively supply at the chain level. The corpus has event-direction binary (P11) but Paper_089 T18 forecloses its use at chain level, and continuous P09 polarity does not collapse to binary under SC-4.x. P-BinaryAdmission encodes the binary-restriction structurally pending substrate-graph closure.

### 3.4 Stability selection: aligned vs anti-aligned admissibility

Under P-BinaryAdmission, both $\chi_C = 0$ and $\chi_C = \pi$ are admissible in the post-SCBU regime. Whether both are admitted *symmetrically* or *asymmetrically* in the saturation phase of the ignition regime is the load-bearing OPEN structural item, addressed in §3.5.

If symmetric admission held, the framework would predict equal abundances of aligned-chirality and anti-aligned-chirality chains in the post-SCBU substrate. The observed matter-dominance (the absence of large antimatter content per §3.8) requires asymmetric admission in the saturation phase.

**Stability selection** is the substrate-side mechanism by which one chirality class is preferentially admitted while the other fails Paper_072 individuation. Its substrate-graph derivation requires a derivation of asymmetric admission cost between the two classes (§3.5).

The substrate-graph derivation of **which** chirality class — aligned or anti-aligned — is preferentially admitted in saturation is **OPEN**. The structural framework requires:

(ii) A derivation that $C_{\mathrm{admit}}(0) \ne C_{\mathrm{admit}}(\pi)$ in the saturation phase — specifically, that the costs are asymmetric.

(iii) A determination of which class has lower cost (and is therefore preferentially admitted in saturation).

**Identification claim (D-via-I, conditional on §3.5 OPEN closure):** if (i)–(iii) close and the asymmetric admission preferentially admits one class, the post-saturation substrate contains one chirality class as accumulated chain content (matter) and the other as decohered remnant content (antimatter). ### 3.6 Restoration of symmetric admission post-saturation

As the substrate cosmic phase advances out of the ignition regime, event-density saturation relaxes (capacity grows above demand). The asymmetric admission cost from §3.5 (i)–(iii) becomes structurally invisible: both chirality classes are symmetrically admissible.

**Symmetric pair-production resumes.** Standard QFT pair-production processes (creating matter–antimatter pairs in equal abundance at the post-saturation substrate) operate without restriction.

The asymmetry from §3.5 is therefore a **one-time-event** imprinted on the substrate during the saturation phase; subsequent symmetric pair-production does not erase the imprint because the matter content accumulated during saturation persists as bound chains. This is the structural content of "asymmetry as a one-time event" in ED-I-11's framing.

This step is D-via-I conditional on §3.5 closing.

### 3.7 Motion = V1-geometric; chirality is orthogonal to motion

Per Paper_089, V1 retarded propagation is **chirality-agnostic**: V1 carries chain content along substrate edges in the kernel-arrow direction, independent of the chain's $\chi_C$ value. Motion is determined by V1-geometric content (substrate-graph geodesics per Paper_032 family + Paper_ED_CCC §3.7); chirality is determined by the P09 phase relationship of the chain to the kernel-arrow.

**Consequence:** matter chains ($\chi_C = 0$) and antimatter chains ($\chi_C = \pi$) follow identical V1-geometric geodesics. They respond identically to substrate-curvature content (gravity in the continuum-side reading). This is the substrate-side reason for the experimental observation that antimatter falls identically to matter (ALPHA-g 2023 with antihydrogen; AEGIS, GBAR forthcoming).

The "motion = geometry; coherence = polarity" separation in ED-I-11's framing is structurally cleanly identified: motion is V1-geometric (chirality-agnostic); coherence is Paper_072 individuation under chirality (chirality-dependent via P-BinaryAdmission). The two axes are orthogonal substrate-side.

### 3.8 No-large-antimatter-domains; identical fall; no new CP phases

Three downstream consequences of §3.1–§3.7, all D-via-I conditional on §3.5 closure:

- **No large antimatter domains.** Under §3.5 asymmetric admission, the post-saturation substrate is uniformly dominated by one chirality class across cosmic-scale loci (the saturation regime applies homogeneously per §3.1 global homogeneity). No domain structure can form because the asymmetric admission applies uniformly. Consistent with the cosmic gamma-ray background absence of annihilation signatures, BBN homogeneity, and bullet-cluster constraints.

- **Identical gravitational fall.** Per §3.7, matter and antimatter follow identical V1-geometric geodesics. Direct prediction: ALPHA-g (2023) result that antihydrogen falls down at the same rate as hydrogen is consistent with the substrate-side V1 chirality-agnosticism. Forthcoming AEGIS / GBAR results expected to confirm.

- **No new CP-violating phases or sterile neutrinos required.** The asymmetry is structural (P-BinaryAdmission + asymmetric admission cost OPEN), not dynamical-CP-driven. Standard Model CP violation remains small because it plays no role in the substrate baryogenesis mechanism. No sterile neutrinos are required because the substrate mechanism is chirality-admission, not neutrino-sector-extension.

These three consequences are not derived in this paper; they are inherited from §3.5 closure (currently OPEN). The paper identifies the structural framework that makes them substrate-side consequences; the quantitative consequences require §3.5 closure.
