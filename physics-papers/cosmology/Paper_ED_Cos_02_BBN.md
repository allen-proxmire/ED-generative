### §3.2 Weak freeze-out (n/p ratio)

The weak-interaction rate maintaining $n \leftrightarrow p$ equilibrium scales as $\Gamma_W \sim G_F^2 T^5$ (standard particle physics; Fermi constant $G_F$). Freeze-out occurs when $\Gamma_W \sim H(t)$:

$$
T_{\mathrm{freeze}} \sim \left(\frac{H(t)}{G_F^2}\right)^{1/3} \approx 1\,\text{MeV}.
$$

At $T_{\mathrm{freeze}}$, the n/p equilibrium ratio is $\exp(-\Delta m/T_{\mathrm{freeze}}) \approx 1/6$ (with $\Delta m = m_n - m_p \approx 1.29$ MeV). After freeze-out, neutron decay ($\tau_n \approx 880$ s) reduces the n/p ratio to $\sim 1/7$ by the time deuterium formation begins.

Substrate-graph composition: standard particle physics $\Gamma_W$ + substrate-graph-inherited $H(t)$. The $T_{\mathrm{freeze}}$ value inherits standard cosmology + Route A4 substrate-parameter-INHERITED $H_0$ scaling at the BBN epoch.

### §3.3 Deuterium bottleneck

The deuterium photodissociation rate dominates over D-formation while the radiation field maintains substantial $\gamma$-flux above the D binding energy $B_D = 2.22$ MeV. Photodissociation rate scales as $\Gamma_{\gamma D} \propto \eta_b T^{3/2} \exp(-B_D/T)$; D-formation rate via $p + n \to D + \gamma$. Bottleneck breaks when photodissociation rate falls below D-formation rate:

$$
T_{\mathrm{D-bottle}} \approx \frac{B_D}{\ln(\eta_b^{-1}) - \frac{3}{2}\ln T} \approx 0.07\,\text{MeV}.
$$

At $T_{\mathrm{D-bottle}}$, D begins to accumulate; nuclear reactions $D + D \to T + p$, $D + D \to {^3}\mathrm{He} + n$, $D + T \to {^4}\mathrm{He} + n$, etc., initiate. Substrate-graph composition: standard nuclear physics + baryon-to-photon ratio $\eta_b$ inherited from CMB observations + substrate-graph-inherited $H(t)$ controlling the time-scale for nuclear-reaction sequence to complete.

### §3.4 Helium-4 synthesis

Once the D bottleneck breaks, nearly all available free neutrons are captured into He-4 via the chain $p + n \to D$ → $D + D \to {^3}\mathrm{He} + n$ or $D + D \to T + p$ → ${^3}\mathrm{He} + D \to {^4}\mathrm{He} + p$ (and analogous T-mediated paths). The He-4 mass fraction:

$$
Y_p = \frac{2 (n/p)_{\mathrm{D-bottle}}}{1 + (n/p)_{\mathrm{D-bottle}}} \approx 0.247
$$

with $(n/p)_{\mathrm{D-bottle}} \approx 1/7$ inherited from §3.2 + §3.3. Substrate-graph composition: standard nuclear-physics neutron-capture chain + substrate-graph-inherited $H(t)$ + standard cosmology time-scale.
