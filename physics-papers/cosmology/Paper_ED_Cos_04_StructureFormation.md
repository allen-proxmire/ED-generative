## §3 Derivation

### §3.1 Radiation-era suppression (Meszaros effect)

During the radiation-dominated era ($z > z_{\mathrm{eq}} \approx 3400$), sub-horizon matter density perturbations $\delta_m$ satisfy the linearized perturbation equation:

$$
\ddot\delta_m + 2H \dot\delta_m - 4\pi G \rho_m \delta_m = 0
$$

In RDE, $\rho_{\mathrm{rad}} \gg \rho_m$ and $H \propto T^2$ (inherits substrate-graph $H(t)$ via Cos_01 + Cos_05 framework + standard cosmology at RDE). The driving term $4\pi G \rho_m$ is subdominant compared to the Hubble friction $2H\dot\delta$; matter perturbations grow only logarithmically ($\delta_m \sim \ln a$) rather than as a power law. This is the **Meszaros effect** — matter perturbation suppression during radiation domination.

Super-horizon modes ($k < a H$) grow as $\delta_m \propto a^2$ during RDE (gauge-dependent; in synchronous gauge); these match continuously to sub-horizon evolution at horizon-crossing. Substrate-graph composition: standard linear theory + substrate-graph-inherited $H(t)$ + Cos_02 baryon density at RDE → MDE transition.

The matter-radiation equality scale $k_{\mathrm{eq}} \equiv a_{\mathrm{eq}} H_{\mathrm{eq}} \approx 0.01$ h/Mpc sets the turnover in $P(k)$: modes with $k > k_{\mathrm{eq}}$ entered the horizon during RDE (Meszaros-suppressed); modes with $k < k_{\mathrm{eq}}$ entered the horizon during MDE (no Meszaros suppression).

### §3.2 Matter-era growth $D(a) \propto a$

Once $a > a_{\mathrm{eq}}$ and the universe is matter-dominated, the growth equation becomes:

$$
\ddot\delta_m + 2H \dot\delta_m - \frac{3}{2}\Omega_m H^2 \delta_m = 0
$$

In Einstein-de Sitter (EdS; $\Omega_m = 1$) limit, the growing-mode solution is $D(a) \propto a$ (linear growth). For realistic $\Lambda$CDM with $\Omega_m \approx 0.31$ today, the growth factor at high $z$ matches EdS ($D \propto a$); at low $z$ (LDE-onset epoch), $D$ slows.

Substrate-graph composition: standard linear theory + substrate-graph-inherited $H(t)$ from Cos_05 expansion history + Cos_02 baryon density + Cos_03 baryon-photon decoupling at recombination.

### §3.3 Baryon acoustic imprint on $P(k)$

Pre-recombination, baryon-photon coupling means baryons participate in acoustic oscillations at sound speed $c_s$ (Cos_03 §3.1); these oscillations imprint a characteristic length scale $r_s \approx 147$ Mpc (comoving) on the matter distribution at recombination. After recombination, baryons decouple from photons; the acoustic-feature imprint becomes a **Baryon Acoustic Oscillation (BAO)** signature in the matter power spectrum $P(k)$ as an oscillatory feature centered at wavenumber $k_{\mathrm{BAO}} \approx 2\pi/r_s \approx 0.04$ h/Mpc, and equivalently as a peak in the matter correlation function $\xi(r)$ at $r \approx r_s$.

Substrate-graph composition: Cos_03 sound horizon $r_s$ + standard cosmological-perturbation-theory + Cos_02 baryon density → BAO feature in $P(k)$. The BAO scale serves as a **standard ruler** for galaxy-survey distance measurements (BOSS, eBOSS, DESI).

Substrate-parameter-INHERITED scaling: $r_s \propto 1/H_0 \propto \Theta_{\mathrm{ED}}^{-1/2}$ via Route A4; observed BAO scale provides $H_0$ + dark-energy-equation-of-state constraint via galaxy-survey distance-redshift measurements.

### §3.4 Transfer function $T(k)$

The transfer function $T(k)$ encodes the relative suppression of small-scale (large-$k$) modes that entered the horizon during radiation era compared to large-scale (small-$k$) modes that entered during matter era:

$$
T(k) \approx \begin{cases} 1 & k \ll k_{\mathrm{eq}} \\ (k_{\mathrm{eq}}/k)^2 \ln(k/k_{\mathrm{eq}}) & k \gg k_{\mathrm{eq}} \end{cases}
$$

(standard analytic approximation; precise $T(k)$ from CAMB / CLASS Boltzmann codes). Combined with primordial spectrum $P_{\mathrm{init}}(k) \propto k^{n_s - 1}$:

$$
P(k, a = 1) = D^2(1) \cdot T^2(k) \cdot P_{\mathrm{init}}(k)
$$

gives the present-epoch matter power spectrum with characteristic peak at $k_{\mathrm{eq}}$ + small-scale slope $P(k) \propto k^{n_s - 4}$ at $k \gg k_{\mathrm{eq}}$ + BAO oscillatory feature.

Substrate-graph composition: Cos_03 acoustic-physics + $k_{\mathrm{eq}}$ inheritance from Cos_01 + Cos_02 matter-radiation equality + standard cosmological-perturbation-theory transfer-function calculation.

### §3.5 Late-LDE suppression of growth

At late epochs ($z < z_\Lambda \approx 0.7$) when dark energy begins to dominate the cosmological energy budget, the growth factor $D(a)$ grows more slowly than $a$. Approximate analytic form:

$$
D(a) \propto a \cdot g(a), \quad g(a) \to 1 \text{ at } a \ll a_\Lambda, \quad g(a) \to \mathrm{const} \text{ at } a \gg a_\Lambda
$$

In the late-LDE / dark-energy-dominated asymptote, growth eventually freezes: $D(a) \to \mathrm{const}$ as universe approaches Cos_05 saturation regime (de Sitter). Growth-rate parameter $f \equiv d\ln D / d\ln a$ today $\approx \Omega_m^{0.55} \approx 0.5$ — observable via redshift-space-distortion (RSD) measurements in galaxy surveys.

The combination $f\sigma_8(z)$ is the standard structure-formation observable; measurements across $z \in [0, 1]$ from BOSS / eBOSS / DESI provide tight constraints on dark-energy equation-of-state + ED-vs-alternative-cosmology discriminators.
