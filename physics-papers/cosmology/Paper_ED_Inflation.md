## §3 Derivation

### 3.1 Ontology: saddle geometry, no metric, no fields

Per Paper_087, no fundamental spacetime metric, no fundamental scalar field, no fundamental potential, no Hilbert-space mode. ### 3.2 Substrate diffusion and production rates

Per Paper_073 DCGT (within hydrodynamic window $\ell_{ED} \ll R_{cg} \ll L_{flow}$), V1 + V5 supply diffusion-rate $\Gamma_{\mathrm{diff}}(\ell)$ as a substrate-graph quantity. ### 3.3 The diffusion–production imbalance

When $\Gamma_{\mathrm{diff}} > \Gamma_{\mathrm{prod}}$, diffusion redistributes $\Psi$ content faster than commitment can replenish. Compression-dominant Hessian axes deplete; expansion-dominant axes are sustained by diffusion-driven redistribution. Saddle-Hessian gradient-sign structure is temporarily unbalanced in favor of expansion. **Whether the substrate naturally produces this regime in the post-SCBU ignition phase is structurally plausible from Paper_ED_CCC §3.7 non-stationarity** but not constructively derived; substrate-graph closure OPEN (audit row 11).

### 3.4 Outward V1 propagation of the unbalanced-saddle pattern

The substrate-graph object that propagates is the **unbalanced-saddle Hessian-signature pattern**, not amplitude in a fixed $\Psi$-direction nor a spatial region per se. V1 retarded propagation along the kernel-arrow (Paper_089 + Paper_093 T18) carries this pattern outward at substrate-c — the substrate-graph region within which the unbalanced-saddle structure obtains grows at substrate-c.

**Direct substrate-c-bounded region-growth gives at most LINEAR scale-growth** $a(t) \sim t$ at substrate-graph level. **Exponential scale-growth $a(t) = a_0 e^{Ht}$ characteristic of standard inflation now closes via the M3 substrate-graph chain (§3.8 below):** the substrate-c bound is preserved at substrate-graph level, and exponential growth emerges as a continuum-side coarse-grained-geometry effect via DCGT translating the saturation-regime constant substrate-action density to a vacuum-energy-like continuum stress-energy. The quantitative value derivation is OPEN-4 (still OPEN; quantitative consolidation, not load-bearing for M3 framing).

### 3.5 Termination — production catches up

Inflation ends when $\Gamma_{\mathrm{prod}}$ recovers. Two structural mechanisms supplied by the post-SCBU ignition phase (Paper_ED_CCC §3.7): event-density climbs through ignition, lifting commitment frequency per Paper_012; expansion-dominant axes extending over larger substrate-graph regions reduce per-locus diffusion-rate. When $\Gamma_{\mathrm{prod}} \gtrsim \Gamma_{\mathrm{diff}}$, gradient-sign structure re-equilibrates; standard individuation regime (Paper_072) resumes. **Whether termination is smooth (not abrupt) and whether the regime duration corresponds to GR-side ~60 e-folds requires substrate-graph closure — OPEN** (audit rows 14, 15).
