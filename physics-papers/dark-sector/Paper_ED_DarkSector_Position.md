## 1. The two halves of "dark matter," and ED's split

"Dark matter" is not one observation; it is at least two, with different character, and they have resisted a single explanation for two decades:

- **Galactic dynamics** (rotation curves, the baryonic Tully–Fisher relation, a single acceleration scale `a₀`): fit *extraordinarily well* by MOND, and *badly* by particle dark matter, which has no reason to track the baryons so tightly.
- **The CMB acoustic peaks and cluster masses**: fit *extraordinarily well* by a cold, decoupled, `a⁻³` component (particle dark matter), and *badly* by pure MOND, which cannot supply a component that gravitates without oscillating with the baryon–photon fluid.

ED's position is the one the *data* has been pointing at: **MOND where MOND works (galaxies), a cold component where MOND does not (clusters, CMB).** The two are separated by *scale*, which is why they do not double-count (§5).

## 2. Galaxies: MOND, native, no particle needed *(strongest tier)*

ED reproduces galactic MOND phenomenology natively: the khronon (the arrow, P11, made dynamical) supplies the low-acceleration boost, with `a₀ = cH₀/2π` tied to the cosmic horizon (Papers KM-I, 027–030), and the baryonic Tully–Fisher relation with slope 4 (Paper_031). **No dark-matter particle is invoked for galaxies, and none is needed.** This is the sector's standing result and it is strong.

## 3. What "no dark matter" does *not* mean *(the correction a referee needs)*

The galactic result is routinely over-read — including, at length, in ED's own working notes — as "ED claims there is no dark matter." **It does not.** ED's own gravity papers state the boundary explicitly:

> *"ED does not refute dark matter as particle content."* — Paper_029 (a₀), Paper_031 (BTFR)

The galactic success shows a particle is **not needed for galaxies**; it says nothing against a particle existing as a *separate* component for clusters and the CMB. The door to a dark-matter particle was never closed by ED. (One specific candidate *is* closed in-corpus: the Planck-mass black-hole remnants are explicitly **not** the ED dark-matter candidate — Paper_041 §4 — being both future-forming and, at the crossover, dissolved.)

## 4. Clusters + CMB: a separate, mapped, addressable debt *(candidate direction)*

MOND handling galaxies does not resolve the **cluster-scale missing-mass shortfall** — flagged as a *separate* debt in KM-I §8 — nor the CMB acoustic peaks. These land on ED's cosmological sector and are mapped (not discharged) in `DarkSector_DebtMap.md`. The requirement is one component that (a) clusters like pressureless dust (`c_s ≈ 0`) at recombination and (b) supplies the missing cluster mass MOND alone cannot cover.

**ED's candidate for that component is a committed neutral relic**, produced at the inflation/saturation exit by the *same* admission mechanism that makes the baryons:

- **Native mechanism.** Baryogenesis in ED (Paper_ED_Baryogenesis, M3) is single-template admission under the saturation regime: a no-slack capacity frontier commits matter as the uniform early state breaks up. Applied on the *channel* axis rather than the chirality axis, commitment to the **EM (charged) channel** gives baryons; commitment to a **neutral channel** gives a decoupled dark relic.
- **Neutral ⟹ decoupled.** A zero-winding (uncharged) state does not couple to photons (charge = EM winding, Paper_ChargeAsTopology_B4), so it does not oscillate with the baryon–photon fluid — exactly the "held-still well-maker" the CMB peaks require.
- **Bound ⟹ cold-capable.** A committed relic is bound, and binding gives it rest mass (Paper_MassWithoutMass_BindingInertia, measured on the certified simulator), so it can be non-relativistic (cold). It dilutes as `a⁻³` and, being committed (P11-irreversible), survives reheating rather than re-thermalizing.
- **Structurally allowed.** ED's channel structure contains a neutral singlet (the "{1}" family; Paper_MS-II) — there is a slot for such a state.

This is **form-complete and native — not a bolt-on.** It is the neutral-channel sibling of the baryons, from the same exit, by the same mechanism.

## 5. Why there is no double-counting *(the galaxy/cluster reconciliation)*

The obvious objection — a cold component that clusters in galaxies would spoil the MOND rotation-curve fits — is answered by **scale separation**, and it is already the corpus's framing (KM-I §8; Round-5 §3):

- **Galaxies (~kpc):** the relic is too diffuse to fall into a galaxy-sized well (free-streaming length exceeds galactic scale), so galactic dynamics stay baryons + MOND boost, untouched.
- **Clusters (~Mpc):** the relic *can* concentrate at cluster scale and supplies the missing cluster mass.
- **CMB (largest scales):** the relic provides the decoupled acoustic dust; the khronon dial is sequestered from static tests by the Orthogonality Theorem (KM-II).

This is the MOND-plus-neutral-component picture with a literature precedent (νHDM; AeST is the relativistic-field version). It works **if** the relic's free-streaming length lands near cluster scale.

## 6. The honest open crux, and the risk *(the frontier)*

Everything above is form-complete. The whole account then turns on **one inherited number**: the relic's mass, which sets its free-streaming length.

- ED's mass sector produces **no numerical mass values** (Paper_MassWithoutMass); the binding magnitude is unpinned and the fundamental mass scale is inherited. The inflation-exit scale is likewise inherited (Cos_01). So the relic mass — and thus whether it lands in the galaxy-sparing window — is **value-inherited, not predicted.**
- Worse, natural-scale arguments lean the *wrong* way: a minimally-bound relic sits near the substrate (Planck) scale, and a baryon-sibling relic near the ~GeV scale — both **cold**, which is the galaxy-*unfriendly* end (cold matter clusters into galaxies and would spoil MOND). The *warm* relic the reconciliation needs is a light mass ED does not motivate.

So the dark sector is an **open frontier with a live failure path**: if the relic is forced cold and a cold relic is shown to break ED's own galactic MOND fits, this sector fails. It is not a bookkeeping debt and not an ad-hoc addition — it is a native, form-complete account whose one free value is expected to be problematic, stated plainly.

## 8. Falsifiers

- **F1:** A demonstration that ED's committed neutral relic is *forced* cold (Planck- or GeV-scale) **and** that a cold relic at the ~5×-baryon abundance breaks ED's galactic MOND fits — kills the reconciliation (§5–§6).
- **F2:** A CMB acoustic-peak computation from ED's cosmological sector that cannot reproduce the observed peak heights with the sequestered khronon dial plus the relic — the standing cluster/CMB debt becomes undischargeable (debt map).
- **F3:** Detection of a dark-matter particle whose properties are inconsistent with a neutral committed relic of ED's channel structure (e.g. a stable fundamental SU(N≥4)-charged state, which MS-II forbids).
- **F4:** Galactic dynamics shown to *require* a clustered dark component (not MOND) at galactic scale — would break §2 and the scale split.

## 9. Position

ED's dark sector is: **MOND for galaxies (native, strong, no particle needed); no refutation of a dark-matter particle; a separate, mapped cluster/CMB debt; and a form-complete, native candidate — a committed neutral relic from the saturation exit — whose one free value (the relic mass / free-streaming length) is inherited and expected to be problematically cold.** It is an honest open frontier with a named failure path, stated alongside the framework's cross-sector explanatory record — a frontier to be worked or falsified, not a solved sector and not a hidden hole.
