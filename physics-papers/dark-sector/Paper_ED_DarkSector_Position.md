## 1. The two halves of "dark matter," and ED's split

"Dark matter" is not one observation; it is at least two, with different character, and they have resisted a single explanation for two decades:

- **Galactic dynamics** (rotation curves, the baryonic Tully–Fisher relation, a single acceleration scale `a₀`): fit *extraordinarily well* by MOND, and *badly* by particle dark matter, which has no reason to track the baryons so tightly.
- **The CMB acoustic peaks and cluster masses**: fit *extraordinarily well* by a cold, decoupled, `a⁻³` component (particle dark matter), and *badly* by pure MOND, which cannot supply a component that gravitates without oscillating with the baryon–photon fluid.

ED's position is the one the *data* has been pointing at: **MOND where MOND works (galaxies), a cold component where MOND does not (clusters, CMB).** The two are separated by *scale*, which is why they do not double-count (§5).

## 2. Galaxies: MOND, native, no particle needed *(strongest tier)*

ED reproduces galactic MOND phenomenology natively: the khronon (the arrow, P11, made dynamical) supplies the low-acceleration boost, with `a₀ = cH₀/2π` tied to the cosmic horizon (Papers KM-I, 027–030), and the baryonic Tully–Fisher relation with slope 4 (Paper_031). **No dark-matter particle is invoked for galaxies, and none is needed.** This is the sector's standing result and it is strong.

## 3. What "no dark matter" does *not* mean *(the correction a referee needs)*

The galactic result is routinely over-read — including, at length, in ED's own working notes — as "ED claims there is no dark matter." **It does not.** ED's own gravity papers state the boundary explicitly:

> *"ED does not refute dark matter as particle content."* — Paper_029 (a₀), Paper_031 (BTFR)

The galactic success shows a particle is **not needed for galaxies**; it says nothing against a particle existing as a *separate* component for clusters and the CMB. The door to a dark-matter particle was never closed by ED. (One specific candidate *is* closed in-corpus: the Planck-mass black-hole remnants are explicitly **not** the ED dark-matter candidate — Paper_041 §4 — being both future-forming and, at the crossover, dissolved.)

This is **form-complete and native — not a bolt-on.** It is the neutral-channel sibling of the baryons, from the same exit, by the same mechanism.

## 5. Why there is no double-counting *(the galaxy/cluster reconciliation)*

The obvious objection — a cold component that clusters in galaxies would spoil the MOND rotation-curve fits — is answered by **scale separation**, and it is already the corpus's framing (KM-I §8; Round-5 §3):

- **Galaxies (~kpc):** the relic is too diffuse to fall into a galaxy-sized well (free-streaming length exceeds galactic scale), so galactic dynamics stay baryons + MOND boost, untouched.
- **Clusters (~Mpc):** the relic *can* concentrate at cluster scale and supplies the missing cluster mass.
- **CMB (largest scales):** the relic provides the decoupled acoustic dust; the khronon dial is sequestered from static tests by the Orthogonality Theorem (KM-II).

This is the MOND-plus-neutral-component picture with a literature precedent (νHDM; AeST is the relativistic-field version). It works **if** the relic's free-streaming length lands near cluster scale.

**The Bullet Cluster follows for free.** Being collisionless (neutral, no ram pressure), the relic sails through a cluster merger with the galaxies while the gas is ram-stripped, producing the famous gas–lensing offset the standard (ΛCDM-class) way — the Bullet is a *relic* (cluster-scale) phenomenon, not a MOND one. The same warm-`keV` window that keeps the relic out of galaxies keeps it clumped at cluster scale, so no extra mechanism is needed. This supersedes ED's earlier topological-defect reading (`gravity/Paper_117`) as the primary account; that paper's distinctive offset-velocity law survives only as a speculative bonus signal.

## 6. The honest open crux, and the risk *(the frontier)*

Everything above is form-complete. The whole account then turns on **one inherited number**: the relic's mass, which sets its free-streaming length.

- ED's mass sector produces **no numerical mass values** (Paper_MassWithoutMass); the binding magnitude is unpinned and the fundamental mass scale is inherited. The inflation-exit scale is likewise inherited (Cos_01). So the relic mass — and thus whether it lands in the galaxy-sparing window — is **value-inherited, not predicted.**
- **The window is now quantified** (`evaluation/DarkSector/relic_mass_bound.py`). Two-component consistency bounds the relic from both sides: it must be **warm enough to be diffuse in galaxies** (or it clumps as a halo ~5× the baryons and, with MOND on top, over-predicts rotation curves — the double-count), yet **cold enough to cluster at cluster scale and be pressureless dust by recombination** (the CMB peaks). Mapping free-streaming to a thermal-equivalent mass (standard WDM cosmology) puts the relic at **~0.2–0.7 keV** — the **warm dark matter** window, coinciding with the much-studied keV sterile-neutrino candidate (neutral, warm). What is really bounded is the relic's *warmth* (free-streaming ~0.1–1 Mpc); the keV is the thermal-equivalent, and because ED's relic is non-thermal (committed at the saturation exit), its mass at fixed warmth depends on the inherited production velocity.
- **This is a genuine, falsifiable directional prediction:** ED's dark sector must be **warm (~keV), not cold WIMP-like CDM.** If dark matter is shown to be a heavy cold WIMP, or perfectly cold with no warm free-streaming, ED's picture fails. That is a real bet against ΛCDM's cold default.
- **The tension (the live risk), now with numbers:** ED's mass *mechanism* leans the wrong way — a baryon-sibling binding scale (~GeV) is ~6 orders too heavy, the substrate (Planck) scale ~26 orders too heavy. Both are **cold**. So consistency *demands* ~keV warm while the mechanism *offers* GeV–Planck cold: a gap ED must close, either by motivating a keV-scale (sterile-neutrino-like) relic or by a *hot production* channel that decouples warmth from mass (in tension with Paper_MassWithoutMass's "the composite heads to rest").
- **One constraint is softer than it looks:** the standard Lyman-α bound (`m_WDM ≳ 3–5 keV`) that would *exclude* a sub-keV relic assumes the relic makes the small-scale structure. In ED it does **not** — MOND does — so that bound does not directly apply, and the relic may sit warmer/lighter than Lyman-α would otherwise allow. This ties the relic's viability to MOND reproducing the small-scale (Lyman-α) power, its own open test.

**Can ED *derive* the keV scale (turn the prediction into a first-principles result)? No — and the reason locates it, not excuses it (2026-07-19 corpus survey).** Every candidate handle is blocked by the *general* ED mass-value wall, not a dark-sector-specific gap: (i) a keV *mass value* is refused for the same reason as the electron mass — ED derives no mass values, and the mass hierarchy/ratios were attempted six ways and all refuted (Paper_113 "Arc-M six ratio-mechanism refutation"); (ii) there is no ED neutrino-mass, sterile-neutrino, or see-saw mechanism (Paper_116: the neutrino-mass case is INHERITED / empirical, the massless-slot occupancy OPEN); (iii) the inflation-exit / reheating epoch that would set a production warmth is itself OPEN and inherited (Cos_01, Route C1), and an early-produced relic redshifts *cold* regardless. A tempting shortcut — "the relic is neutral, so it binds weakly, so it is naturally light/warm" — is **invalid** and is recorded here so it is not re-derived: neutrality does not set the binding scale (the neutron is neutral and ~1 GeV). So the relic mass is **not a separable problem**: it is ED's standing mass-sector frontier (Paper_113's H1→H2 upgrade) wearing a dark-sector hat — the relic mass and the electron mass are pinned by the same breakthrough or neither is. What survives is exactly what did before the survey: the **directional prediction (warm, not cold-WIMP) is real and falsifiable**, the **inherited value (~keV, neutral) is a respected candidate** (the keV sterile neutrino), and the **scale is inherited**, sitting below ED's two natural reference scales (Planck grain, GeV baryon-sibling), hence unmotivated though not forbidden.

So the dark sector is an **open frontier with a live failure path**: consistency forces a warm ~keV relic (a falsifiable prediction), but ED's mass mechanism naturally gives cold/heavy and cannot derive the required scale (the general mass-value wall), and if the relic is forced cold and a cold relic breaks ED's galactic MOND fits, the sector fails. It is not a bookkeeping debt and not an ad-hoc addition — a native, form-complete account whose one free value is now bounded to a specific, testable, and uncomfortable window, with its derivation identified as the same problem as deriving any ED mass, stated plainly.
