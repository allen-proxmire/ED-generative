## §3 Derivation

### §3.1 Hessian sign structure at substrate locus

Per Paper_ED_SC_4_9, the substrate-action Hessian $\mathcal{H}(\ell, \ell') = \delta^2 S_{\mathrm{sub}}/\delta\Psi(\ell)\delta\Psi(\ell')$ at each substrate locus has eigenvalue decomposition

$$
\mathcal{H} \vec u_i = \lambda_i \vec u_i, \quad i = 1, \ldots, d_{\mathrm{sub}}
$$

where $\vec u_i$ are eigenvectors in the substrate-graph direction space and $\lambda_i$ are eigenvalues. The eigenvalue-signature pattern $\mathrm{sign}(\lambda_1, \ldots, \lambda_d)$ partitions substrate directions into compression-dominant ($\lambda_i < 0$, substrate-action concavity — local *contraction* under V1/V5 dynamics) and expansion-dominant ($\lambda_i > 0$, convexity — local *expansion*).

This Hessian-signature classification is the **substrate-graph analog of standard Morse theory** applied to the substrate-action functional. It is form-IDENTIFIED at substrate-graph level via SC-4.9 + standard differential geometry of critical points.

### §3.2 Kernel-induced curvature

V1 + V5 kernels enter the Hessian via the kernel-derivative terms in $\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{sub}}[\Psi, \nabla_{V_1}\Psi, \nabla_{V_5}\Psi]$:

$$
\mathcal{H}_{\mathrm{kernel}}(\ell, \ell') = \sum_{K \in \{V_1, V_5\}} G_K \cdot K(\ell - \ell')
$$

where $G_K$ are kernel-derivative coupling coefficients. Per Memo_ED_RouteA_A2_Construct §2.2, the kernel terms have long-wavelength expansion $\tilde K(q) \to G_K \ell_K^2 q^2$ for $q \ll 1/\ell_K$, giving IR-limit dispersion $\lambda(q) = U''(\Psi^{\mathrm{const}}) + \beta q^2$ where $\beta = \sum_K G_K \ell_K^2$.

The **kernel-induced curvature** is the substrate-graph analog of the standard QFT field-curvature term; its sign determines whether V1/V5 propagation around the saddle is stable (positive curvature; oscillatory) or unstable (negative curvature; growth/collapse).

### §3.3 Saddle identification — compression vs expansion directions

At a generic non-degenerate substrate-action saddle, the Hessian eigenvalue spectrum has mixed signature. The orthogonal directions partition into:

- **Compression-dominant axes** ($\lambda_i < 0$): substrate-action increases under contraction along $\vec u_i$ → local dynamics favor *collapse* in $\vec u_i$ direction
- **Expansion-dominant axes** ($\lambda_i > 0$): substrate-action decreases under contraction along $\vec u_i$ → local dynamics favor *expansion* in $\vec u_i$ direction

The number of compression-dominant axes vs expansion-dominant axes defines the *Morse index* of the saddle. The three universal regime classes of §1 correspond to Morse-index extremes ($d_{\mathrm{sub}}$ or $0$, all-compression or all-expansion) and the intermediate proper-saddle range ($0 < \mathrm{index} < d_{\mathrm{sub}}$).

This is form-IDENTIFIED via SC-4.9 + standard Morse theory; no substrate-research-frontier work required.
