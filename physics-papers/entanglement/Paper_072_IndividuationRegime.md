## Abstract

Entanglement is structurally identified in ED as the **unresolved regime of participation-rule individuation**: a substrate-level configuration where multiple rule-type assignments coexist without P11 commitment-irreversibility forcing a unique individuation. The identification composes: (i) UR-1 theorem (Paper_054) characterizing the unresolved regime at substrate level; (ii) V5 cross-chain correlation kernel (Paper_090) supplying the cross-chain participation correlation that defines bipartite entanglement; (iii) commitment-irreversibility (P11) marking the threshold above which the unresolved regime ends. Entanglement, in ED's framing, is **not** a non-local action-at-a-distance; it is the substrate-level signature of multi-rule-type coexistence before commitment selects individuation.

## §3 The Interpretation

### 3.1 The unresolved regime

By UR-1 (Paper_054), the substrate-level unresolved regime is the configuration $\mathcal{M}_{\mathrm{cap}}(s) < \mathcal{M}_{\mathrm{crit}}$ — multiple rule-type assignments coexist; P11 commitment-irreversibility has not yet selected a unique one.

### 3.2 V5 cross-chain content

V5 cross-chain correlation kernel (Paper_090) supports correlations **between** chain configurations even when no single chain has unique rule-type assignment. This is the substrate origin of bipartite entanglement: cross-chain correlations + per-chain unresolved-regime structure.

### 3.3 Entanglement as substrate signature

By P-Entanglement-Identification, bipartite entanglement **is** the substrate-level signature of:
- Each constituent chain in unresolved regime (UR-1).
- V5 cross-chain correlations between them.
- No P11 commitment yet forcing classical individuation.

The mathematical content (tensor-product, Schmidt, monogamy, no-signaling, von Neumann entropy — all from Papers_063–068) is the substrate-level manifestation of this unresolved regime.

### 3.4 Resolution = commitment

By P-Resolution-on-Commitment, when P11 commitment-irreversibility is crossed (typically by measurement coupling), the unresolved regime ends:
- Classical individuation results.
- V5 cross-chain correlations collapse to classical correlations.
- Entanglement is "destroyed" (consumed by commitment).

This is the substrate-level account of measurement-induced collapse.

### 3.5 What this is NOT

Entanglement is **not** "non-local action-at-a-distance" in this framing. It is the substrate-level signature of a regime that is **pre-individuated** — not yet committed. No "spooky" influence is required because no operational influence is transmitted (Paper_066 no-signaling).

This is an **interpretive A→position framing** that resolves several conceptual puzzles (action-at-a-distance, instantaneous collapse) at substrate level without modifying QM predictions.

## §4 Falsification Criteria

- **F1:** Empirical detection of entanglement without underlying unresolved substrate regime — refutes P-Entanglement-Identification.
- **F2:** Demonstration that commitment-irreversibility (P11) does NOT mark entanglement-destruction at substrate level — refutes P-Resolution-on-Commitment.
- **F3:** Substrate-level audit showing entanglement does support operational non-local influence — would refute the no-action-at-a-distance framing.
