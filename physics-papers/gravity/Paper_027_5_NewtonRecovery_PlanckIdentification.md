## §3 Derivation

### 3.1 Newton's law at substrate level

Paper_027 derives Newton's law $a = GM/r^2$ at substrate level with the gravitational constant given by
$$G = \frac{c^3 \ell_{ED}^2}{\hbar} ,$$
where $\ell_{ED}$ is the P08 substrate scale.

The substrate-level mechanism: V1 long-range kernel content produces a $1/r^2$ acceleration at scales $r \gg \ell_{ED}$ (Paper_089); the proportionality constant involves $\ell_{ED}^2$ through the substrate participation-budget scaling.

### 3.2 Empirical matching to Newton's law

Empirically, $G \approx 6.674 \times 10^{-11}\,\text{m}^3/(\text{kg}\,\text{s}^2)$.

The Planck length is defined as
$$\ell_P = \sqrt{\frac{\hbar G}{c^3}} \approx 1.616 \times 10^{-35}\,\text{m} .$$

Substituting $G = c^3 \ell_{ED}^2/\hbar$ from Paper_027:
$$\ell_P^2 = \frac{\hbar}{c^3} \cdot \frac{c^3 \ell_{ED}^2}{\hbar} = \ell_{ED}^2 .$$

Therefore **$\ell_{ED} = \ell_P$**. The substrate scale equals the Planck length.

### 3.3 Why this identification is forced

Three independent constraints jointly force $\ell_{ED} = \ell_P$:

**Constraint 1: Newton-recovery.** The substrate-gravity arc must reproduce Newton's law at appropriate scales. Paper_027's derivation gives $G$ in terms of $\ell_{ED}$.

**Constraint 2: V1 long-range behavior.** V1 kernel (Paper_089) has finite second moment $\ell_{V1}^2 \sim \ell_{ED}^2$; long-range $1/r^2$ behavior emerges at scales $r \gg \ell_{ED}$.

**Constraint 3: Empirical $G$ value.** $G$ has been measured to high precision; combined with Constraint 1, this fixes $\ell_{ED}^2 = \hbar G/c^3 = \ell_P^2$.

The combination is forced: any deviation $\ell_{ED} \neq \ell_P$ would produce a different $G$, contradicting empirical matching. The Planck identification is therefore the **unique** substrate-scale consistent with substrate gravity + Newton's law.

### 3.4 Why $\ell_P$, not some other Planck-class scale

Multiple Planck-class scales exist in standard physics:
- $\ell_P = \sqrt{\hbar G/c^3} \approx 1.6 \times 10^{-35}$ m.

The substrate framework picks out **specifically** $\ell_P = \sqrt{\hbar G/c^3}$ because this is the scale that appears directly in $G = c^3 \ell_{ED}^2/\hbar$ from Paper_027. Other conventions (reduced Planck, etc.) differ by $O(1)$ factors absorbed into the substrate normalization but do not change the identification's structural content.
