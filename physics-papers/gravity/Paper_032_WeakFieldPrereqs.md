This paper supplies the **six weak-field-limit prerequisites** referenced in Paper_039 §8.1 as the checkable structural-empirical conditions for bridging ED's flat-spacetime substrate-gravity arc to Arc ED-10 (curvature emergence). Each prerequisite is a testable structural-empirical commitment whose satisfaction is **load-bearing** for substrate-level curvature emergence:

6. **Prerequisite WF-6: V1 + V5 substrate kernels are gauge-covariant and Lorentz-covariant** in flat spacetime (Paper_089 §4.5–§4.6 + Paper_090 §4 — fulfilled).

The paper makes no claim that satisfaction of these six prerequisites implies curvature emergence (Arc ED-10 in-queue), no claim that they are exhaustive, and no claim that GR is derived. The six are the **checkable structural-empirical bridge points** from flat-spacetime gravity to substrate-level curvature emergence.

## 3. The Six Prerequisites

### WF-1. Newton's law at substrate-level cumulative-strain reading

**Statement:** *Substrate-level cumulative-strain reading of P12 (Paper_026, P-Potential-Reading) produces the Newtonian acceleration $a_N = GM/R^2$ from holographic-bound + V1 kernel structure, with $G = c^3\ell_{\mathrm{ED}}^2/\hbar$ assembled per Paper_027.*

**Cross-references:**

- Paper_026 (cumulative-strain reading).
- Paper_027 (Newton's $G$ from substrate constants).
- Paper_025 (holographic bound; P-Codim-1, P-Sat upstream).

**Fulfillment status:** **fulfilled** at structural-form level. $G$ numerical value identified via $\ell_{\mathrm{ED}} = \ell_P$ inheritance.

**Why load-bearing for Arc ED-10:** Newton's law is the weak-field limit of GR. Substrate-level reproduction of Newton (without invoking spacetime curvature) is necessary for the curvature-emergence bridge: any substrate-derived curvature must reduce to Newton in weak-field regime.

**Falsifier sentence:** *Empirical demonstration that the substrate-level cumulative-strain reading does not reproduce Newton's law at flat-spacetime scales — even after Paper_026 + Paper_027 derivations — would falsify WF-1.*

### WF-2. Transition acceleration $a_0 = cH_0/(2\pi)$ from cosmic decoupling

**Statement:** *Substrate-level dipole-projection of cosmic decoupling surface (Paper_028) produces the transition acceleration $a_0 = cH_0/(2\pi)$, structurally present via residual-$SO(2)$ Fourier-mode normalization (Paper_028 §6.4).*

**Cross-references:**

- Paper_028 (cosmic decoupling surface).
- Paper_029 (transition acceleration $a_0$).

**Fulfillment status:** **fulfilled** at structural-form level. Numerical match: $a_0^{\mathrm{ED}} \approx 1.08 \times 10^{-10}\,\mathrm{m/s^2}$ at $H_0 = 70\,\mathrm{km/s/Mpc}$; empirical MOND $a_0 \approx 1.2 \times 10^{-10}$; ~10% match within $H_0$-tension range.

**Why load-bearing for Arc ED-10:** $a_0$ is the substrate-level cosmic-anisotropy contribution. Curvature emergence must accommodate $a_0$ — any curvature-based gravity theory must produce the substrate-level transition acceleration scale.

**Falsifier sentence:** *Empirical resolution of the Hubble tension at $H_0$ outside the $[60, 80]\,\mathrm{km/s/Mpc}$ range, combined with tighter galaxy-catalog $a_0$ measurements that place empirical $a_0$ inconsistent with $cH_0/(2\pi)$ at the resolved $H_0$ — would falsify WF-2.*

### WF-3. ED Combination Rule $a = \sqrt{a_N a_0}$ in joint weak-gradient regime

**Statement:** *In the joint weak-gradient regime ($a_N \sim a_0$), substrate-level bilocal coupling (P14, Paper_030) produces the ED Combination Rule $a = \sqrt{a_N \cdot a_0}$.*

**Cross-references:**

- P14 placeholder (Paper_087 §4.14).

**Fulfillment status:** **fulfilled conditional on the P14 placeholder postulate** (Paper_030 §2; Paper_087 §4.14 placeholder slot). P14 is **not** in the canonical 13; whether P14 can be derived from canonical 13 (rather than remaining placeholder-postulated) is open. **WF-3's "fulfilled" status is therefore inherently conditional**: refutation of P14 (e.g., via empirical demonstration of non-bilocal-coupling structure in joint weak-gradient regime, Paper_030 §9) would unfulfill WF-3.

**Why load-bearing for Arc ED-10:** the ECR bridges Newtonian and deep-MOND regimes. Any curvature-based gravity must reproduce the ECR structural form in the joint weak-gradient regime.

**Falsifier sentence:** *Empirical demonstration that galactic rotation curves in the transition regime ($a \sim a_0$) follow a different combination — additive, max, non-geometric-mean power-law — would falsify WF-3 + P14.*

### WF-4. Slope-4 BTFR with zero intrinsic scatter

**Statement:** *Slope-4 baryonic Tully-Fisher relation $v_{\mathrm{flat}}^4 = GM_b a_0$ with zero intrinsic scatter holds in the deep-MOND asymptotic regime (Paper_031).*

**Cross-references:**

**Why load-bearing for Arc ED-10:** BTFR slope-4 + zero intrinsic scatter is the empirical signature of the substrate-gravity arc. Curvature emergence must preserve this.

**Falsifier sentence:** *Empirical demonstration of BTFR slope inconsistent with 4 (e.g., 3.5 or 4.5 at high precision) or non-zero intrinsic scatter beyond observational sources in the deep-MOND regime would falsify WF-4.*

### WF-5. Cumulative-strain reading produces additive potential structure

**Statement:** *Under Paper_026 P-Potential-Reading, the substrate-level cumulative-strain reading of P12 produces additive potential structure: $\Phi_{\mathrm{total}}(R) = \sum_{\mathrm{sources}}\Phi_i(R)$ for multiple mass sources (linear superposition in the Newtonian regime).*

**Cross-references:**

- Paper_026 (P-Potential-Reading, cumulative-strain reading).
- Paper_027 (Newton's $G$).

**Fulfillment status:** **fulfilled** at structural-form level for independent sources in flat spacetime.

**Why load-bearing for Arc ED-10:** linear superposition holds in the Newtonian regime; curvature-based gravity has non-linear field equations (Einstein equations), but the **weak-field limit** must reproduce linear superposition. WF-5 is the substrate-level statement of the linearity that curvature emergence must reduce to in weak-field regime.

**Falsifier sentence:** *Demonstration that the substrate-level cumulative-strain reading produces non-additive potential structure even in weak-field regime — i.e., multiple-source potentials at substrate level do not superpose linearly at flat-spacetime scales — would falsify WF-5.*

**Why load-bearing for Arc ED-10:** V1 + V5 substrate kernels must be gauge-covariant and Lorentz-covariant in flat spacetime for curvature emergence to operate on them via DCGT or Arc-ED-10-specific machinery. WF-6 is the substrate-level Poincaré-symmetry baseline.

**Falsifier sentence:** *Empirical demonstration of substrate-level kernel-mediated content with broken Lorentz covariance or broken $U(1)$ gauge-covariance in any regime where the substrate framework predicts these symmetries hold — would falsify WF-6 and the substrate-gravity arc foundations.*
