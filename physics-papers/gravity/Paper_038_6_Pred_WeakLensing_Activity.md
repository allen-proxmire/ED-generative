## §3 Derivation

### 3.1 Standard weak-lensing in the MOND-class regime

Weak-lensing measurements infer total gravitational potential along the line of sight through the lensing convergence $\kappa(\theta)$ and shear $\gamma(\theta)$. In the MOND-class regime (deep-MOND galactic outskirts, low-acceleration regime $a \ll a_0$), the gravitational acceleration is
$$a(r) \;=\; \sqrt{a_N(r) \cdot a_0} \quad \text{(ED Combination Rule, Paper_030)} ,$$
producing a logarithmic gravitational potential and flat rotation curves.

The corresponding weak-lensing signal in MOND-class is enhanced at large radii compared to Newtonian baryonic content alone — this is empirically attributable to a "dark-matter-like" effective mass distribution.

### 3.2 V5 mobility content and activity

By Paper_090, V5 cross-chain kernel has finite bandwidth $\ell_{V5}$ and memory $\tau_{V5}$. The substrate-level **mobility** field $\mu(x)$ (Paper_EDSC_AcousticCurvature) is supplied by V5 cross-chain content: regions with high cross-chain coupling have high mobility; regions with low cross-chain coupling have low mobility.

**Activity-dependence:** High-activity systems (star-forming, AGN-active, dynamically-unrelaxed) exhibit:
- Large local mobility gradients $|\nabla\mu(x)|$ from non-equilibrium substrate participation rearrangement.
- Enhanced V5 cross-chain bandwidth circulation as substrate content reorganizes.
- Time-varying $\mu(x, t)$ during the activity episode.

Low-activity systems (quiescent, relaxed) exhibit:
- Smooth mobility gradients (quasi-equilibrium $\mu$).
- Stationary V5 cross-chain bandwidth.
- Time-stationary $\mu(x)$.

The substrate-level distinction is structural: activity = non-equilibrium V5 mobility content; quiescence = equilibrium V5 mobility content.

### 3.3 Acoustic-metric curvature modification from activity

By Paper_EDSC_AcousticCurvature, acoustic-metric curvature scales as $R_{\mathrm{ac}} \propto |\nabla\mu|^2/\mu^2$. High-activity systems with large $|\nabla\mu|$ exhibit enhanced local curvature; low-activity systems exhibit relaxed curvature.

### 3.4 Weak-lensing scaling laws

The activity-dependent $\mu_{\mathrm{eff}}$ modifies the deep-MOND gravitational potential. Weak-lensing convergence $\kappa$ inherits the modification:

**High-activity systems:**
$$\kappa_{\mathrm{active}}(\theta) \;=\; \kappa_{\mathrm{MOND}}(\theta) \cdot [1 + \alpha_{\mathrm{act}} \cdot \langle\mathcal{A}\rangle] ,$$
with enhanced lensing signal proportional to activity strength.

**Low-activity systems:**
$$\kappa_{\mathrm{quiescent}}(\theta) \;\approx\; \kappa_{\mathrm{MOND}}(\theta) ,$$
