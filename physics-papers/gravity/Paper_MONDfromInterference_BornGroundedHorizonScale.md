## 3. Where the Interfering Form Comes From (and Where It Does Not)

Q1, the load-bearing modeling commitment, is that gravitational strain is quadratic/interfering (`|Σ P|²`), not linear. It is important to attribute each part correctly, because the Born rule does *less* work here than it might appear.

**Quadratic-in-amplitude is definitional, and the Born rule only confirms it.** ED's participation amplitude is `P = √b · e^{iπ}` (Paper_001), so `b = |P|²`: bandwidth *is* the squared amplitude, by definition. The QM keystone's phase-invariance argument adds only that a *linear*-in-amplitude quantity cannot be a physical observable: under a global phase `P → e^{iθ}P`, `Re P` runs through `0.81, −0.17, −1.29, 0.24` (probe) while `|P|²` stays fixed. So a linear-in-amplitude gravitational strain is inadmissible. But this confirms the definition; it does not supply anything new, and it does not by itself force *interference*.

**The interference comes from the shared channel, not the Born rule.** Phase-invariance says the observable is built from `|amplitude|²`; it is *silent* on whether N sources add *coherently* (`|Σ P_i|²`, with cross terms) or *incoherently* (`Σ |P_i|²`, diagonal only). That is decided by whether the sources share one channel. In ED they do, by the P14 single-carrier structure (one channel carries one bandwidth (P04) and one phase (P09), hence one amplitude `Σ P_i`), so the sourcing quantity is `b_tot = |Σ P_i|²` *with* interference. **This shared-channel input is the load-bearing step, and it is an input, not a consequence of the Born rule.** The credit for the interference belongs to the single-carrier channel structure (corpus-native in P04+P07+P09), not to the QM keystone.

**The one genuine clarification.** What the quadratic-form view *does* settle is the "linear-vs-quadratic" confusion. Paper_026 builds gravitational strain linearly *in mass* (`Σ b_i`), which is *quadratic in amplitude at the diagonal*. So there was never a conflict at the amplitude level: `|Σ P|²` splits into the diagonal `Σ b_i` (`= 7.04` in the probe, additive Newton, linear in mass) plus the off-diagonal interference (`= 4.85`, the term Paper_026 dropped). Paper_026 built the diagonal; the shared channel adds the off-diagonal. Newton and MOND are the diagonal and off-diagonal of one form. (A probe also shows that averaging `|Σ P|²` over random relative phases returns the diagonal `Σ b_i` to `0.1%`, but this is the tautology that random phases wash out cross terms, shown for illustration, not a confirmation of anything.) The physics question is therefore *not* "why is there interference?" but "why is the off-diagonal negligible everywhere Newton holds, yet nonzero against the horizon?", which is the coherence structure of §4.

## 4. Newton (Diagonal) and MOND (Off-Diagonal) Coexist via a Coherence Asymmetry

Write the quadratic form for local sources plus the cosmic horizon:
$$ b_{\text{tot}} = \Big|\sum_i P_i\Big|^2 = \underbrace{\sum_i b_i}_{\text{diagonal = Newton}} + \underbrace{\sum_{i\neq j}\sqrt{b_i b_j}\,\cos(\pi_i-\pi_j)}_{\text{off-diagonal = interference}}. $$

**The obstruction (confronted).** The off-diagonal must vanish for local-local pairs (or the solar system shows anomalous source-source forces, unobserved to `~10⁻⁸`) yet survive for local-horizon pairs (to give MOND). A single "phases are random" setting kills both: random enough to wash out local-local is random against the horizon too, so MOND dies; full coherence breaks Newton. A probe confirms both failures. So the naive recast fails along the whole coherence axis.

**The partial resolution (an exact identity).** Give the local phases a small common bias `σ = |⟨e^{iπ}⟩|` toward the horizon reference (phase 0; the horizon-ward *direction* fixes the attractive sign of the effect and is itself part of the assumption). Then, for independent phases,
$$ \underbrace{\langle\cos(\pi_i-\pi_j)\rangle_{i\neq j} = |\langle e^{i\pi}\rangle|^2 = \sigma^2}_{\text{local-local (Newton-breaking)}}, \qquad \underbrace{\langle\cos(\pi_i-0)\rangle = \sigma}_{\text{local-horizon (MOND)}}. $$
The Newton-breaking local-local term is *quadratically* suppressed relative to the MOND-giving local-horizon term (probe-confirmed to three decimals across `σ ∈ [0.025, 0.81]`). This asymmetry is the mechanism, and it is exact.

**The unresolved tension, and why it is entangled with the dimensional bridge (disclosed, not solved).** Coherence is *transitive*, so the suppression is not by `σ²` alone: if each local mass is `σ`-coherent with the horizon (as MOND requires), any two are mutually `σ²`-coherent, and the local-local off-diagonal sums over all pairs, scaling as `σ²·(N−1)` in the number `N` of separate sources. Many-body Newton then requires `σ²·(N−1) ≪ 1`, while the MOND term `σ·√(b_hor/b)` must be O(1). The critical point is that **the value of `σ` is not free but is not consistently determined either**, and the two constraints pull it to numerically incompatible values:

- The paper's own §6 (deep-MOND normalization, `2σ = a₀_obs/a₀_horizon ≈ a₀/cH₀ ≈ 0.18`) needs **`σ ≈ 0.09`**, at which `σ²·N` fails for `N ≳ 100`.
- An attempted rescue that fixes `σ` from the onset (`σ² ~ b_onset/b_hor`) and appeals to a huge horizon bandwidth gives **`σ ~ 10⁻³⁷`** only by counting the horizon holographically (area, `(R_H/ℓ_P)² ~ 10¹²³`) while counting the local system by mass (`M/m_Pl ~ 10⁵⁰`); a horizon's area count is the *square* of its mass count, so that mismatch is a spurious `~10⁶²` inflation. Counted consistently (both by mass), `σ ~ 10⁻⁶` and a galaxy at star-counting already gives `σ²·N ~ 0.3`, i.e. Newton fails.

So the `σ` that would protect Newton and the `σ` that sets `a₀` differ by many orders, and which value is correct hinges on the **unworked bandwidth↔acceleration bridge** (§7, preamble 5). The many-body tension and that dimensional gap are therefore *one* problem, not two, and until the bridge is worked in a single consistent bandwidth measure (same for the local source and the horizon) that both reproduces the fixed-acceleration universal MOND onset and lands `σ` in a Newton-safe range, **Newton-safety is not established. This is the mechanism's central open problem and a genuine potential showstopper**, stated as such rather than buried. What *is* clean: the strong-field limit is safe regardless (when `g_N ≫ a₀` the cross-term is `≪` the diagonal, §6), and the within-particle case is safe (the two paths of one particle share its alignment, which cancels; §5), but neither addresses the between-mass many-body sum.

## 9. Falsification Criteria

### 9.1 Gravitational strain is not interfering

**Falsifier sentence:** *A demonstration that ED's gravitational strain is not a quadratic/interfering observable (that a linear-in-amplitude gravitational strain is admissible, contrary to phase-invariance, or that the sources do not share a bilocal channel so no cross term exists) would remove the interference and with it the entire MOND sector (§3-4).*

### 9.2 The coherence asymmetry does not hold

**Falsifier sentence:** *A demonstration that no single `σ` can be simultaneously small enough to hold the many-body Newton violation `σ²·(N−1)` below observational bounds and large enough to source the MOND term would falsify the Newton+MOND coexistence (this is the mechanism's central open tension, §4, and its most likely failure mode). ### 9.3 The σ-alignment breaks QM/gauge

**Falsifier sentence:** *A detected effect of the horizon phase reference on local (co-local) interference or on gauge observables (a σ-dependent double-slit visibility, an observable global phase, a photon mass) would falsify the QM/gauge consistency (§5) and kill the mechanism.*

### 9.4 The interpolation function is wrong

**Falsifier sentence:** *Galactic rotation curves requiring an interpolation function outside the family bracketed by the simple and standard forms, or requiring particle dark matter rather than the low-acceleration scalar sector, would falsify the μ(x) result (§6).*

### 9.5 a₀ does not track a horizon

**Falsifier sentence:** *A measured a₀ that neither matches the cosmic-horizon acceleration scale (`~cH₀ ~ c²√Λ`) within an O(1) factor nor tracks a horizon (constant if the de Sitter/Λ horizon; redshift-evolving if the Hubble horizon) would falsify the a₀-as-horizon-scale result (§7). ## Appendix: Glossary and Reader Map

### Glossary

- **Quadratic / interfering strain.** Gravitational strain as `|Σ P|²`, not a linear sum. Diagonal `Σ b_i` = Newton; off-diagonal interference = MOND. The quadratic-in-amplitude form is definitional (`b=|P|²`); the interference (the off-diagonal) comes from the shared channel, not the Born rule.
- **Born rule (here).** The physical observable is `|amplitude|²` (quadratic, phase-invariant), not the amplitude (linear, not observable). Applied to ED's `b = |P|²` this only confirms the definitional quadratic form; it does **not** force the *interference*, which comes from the shared bilocal channel (single-carrier).
- **σ (horizon-aligned phase bias).** A small universal alignment of local participation phases toward the cosmic-horizon reference. Gives local-local coherence `σ²` (Newton) and local-horizon coherence `σ` (MOND). Account-tier; sets at most the dimensionless O(1) coefficient of a₀ (not a₀'s scale, §7), and is bounded by the many-body-Newton tension (§4).
- **Coherence asymmetry.** The exact identity that local-local coherence (`σ²`) is quadratically smaller than local-horizon coherence (`σ`). It lets Newton and MOND coexist per-pair, but the local-local term sums as `σ²·(N−1)` over sources, the unresolved many-body tension (§4).
- **μ_ED(x).** The interpolation function `[√(1+4x²)−1]/(2x)`; correct Newton and deep-MOND limits; near (not identical to) the simple and standard forms; exact shape model-dependent.
- **a₀ as a horizon scale.** The MOND acceleration *scale* `~ c²/R_H ~ cH₀`, the cosmic horizon's own acceleration, forced (as a scale) because MOND is the local-vs-horizon interference sector; the dimensionless O(1) coefficient is not derived. Conjecturally the same `b→0` endpoint as dark energy Λ (`a₀ ~ c²√Λ`).
