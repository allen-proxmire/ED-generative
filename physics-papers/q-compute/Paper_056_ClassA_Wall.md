## 3. The Multiplicity-Cap Function

### 3.1 Substrate effective multiplicity $M_{\mathrm{eff}}$

For a coherent state $|\Psi\rangle = \sum_K c_K |K\rangle$:

$$
M_{\mathrm{eff}} = \frac{(\sum_K |c_K|^2)^2}{\sum_K |c_K|^4} = \frac{(\sum_K b_K)^2}{\sum_K b_K^2}.
$$

Inverse-participation-ratio measure; counts effective coherent-superposition substrate channels. Uniform superposition over $N$ channels: $M_{\mathrm{eff}} = N$.

### 3.2 Mass-scaling of composite-system multiplicity

- **Molecular systems (matter-wave):** $N_{\mathrm{DOF}}(m) \sim \alpha\,(m/m_u)$ with $\alpha$ an order-unity molecular-structure coefficient.
- **Multi-qubit platforms (SC, ion):** $N_{\mathrm{DOF}}$ scales with qubits + ancilla + control DOFs.

Effective multiplicity in Class-A configurations:

$$
M_{\mathrm{eff}}(m) \approx \alpha\beta\,(m/m_u),
$$

with $\beta \lesssim 1$ the fraction of total DOFs actually participating in the coherent superposition.

### 3.3 The substrate-level cap function $M_{\mathrm{cap}}$

By P11, substrate-level commitment-injection rate scales as $\Gamma_{\mathrm{commit}}(M_{\mathrm{eff}}) \approx \Gamma_0\,M_{\mathrm{eff}}^\gamma$, with $\Gamma_0$ set by V1 finite-width scale and $\gamma$ a substrate-level exponent. UR-1 (§4) requires $\Gamma_{\mathrm{commit}} < \Gamma_{\mathrm{indiv}}^{-1}$; setting these equal defines the cap:

$$
M_{\mathrm{cap}} = \left(\frac{\Gamma_{\mathrm{indiv}}^{-1}}{\Gamma_0}\right)^{1/\gamma}.
$$

$M_{\mathrm{eff}} < M_{\mathrm{cap}}$ → coherent superposition maintained; $M_{\mathrm{eff}} > M_{\mathrm{cap}}$ → commitment triggered, superposition resolves. **Transition is sharp** at substrate level (power-law $M_{\mathrm{eff}}^\gamma$ exceeds threshold rapidly relative to coherent-evolution timescale).

### 3.4 Empirical anchoring of $M_{\mathrm{cap}}$

The substrate-level coefficients $\Gamma_0$, $\gamma$, $\alpha$, $\beta$ are *characterized* by the substrate primitives but their *specific numerical values* are anchored from existing experiments:

- **Eibenberger 2013:** matter-wave coherence at $\sim 10$ kDa → $M_{\mathrm{eff}}(10\,\mathrm{kDa}) < M_{\mathrm{cap}}$.
- **Fein 2019:** matter-wave coherence at $\sim 25$ kDa → $M_{\mathrm{eff}}(25\,\mathrm{kDa}) < M_{\mathrm{cap}}$.

Both confirm $M_{\mathrm{cap}}$ is larger than these masses. Extrapolating from anchors with the substrate-level scaling form gives the 140–250 kDa Class-A wall extrapolation (§5.2).

### 4.3 Why UR-1 has three components — and why the Class A/B/C ↔ UR-1.1/1.2/1.3 mapping is structurally justified (not just suspicious symmetry)

The reviewer flagged the structural symmetry — three architectural classes, three UR-1 components, one-to-one mapping — as suspicious. This is an honest concern. Here is the structural defense, including a more general statement of the relationship.

3. **Single-channel commitment-injection rate** — how fast substrate-level commitment is injected at fixed multiplicity due to stability-landscape gradient (UR-1.3, distinct from UR-1.1's *multiplicity-driven* commitment growth).

These three rate quantities are **structurally independent at the substrate level**: each is set by distinct primitive content (P11 commitment-injection coupling to P12 vs. P02/P04 multiplicity content vs. V5 cross-chain bandwidth structure). Failing any one breaks the unresolved regime via a structurally distinct mechanism.

The mapping is therefore **architectural strategies = engineering each independent failure-mode lever**, not an arbitrary three-way classification. The three classes are the **structurally distinct ways to engineer coherence preservation** given UR-1's three structurally distinct failure modes.

**More general relationship — hybrid architectures and dominant-lever classification.** The strict one-to-one map A↔UR-1.1, B↔UR-1.2, C↔UR-1.3 is the **pure-class** statement: each class engages exactly one lever. The substrate-level structural form *of each class's wall behavior* is the constrained prediction, not the three-way classification itself. $$
\alpha\beta\,(m_{\mathrm{wall}}/m_u) = M_{\mathrm{cap}} \implies m_{\mathrm{wall}} = \frac{M_{\mathrm{cap}}\,m_u}{\alpha\beta}.

**The structural prediction is the *existence* of such an $m_{\mathrm{wall}}$ as a sharp, architecture-independent (within Class-A) boundary**, not the specific numerical value of $m_{\mathrm{wall}}$.

$$
m_{\mathrm{wall}} \approx 140\text{–}250\,\mathrm{kDa}\quad(\text{extrapolation from anchors, central } \sim 180\,\mathrm{kDa}).

The range covers:

- **Substrate-level coefficient uncertainty** in $\alpha, \beta, \gamma$ given only two anchor points.

## 7. Why Decoherence-Only Models Cannot Predict a Sharp Architecture-Independent Wall

### 7.1 Standard decoherence analysis

In standard QM + environmental decoherence:

$$
\Gamma_{\mathrm{decoh}} \sim N \cdot \lambda^2 \cdot k_B T / \hbar
$$

(schematic). Mass scale at which coherence is unsustainable depends on bath coupling $\lambda$, temperature $T$, DOF count $N$, isolation timescale. Scenario-dependent estimates span orders of magnitude.

### 7.2 Why standard analysis cannot give a sharp architecture-independent wall

- **Decoherence rates are continuous functions** of mass, coupling, temperature. No sharp transition.
- **Operationally-defined coherence limits** depend on experimental signal-to-noise, not on a fundamental threshold.
- **No architecture-independent prediction:** every prediction depends on platform-specific bath structure.

### 7.3 Decoherence is real, but is not the wall

Decoherence is real and matters for experimental design. The substrate-level claim is more specific:

- **Decoherence affects coherence *within* the unresolved regime.** Decoherence-mitigation (cooling, isolation, error correction within regime) can extend coherence times but cannot push past the substrate-level UR-1.1 wall.
- **The Class-A wall is a structural boundary of the unresolved regime itself.** Even with infinite isolation and zero environmental coupling, Class-A platforms hit the wall at the multiplicity-cap scale.

This is the substrate-level prediction that distinguishes ED from decoherence-only accounts.

### 7.4 Cross-platform unification as evidence

If the wall is purely decoherence-driven: different platforms should show wildly different ceiling masses reflecting different bath structures. If the wall is substrate-level: ceilings (modulo platform-scaling) should be consistent.
