## §3 The Seven Substrate-Level Results

### 3.1 $\mathcal{M}_{\mathrm{cap}}$ as one object (Paper_053)

### 3.2 UR-1 theorem (Paper_054)

Unresolved-Regime Characterization theorem: the regime $\mathcal{M}_{\mathrm{cap}}(s) < \mathcal{M}_{\mathrm{crit}}$ is the substrate-level definition of "unresolved" (quantum-coherent / superposition-supporting) configurations.

### 3.3 A/B/C exhaustiveness (Paper_055)

Three architectural classes A (engineered-low-multiplicity), B (global-geometric-rigidity), C (high-multiplicity-redundancy) are FORCED-exhaustive.

### 3.4 Three sharp predictions (Papers_056–058)

- **Class A wall (140–250 kDa):** matter-wave Q-C boundary.
- **Class B exponential gap-suppression:** global-geometric-rigidity platforms exhibit exponential suppression of gap-protected quantum behavior with system size.
- **Class C correlation-budget plateau:** high-multiplicity-redundancy platforms exhibit plateau in achievable correlation budget per qubit.

### 3.5 Meta-architectures as compositions (Paper_059)

EC, DD, reservoir engineering, hybrids are substrate-shifts of binding constraints; not new classes.

### 3.6 Matter-wave / qubit unification (Paper_060)

Single $\mathcal{M}_{\mathrm{crit}}$ threshold, cross-platform universal.

### 3.7 Conceptual reframing: decoherence → multiplicity

Standard quantum-computing framings emphasize decoherence (environment-induced loss of coherence) as the bottleneck. ED's reframing: **multiplicity** is the substrate-level bottleneck, with decoherence appearing as a coarse-grained symptom of substrate-level commitment-irreversibility under $\mathcal{M}_{\mathrm{cap}}(s) \to \mathcal{M}_{\mathrm{crit}}$.
