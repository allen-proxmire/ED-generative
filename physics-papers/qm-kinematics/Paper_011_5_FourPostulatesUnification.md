## §3 The Four-Postulate Unification

### 3.1 Postulate I: State as Hilbert-space vector

**Standard postulate:** A pure quantum state is represented by a normalized vector $|\Psi\rangle$ in a complex separable Hilbert space $\mathcal{H}$.

**Substrate-level derivation:**
- Participation measure $P_K = b_K e^{i\pi_K} \in \mathbb{C}$ assigned to each motif $K$ (Paper_U1).
- Sesquilinear inner-product (Paper_U2-Discrete + Paper_U2-Continuum) on the motif-space yields a Hilbert space.
- Hilbert-space emergence via completion of motif algebra (Paper_007).
- Normalization $\sum_K b_K^2 = 1$ (Born-Gleason, Paper_002).

The "state" is the participation measure on substrate motifs; the "Hilbert space" is its natural arena.

### 3.2 Postulate II: Observables as self-adjoint operators

**Standard postulate:** Physical observables are represented by self-adjoint operators on $\mathcal{H}$.

**Substrate-level derivation:**
- Position operator $\hat x$ (multiplicative; from P03 locus indexing).
- Momentum operator $\hat p = -i\hbar\nabla$ (translation generator; Paper_U5).
- Hamiltonian $\hat H$ (time-translation generator; Paper_U3).
- All are self-adjoint by Stone's theorem applied to substrate-level symmetry groups.

The "observables" are infinitesimal generators of substrate-level symmetry groups (P03 spatial, P13 temporal, etc.); self-adjointness is forced by Stone's theorem on the substrate Hilbert space.

### 3.3 Postulate III: Born rule

**Standard postulate:** Probability of outcome $a$ on measuring observable $\hat A$ in state $|\Psi\rangle$ is $|\langle a|\Psi\rangle|^2$.

**Substrate-level derivation:**
- Born-Gleason rule (Paper_002): $\mathrm{Prob}(K) = |P_K|^2 = b_K^2$.
- Substrate origin: P04 bandwidth content $b_K^2$ is the substrate-level participation density, which under operational measurement provides the empirical frequency = probability.

The Born rule is the **bandwidth-to-probability** identification at substrate level. It is form-COMPOSED by P04 + measurement-projection structure (no D rows in audit table; downgraded from FORM-FORCED per Paper_095 §2.3).

### 3.4 Postulate IV: Measurement / unitary evolution

**Standard postulate (dual):**
- **Unitary evolution** between measurements: $|\Psi(t)\rangle = U(t)|\Psi(0)\rangle$ with $U(t) = e^{-i\hat H t/\hbar}$.
- **Measurement-induced collapse:** measurement projects $|\Psi\rangle$ onto eigenspace of measured observable.

**Substrate-level derivation:**
- Unitary evolution: Paper_U3 (Stone's theorem) + Paper_006 (V1 kernel propagation).
- Measurement collapse: Paper_005 (P11 commitment-irreversibility producing the projection).

Both halves of Postulate IV emerge from substrate primitives: P11 (commitment) supplies the collapse direction; P13 (time homogeneity) supplies the unitary-evolution generator structure.

### 3.5 Why the four postulates are NOT independent in ED

In standard axiomatic QM, the four postulates are typically presented as **independent** axioms. In the ED substrate framework:

- **States and observables share a single substrate origin:** the participation measure $P_K$.
- **Born rule is the substrate-level identification** of $|P_K|^2$ with probability — not an independent axiom.
- **Unitary evolution and measurement-collapse share a single substrate origin:** P11 (commitment-irreversibility) supplies both the directional flow of unitary time-translation AND the irreversible collapse at measurement.

The four standard postulates are therefore **four facets of one substrate-level structure**. The unification is the substantive synthesis content of this paper.
