## §3 Derivation

### 3.1 Substrate mass parameter $\sigma$

A rule-type's **substrate mass parameter** $\sigma$ is the substrate-level analog of the standard particle mass $m$. It is the rest-frame bandwidth-budget content of the rule-type — the substrate-cell budget required to "carry" the rule-type at rest.

For rule-types with $\sigma = 0$, the rest-frame budget is zero; **the rule-type has no rest frame**. Its propagation is dictated entirely by V1 kernel content and substrate dispersion at $m = 0$.

### 3.2 Massless dispersion relation

Setting $\sigma = 0$ in the relativistic dispersion:
$$E^2 = p^2 c^2 ,$$
i.e., $E = |p| c$ (taking positive-frequency branch).

For a $\sigma = 0$ rule-type, the substrate propagates the amplitude along a worldline satisfying
$$\frac{dE}{dp} = c , \quad \text{i.e., group velocity} = c .$$

Standard relativistic kinematics: a particle with group velocity $c$ travels on a null geodesic of the spacetime metric. In the substrate framework, this is the acoustic metric $g^{\mathrm{ac}}_{\mu\nu}$ (Paper_017).

The worldline $x^\mu(\lambda)$ satisfies the null condition
$$g^{\mathrm{ac}}_{\mu\nu} \frac{dx^\mu}{d\lambda} \frac{dx^\nu}{d\lambda} = 0 .$$

This is the **lightlike worldline** statement.
