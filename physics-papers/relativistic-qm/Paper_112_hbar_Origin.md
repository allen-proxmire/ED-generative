## §3 Derivation

### 3.1 Action dimensions and chain-step structure

Standard QM places $\hbar$ as the conversion factor between action (units: energy × time) and phase ($e^{iS/\hbar}$). Action has dimensions $[S] = \text{kg·m}^2/\text{s} = \text{J·s}$.

The substrate-level question: what is the substrate-level origin of this dimensional combination?

P05 (polarity-transport) describes edge-traversal: a substrate chain traverses edges with polarity rotating under P09 ($U(1)$-valued polarity). Each chain-step (single edge traversal) accumulates:
- A finite increment of substrate time (per P11 commitment-irreversibility).
- A finite increment of polarity rotation (per P09 + P05).
- A finite participation budget consumption (per P04 bandwidth).

The **participation budget per chain-step** has dimensions of **bandwidth × time** = **action**. This is the substrate origin of $\hbar$'s dimensional structure.

### 3.2 Chain-step invariance

By P03 (channel + locus indexing, spatial homogeneity) and P13 (time homogeneity), the substrate is **homogeneous**: chain-steps at different loci and different times consume the same participation budget. The participation quantum per chain-step is a **substrate-invariant**.

This invariance is the substrate analog of the empirical observation that $\hbar$ is the same in every QM experiment, every laboratory, every epoch. The substrate-level statement: chain-step invariance under P03 + P13 forces $\hbar$ to be a universal constant.

### 3.3 ℏ as the invariant quantum

Definition:
$$\hbar \;\equiv\; \int_{\mathrm{one\,chain-step}} \mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{P04}}(s) \, ds .$$

The integral is over a single substrate chain-edge traversal. $\mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{P04}}(s)$ is the bandwidth density (P04) along the edge. By P03 + P13 invariance, this integral is the same across all substrate chain-steps; hence $\hbar$ is a universal constant.

By dimensional analysis:
- $[\mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{P04}}] = $ bandwidth = energy / time = $\text{J/s}$.
- $[ds] = $ time × c (for null-edge traversal) or time (for time-like edge traversal); in either case, dimensions of time.
- $[\hbar] = (\text{J/s}) \times \text{s} = \text{J·s}$.

The structural form of $\hbar$ as bandwidth × time is FORM-FORCED.

**Circularity disclaimer.** $\mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{P04}}$ has no independent substrate definition in the current corpus; the equation $\hbar = \int \mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{P04}}\,ds$ is a substrate-side **relabeling** of $\hbar$, not an independent computation. $\hbar$'s structural role (action-quantum coupling P04 ↔ P13) is identifiable; $\hbar$'s numerical value is empirically INHERITED. An independent substrate definition of $\mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{P04}}$ is OPEN.

### 3.5 Connection to chain-step participation in standard QM

The substrate-level identification of $\hbar$ with chain-step participation connects to several standard-QM facts:

**De Broglie wavelength** $\lambda = h/p$: a particle's wavelength equals $h$ divided by momentum. Substrate-level: the chain-step participation budget per unit momentum carry; particles with larger momentum traverse more chain-steps per unit substrate length, giving shorter wavelength.

### 3.6 Why $\hbar$ is small

The substrate scale $\ell_{\mathrm{ED}} \sim \ell_P$ is extremely small. The chain-step duration is $\tau \sim \ell_P/c \sim 10^{-43}$ s. The bandwidth-density $\mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{P04}}$ is correspondingly large per substrate cell to give the empirical $\hbar$.

Quantitatively: if $\mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{P04}}(s)$ is constant over a chain-step, then
$$\hbar = \mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{P04}} \cdot \tau \implies \mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{P04}} = \hbar/\tau \sim 10^{-34}/10^{-43} = 10^9 \,\text{J/s} = 10^9 \,\text{W} ,$$
i.e., gigawatt-level bandwidth density per substrate cell.

This is **structurally interpretable**: substrate cells operate at high bandwidth per cell but cells are extremely small in spacetime extent, giving a small per-step total. The macroscopic $\hbar$ is the time-integrated chain-step budget. *Note (per §3.3 circularity disclaimer): this numerical "check" is **tautological by construction** — it solves for $\mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{P04}}$ given $\hbar$ rather than computing $\hbar$ from $\mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{P04}}$; it is a relabeling, not a computation.*
