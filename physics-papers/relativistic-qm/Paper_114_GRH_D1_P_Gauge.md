## §3 Derivation

### 3.1 Case-P gauge classification

In the Arc-Q rule-type taxonomy, gauge rule-types fall into two classes:

- **Case-P (Propagating gauge):** Gauge rule-types that themselves carry substrate-level dynamics, propagating like fields. Examples: photon, gluon — gauge bosons with their own particle states.
- **Case-NP (Non-propagating gauge):** Gauge rule-types that exist only as connection structure on substrate bundles without independent particle excitations (e.g., topological gauge fields with no dynamical content).

The GRH D1 claim concerns **Case-P** specifically: at least one Case-P gauge rule-type has $\sigma = 0$.

### 3.2 Why Case-P gauge rule-types must include a massless instance

By T17 (Paper_015), gauge fields are connections on rule-type bundles. A Case-P gauge rule-type's substrate description involves:
1. A connection structure $A_\mu$ on the rule-type bundle.
2. A field-strength tensor $F_{\mu\nu} = \partial_\mu A_\nu - \partial_\nu A_\mu + \text{(non-Abelian terms)}$.
3. A propagating excitation (the gauge boson) whose dispersion relation is determined by the substrate-level dynamics under V1 kernel propagation.

The substrate-level **gauge invariance** requirement: the physical content of $A_\mu$ is unchanged under gauge transformations $A_\mu \to A_\mu - \partial_\mu \alpha(x)$ (in the $U(1)$ case; analogous for non-Abelian). This invariance is enforced by the T17 C8 substrate gauge-quotient (Paper_022).

For gauge invariance to be a substrate-level symmetry (not accidental), the substrate must support gauge rule-types whose **only physical degrees of freedom are transverse**. Two transverse polarizations correspond to two physical degrees of freedom.

A **massive** gauge rule-type would have three physical degrees of freedom (transverse + longitudinal), inconsistent with the gauge-quotient unless an explicit Higgs-like mechanism removes the longitudinal mode. Without such a mechanism, the substrate-level gauge invariance forces the gauge rule-type to be **massless**: $\sigma = 0$.

### 3.3 Why at least one massless Case-P exists

The substrate's structural requirement: P05 + P07 + P09 + P10 support the existence of a $U(1)$-valued polarity-transport structure (P05 + P09). By the GRH construction (Arc-Q), this minimal substrate gauge structure manifests as a Case-P gauge rule-type at the coarse-grained level.

For this Case-P rule-type to be gauge-invariant **without** requiring a Higgs-like mass-generation mechanism, it must be $\sigma = 0$. Hence at least one massless Case-P gauge rule-type **exists** at substrate level.

**Unconditional:** the conclusion does not depend on additional substrate primitives beyond P05, P07, P09, P10. The minimal $U(1)$ gauge structure alone forces the existence of at least one massless gauge rule-type. This is the "D1 unconditional" content of GRH.

### 3.4 Photon as the canonical realization

The standard $U(1)$ gauge boson — the photon — is the canonical empirical realization of the GRH D1 massless gauge rule-type. Its substrate-level description:
- $\sigma = 0$ rule-type (lightlike worldline, Paper_RQM_T10).
- Lorentz vector representation $(1/2, 1/2)$ (Paper_RQM_T7).
- Vacuum + one-particle dual structure (Paper_RQM_Q7Q8).
- Gauge-quotient restricts physical polarizations to two transverse modes (Paper_022).

Whether additional massless gauge rule-types exist (gluon, hypothetical extra gauge bosons) is **empirically determined**; GRH D1 only guarantees the existence of at least one.

### 3.5 Massive gauge bosons (W, Z, etc.)

W and Z bosons have $\sigma > 0$ (massive). Their substrate-level description involves a substrate-level Higgs-like mechanism that supplies the longitudinal mode. This is **consistent** with GRH D1: it requires at least one massless gauge rule-type, but allows additional massive gauge rule-types whose mass arises from Higgs-like substrate structure.

Substrate-level audit of the Higgs mechanism (electroweak symmetry breaking) is OPEN at first-principles level; the standard-model Higgs is consistent with ED but not currently derived from primitives.

### 3.6 Connection to Case-P / Case-NP dichotomy

The Case-P / Case-NP dichotomy distinguishes gauge rule-types by whether they support propagating particle excitations. Both classes inherit substrate-level gauge invariance from T17 + T17 C8.

- Case-P with $\sigma = 0$: photon-class.
- Case-P with $\sigma > 0$: W/Z-class (requires Higgs-like mechanism).
- Case-NP: topological gauge fields with no propagating excitations (e.g., Chern-Simons in 2+1D).

GRH D1 selects Case-P with $\sigma = 0$ as the unconditional existence statement.

### 3.7 Numerical content
