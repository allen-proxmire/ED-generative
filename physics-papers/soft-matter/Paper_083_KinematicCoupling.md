## Abstract

NS and MHD share a unified **kinematic coupling pattern** at substrate level. The pattern: (i) substrate-derived content lives in the dissipative + V5-cross-chain terms (R1 viscosity + V5 magnetic resistivity + V5 cross-channel transfer); (ii) transport-kinematic frame-artifacts (advection + kinematic induction, Paper_082) appear identically as convective-derivative terms. The pattern is FORM-FORCED by the same substrate→continuum coarse-graining (Paper_076 + Paper_081) and identical Galilean covariance (Paper_075 PUA-2). Cross-platform kinematic-coupling unification is FORCED; specific field-equation differences (presence vs absence of $B$, etc.) are platform-specific.

## §3 The Unified Pattern

### 3.1 Substrate-derived content

For both NS and MHD:
- **V1 dissipation:** kinematic viscosity ($u$-channel) and resistivity ($B$-channel) inherit from V1 second moment via Paper_076 + Paper_081.
- **V5 cross-channel transfer:** energy exchange between channels follows V5 cross-chain budget structure.

This substrate-derived content is **the same pattern** in NS and MHD; the channel count differs ($u$ only for NS; $u + B$ for MHD).

### 3.2 Frame-artifact content

For both NS and MHD:
- **Convective-derivative terms** $(u \cdot \nabla) u$ (NS), $\nabla \times (u \times B)$ (MHD) — same frame-artifact origin (Paper_082).

These terms are kinematic (frame-artifact), not substrate-derived.

### 3.3 Unification

By P-Unified-Kinematic-Coupling, NS and MHD share **one substrate-level kinematic coupling pattern**: substrate-derived + frame-artifact decomposition, identical across platforms.

### 3.4 What differs between NS and MHD

- Channel count: $u$-only (NS) vs $u + B$ (MHD).
- Field-equation form: NS has scalar pressure; MHD has Lorentz force.
- Phenomenology: NS supports vortex stretching; MHD supports magnetic reconnection.

These are **platform-specific** differences within the same substrate kinematic-coupling pattern.
