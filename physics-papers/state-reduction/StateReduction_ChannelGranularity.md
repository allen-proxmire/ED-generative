# Is ED's channel/pointer basis coarser than atomic wavepackets? — resolving the load-bearing residual

**2026-07-21. Companion to the state-reduction papers. Status: resolved grounded-in-kind (three corpus pillars), against the exclusion risk; two residuals named.** The emission-signature result (`StateReduction_CollapseRate_ED_Derivation.md` §"The emission signature") showed a committed charge is a dark state of ED's dissipative face — *conditional on* one load-bearing question the referee correctly flagged: an ordinary bound charge (spread ~Å–nm) must sit in a **single** channel/pointer eigenstate. If ED's channel basis were fine-grained toward the substrate scale `ℓ_ED`, ordinary matter would be a superposition of astronomically many channels, the channel-continuum limit would be reached, ED would radiate like CSL/DP, and it would be **excluded**. This note answers the question: **ED's pointer basis is the einselected, environment-set, coarse basis — not `ℓ_ED` — so the escape holds.** The answer rests on three standing corpus results, not on assumption.

## The question, sharply

Two scales must not be conflated:
- **`ℓ_ED`** (P08): the substrate grain — the *finest* structural resolution.
- **The pointer/channel basis**: the basis in which P11 commitment records outcomes — the `Π̂_α` of the FORCED jump operators `Ĵ_α = g_α Π̂_α` (`arcs/arc-Q/lindblad_extension.md`).

The emission escape needs the *second* scale to be coarse (an ordinary charge = one pointer state). The exclusion worry is that it might equal the *first* (`ℓ_ED`-fine). The resolution is that in ED these are **different scales**, and three standing results force the pointer basis to the coarse one.

## Pillar 1 — `ℓ_ED`-fine substrate dynamics is *coherent*, not measurement

ED's QM-emergence arc (Papers 001–012; Report §4; unitarity emergent) derives **unitary Schrödinger evolution** for an isolated system *from* the substrate. The substrate grain `ℓ_ED` is what *builds* the wavefunction as coherent participation structure — it is not a continuous position measurement of it. A charge's substrate-level evolution is coherent; the substrate does not collapse the charge to `ℓ_ED`-fine position cells, it evolves it as a wavefunction. So **the fine grain `ℓ_ED` does not act as a position-resolving environment on the charge.** This already breaks the "`ℓ_ED`-fine channels → continuum diffusion" picture at the root: were the substrate grain a continuous position measurement, ED would have no coherent QM at all — which contradicts its own QM-emergence result. The CSL/DP continuum limit is not merely unreached; it would contradict standing ED physics.

## Pillar 2 — the commitment/decoherence basis is *environmental*, hence the coarse robust basis (einselection)

The dynamics that decoheres a system and can radiate is not the substrate evolution but the **environmental** coupling. The corpus is explicit and consistent on this:
- `lindblad_extension.md` §4.2: the jump operator `Ĵ_α = g_α Π̂_α` projects onto "the channel `K*(α)` **selected by the commitment**," and the commitment is "**triggered by the environmental mode** α" coupling through the P04 four-band `b_K^{env}`. §6.2: the `L̂_α` are "environmental-bandwidth channels."
- `Paper_QuantumDarwinism_RecordBandwidth` §1: "Pointer basis = the arrow-selected commitment basis (**einselection is primitive**)"; imprinting/decoherence is "V5-mediated cross-chain correlation" with the *environment*.

So ED's pointer basis is **environment-selected einselection** — the standard mechanism. And einselection selects the **robust** basis (the predictability sieve): the *coarsest* basis stable under the environmental monitoring, not the finest. The environment (ambient photons, phonons) resolves position only coarsely; it cannot and does not select an `ℓ_ED`-fine basis. The pointer states are quasi-classical, environment-set, and coarse — by the same logic that makes them coarse in ordinary decoherence theory. **ED did not invent a finer pointer basis; it inherited the coarse einselected one.**

## Pillar 3 — commitment individuates only *distinguishable* channels, and distinguishability is *operational*

ED's own measurement account gates commitment on distinguishability: Report §4, "orthogonality reduces to **distinguishability**" — the arrow individuates two branches exactly when they become operationally distinguishable. And distinguishability in ED is **operational**, not substrate-omniscient: the A1 result measures it as *controlled channel capacity* (`Bits/docs/A1_ChannelCapacity_Results.md`), i.e. what can actually be resolved through channels. Operational distinguishability has a **coarse floor** — it is set by what the available channels resolve, far above `ℓ_ED`. So commitment cannot individuate below that floor: **ED's collapse is gated on distinguishability and simply does not localize to `ℓ_ED`-fine position.** This is the ED-native reason the pointer basis is coarse, and it is the same §4/A1 machinery the earlier residuals kept pointing to.

## The germanium case specifically (the actual 2020 experiment)

The bound charges in the germanium are electrons in atomic energy eigenstates. When the self-Hamiltonian (the binding, ~eV) dominates the collapse/gravitational coupling (tiny), einselection makes the **energy eigenbasis** the pointer basis (the standard Paz–Zurek result for a dominant self-Hamiltonian). An energy eigenstate is **stationary** — static charge density, no radiation, in ED or in ordinary QM. So:
- A bound charge in equilibrium is *already* a single pointer (energy) eigenstate → an exact dark state of the dissipative face → **no continuous radiation.** It is not a superposition of many fine position channels waiting to dephase.
- CSL/DP radiate precisely because their noise couples to **absolute position** `x̂`, which does *not* commute with the binding Hamiltonian, so it continuously drives the bound charge off its energy eigenstate — a perpetually jiggling charge. ED's commitment couples to the **einselected (energy) channel the charge already occupies**, so it does not excite it. That single operator difference — position `x̂` (CSL/DP) vs. the einselected channel projector `Π̂_α` (ED) — is the whole difference between radiating and not.

## Net

The load-bearing question resolves **against the exclusion risk**: ED's pointer/channel basis is the einselected, environment-set, distinguishability-gated coarse basis — never `ℓ_ED`. Ordinary and bound matter sit in single-channel pointer states (for bound charges, stationary energy eigenstates), which are dark states of the dissipative face, so there is no continuous spontaneous radiation. The channel-continuum limit that would return CSL/DP diffusion is not reached, and would contradict ED's own emergent unitarity. The risk downgrades from "could exclude ED" to "grounded escape."

## Two honest residuals

1. **The explicit ED theorem is not written.** The three pillars are standing/grounded ED results, but "the pointer basis is bounded below by the operational-distinguishability floor, which is `≫ ℓ_ED`" is assembled here from them, not proved as a single ED theorem with the floor quantified. Writing it (and locating the floor precisely — is it environment-set, or is there an intrinsic ED distinguishability floor above `ℓ_ED`?) is the remaining constructive work.
2. **Regime dependence.** The coarse basis is guaranteed for the *ambient/internal* environment of the germanium experiment (no sub-atomic-resolving probe). A hypothetical environment able to resolve sub-atomic position could push the pointer basis finer; the escape is not a universal guarantee for *every* conceivable environment, only for the ones these experiments actually sit in. This is honest scoping, not a hole for the 2020 bound.
