## The two standing ingredients (cited, not assumed)

1. **The khronon is the commitment-time, and it dilates with the field (Report §5, GR-I/GR-II).** ED's gravity is khronometric; the khronon is the arrow made dynamical, i.e. the substrate's direction of commitment. In the weak field `N = 1 + Φ/c²` — this is exactly gravitational time dilation, and in ED it is literally a **commitment-rate**: where the field is deeper, the arrow commits at a different rate (the sparse-commitment factor of Mass-Without-Mass §5 is this same clock).
2. **Einselection is commitment, and it individuates when branches become distinguishable (Report §4).** P11 commitment is the sole collapse primitive; it resolves a superposition into one channel (a superposition is never itself a commitment outcome). And "orthogonality reduces to distinguishability" — two would-be branches are individuated by commitment exactly when they become perfectly distinguishable. Commitment is irreversible and stochastic, so the outcome is a *definite, recorded* branch, not a mixture.

## The derivation

Put a mass in a superposition of two configurations, `1` and `2` (the standard lump-in-two-places setup). Each configuration sources its own bandwidth field, `b₁(x)` and `b₂(x)`, hence its own lapse `N_i ~ √b_i`, hence — because the khronon *is* the committed-time — **its own commitment-clock.** The two branches do not merely sit in different potentials; in ED they *commit time at different rates*:

$$\frac{d\tau_1}{dt} - \frac{d\tau_2}{dt} = N_1 - N_2 = \frac{\Phi_1 - \Phi_2}{c^2}.$$

The relative phase between two branches of energy `E_1, E_2` runs as `exp[-i(E_1-E_2)t/\hbar]`. The energy that differs between the two *gravitational* configurations — the energy stored in the difference of their committed-participation fields — is the gravitational self-energy of the difference of the mass distributions,

$$E_G = -G\!\int\!\!\int \frac{[\rho_1(\mathbf{x})-\rho_2(\mathbf{x})]\,[\rho_1(\mathbf{y})-\rho_2(\mathbf{y})]}{|\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{y}|}\,d^3x\,d^3y,$$

which is Penrose's `E_Δ`. So the two branches accumulate a **relative committed-time difference** at the rate `E_G/\hbar`. **Honest flag (C4):** this step *identifies* the branch-clock-difference energy with the known Penrose self-energy `E_G` rather than *computing* it from the ED participation fields — no integral is carried out showing the accumulated commitment-action equals `E_G τ`. What follows therefore reproduces the *scaling* `τ ∝ ℏ/E_G`; the coefficient and the exact energy functional (see the QuadraticStrain caveat below) are open.

Now the ED-specific step, and it is where this stops being Penrose. In standard QM this relative phase merely oscillates and the superposition persists forever. In ED it cannot: the arrow is a *single* committed time, and it is being asked to hold two branches whose committed-times run at *different rates*. It individuates them — commits, einselects one branch — as soon as they are commitment-**distinguishable**, i.e. when the accumulated relative commitment-action reaches the commitment quantum,

$$E_G\,\tau \sim \hbar \quad\Longrightarrow\quad \boxed{\;\tau \approx \frac{\hbar}{E_G}\;}.$$

**ED reproduces the `τ ∝ ℏ/E_G` scaling** (coefficient open, energy functional open per the flag above). But look at *why*, because that is where the real content is. Penrose gets the rate from the *ill-definedness* of a preferred time across the two spacetimes. ED gets it from the *presence* of one committed arrow-time that finds the two branches running on **different but well-defined** commitment-clocks and, being a single arrow, cannot carry both — so it commits. Penrose's "no fact about which time flows" becomes ED's "one arrow, two branch-clocks, forced to individuate." Note the quantitative content is still Penrose's (superposed configurations → differential time flow → energy scale → decay); ED's genuine addition is the *supplied preferred time* (the arrow), which turns his ambiguity into a mechanism. The advance is the preferred time, not a new number.

## The emission signature — does the 2020 bound exclude ED? No; the risk is retired (not a win)

The one live risk above was the 2020 Gran Sasso search (Donadi, Diósi, Curceanu, Laubenstein, Piscicchia, Bassi, *Nat. Phys.* **17**, 74 (2021); arXiv 2111.13490), which excluded the parameter-free Diósi–Penrose model by finding **no** spontaneous X-ray emission from germanium. Does ED's collapse emit that radiation? The honest answer: **ED is not excluded by this bound** — but that is *immunity*, not a positive result, and it comes with a residual that touches a standing ED finding.

**Where the DP radiation comes from.** The 2020 bound targets a *specific* mechanism: gravitational collapse implemented as a **continuous stochastic (Markovian Lindblad) process driven by a white-noise field** (Diósi's 1987 model is exactly this — a master equation whose damping term is sourced by a gravitational white noise). That continuous noise produces **momentum diffusion** of *every* particle at all times; a charge that is being continuously, randomly accelerated **radiates**, a faint but unavoidable spontaneous emission. The germanium search found none at the predicted level → the parameter-free version is excluded.

**The scope-limitation is real, but it is not the 2020 authors' caveat — it is later work.** The Donadi et al. paper itself states flatly that the result *rules out the natural parameter-free Diósi–Penrose model*, and attributes the diffusion→radiation mechanism directly to the Penrose proposal; it does not add a "this applies only to continuous-noise models" caveat. That scope-narrowing was established by the **follow-up literature** — analyses of the *effectiveness of the collapse* in the DP model (e.g. NJP 2024) and later work (arXiv 2512.15393) showing that a genuinely **instantaneous / one-time** Penrose-type reduction lies *outside* the class of theories the radiation experiment constrains (an instantaneous collapse does not cause the sudden ongoing localizations that source the diffusion). So the escape is real and literature-grounded — but it must be cited to that follow-up work, not put in the experimenters' mouths.

**Where ED sits.** ED's collapse is a **discrete which-channel commitment (einselection, P11)**, not a continuous white-noise diffusion. For an already-localized charge there is nothing to reduce — commitment returns *the same* channel, so it **locks, it does not kick** (Report §4: a committed channel is the most repeatable outcome). If that is the whole story, ED sits in the instantaneous-reduction class the follow-up work exempts, and is not excluded.
