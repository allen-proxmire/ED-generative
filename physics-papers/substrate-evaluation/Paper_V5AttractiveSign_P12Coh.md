## Abstract

V5, ED's cross-chain correlation kernel, is the program's highest-leverage keystone: it is inherited in the entanglement, dynamics, black-hole, and cosmology arcs and derived in none of them. Its home paper (Paper_090) already fixes most of its structure from the primitives, its existence as a P10 kernel, its retarded support from P11, and its gauge-covariant phase from P05 and P09, and leaves the *sign* of the coupling envelope open, the one open piece that decides whether V5 can bind at all. That sign must be attractive for V5 to bind nearby chains into a shared local clock, but across the corpus it is assumed.

This paper derives it, in two steps. First (the identification), Paper_090's own gauge law is a *difference* phase, $K_{V5}\to e^{i(\alpha_A-\alpha_B)}K_{V5}$, which is the fingerprint of a conjugated amplitude-pair moment $\langle P^A (P^B)^{*}\rangle$; the joint-amplitude *product* $P^AP^B$ transforms as the *sum* $e^{i(\alpha_A+\alpha_B)}$ and is a different object. The gauge-invariant content of that moment is the P05-connection-dressed phase overlap of the two chains, the coherence content of the cross-chain superposition, $\sqrt{b_Ab_B}\cos(\Delta\pi-\int\!\mathcal{A})$. So V5's coupling is the coherence content. Second (the build), coupling two clusters of real certified chains by that coherence content, entered into the certified selection functional with P12's own reward-coherence sign and with no hand-put attractive term, reproduces V5's characteristic signature: nearby chains lock to a shared proper-time rate while distant clusters keep their rate gap, and this collapses to a single universal tick only when the reach reaches the separation. Flipping to the anti-coherence sign removes the local lock at every reach. The attractive sign is therefore not a free assumption; it is inherited from P12's stability landscape rewarding coherence. Tier: measured, with the identification gauge-law-forced, conditional on P-Bipartite-Mapping and the like-chain reading. Existence stays a posit; the reach value stays inherited.

## 1. The keystone and its one open piece

Event Density reads the smooth structures of physics, spacetime, fields, constants, as the coarse-grained pattern made by discrete relational events. Two substrate kernels sit under most of the dynamics: V1, a single-chain vacuum-response kernel, and V5, a cross-chain correlation kernel. V5 carries an unusual load. It is the shared mechanism under the entanglement arc's non-factorizability, the dynamics arc's stress-energy form, the black-hole arc's Hawking-cutoff structure, the soft-matter arc's Maxwell relaxation, and, as the companion paper *The Relational Tick* shows, the binder that assembles nearby chains into a composite object with a single shared proper-time clock. It is postulated in each of these and characterized from the primitives in none. Closing any structural piece of V5 therefore has the widest reach of any single technical move in the program.

Most of V5's structure is in fact already pinned. What is not pinned, until this paper, is the one thing that decides whether V5 can do its job at all: the *sign* of its coupling. A binder must attract. If V5's cross-chain coupling pushed phases apart rather than together, there would be no local clocks, no composite rest frames, no bound matter. The synchronization study behind *The Relational Tick* established that the attractive sign is a *necessity condition*, V5 must be attractive to bind, but it assumed the sign rather than deriving it. This paper removes the assumption.

## 2. What Paper_090 fixes, and what it leaves open

Paper_090 defines V5 through the cross-chain correlation

$$
\langle X^A(u_A,t_A)\cdot Y^B(u_B,t_B)\rangle_{V5} = \int K_{V5}(u_A,t_A;u_B,t_B)\,\mathcal{Q}^{AB}_{X,Y}\,d\mu(\text{intermediate}),
$$

with the kernel

$$
K_{V5}(u_A,t_A;u_B,t_B) = \theta(t_A-t_B)\,F_{V5}\!\left(\frac{\sigma(u_A,u_B)}{\ell_{V5}^2},\,\frac{t_A-t_B}{\tau_{V5}}\right).
$$

Here $\theta$ is the Heaviside step, $F_{V5}$ is a bounded, decaying envelope, $\sigma$ is a substrate Synge-function analog, and $\mathcal{Q}^{AB}$ is the cross-chain participation content the kernel integrates against. Reading the paper's own attributions:

- **Existence** is a P10 posit. P10 licenses kernel rule-types beyond V1; it does not force them. V5 is on the same footing as V1 (§1.1, §3.5).
- **Retarded support**, the $\theta(t_A-t_B)$, is forced by the same P11 commitment-irreversibility argument that fixes V1's retardation (§3.4, §4.6).
- **The gauge phase** comes from P05 and P09: under a $U(1)$ transformation, $K_{V5}\to e^{i(\alpha(u_A)-\alpha(u_B))}K_{V5}$ (§4.3).
- **The reach and memory scales** $\ell_{V5}, \tau_{V5}$, and the specific envelope shape of $F_{V5}$, are inherited value-layer content (§3.3).

The one structural item with no derivation anywhere, and the one that decides whether V5 can bind, is the **sign** of the coupling. $F_{V5}$ is introduced as a general bounded envelope; nothing fixes whether the coupling attracts or repels. (The detailed envelope shape is also open, but it does not bear on the sign and is a separate thread.) The sign is the target.

## 3. The identification: V5's coupling is the coherence content

The envelope $K_{V5}=\theta F_{V5}$ is real, so it cannot be what carries the gauge phase; a real object cannot transform by $e^{i(\alpha_A-\alpha_B)}$. The phase must live in the participation content $\mathcal{Q}^{AB}$ that the kernel multiplies. (Paper_090 writes the gauge law on $K_{V5}$; we resolve that looseness the only internally-consistent way, by locating the phase in the content. This is the section's one interpretive step, and it is forced by consistency, not chosen.)

Now use the canonical participation amplitude $P^C = \sqrt{b_C}\,e^{i\pi_C}$ (P04 sets $|P|^2=b$, P09 sets the phase $\pi$), which transforms under the $U(1)$ as $P^C\to e^{i\alpha(u_C)}P^C$. Two bilinear objects can be built from the two chains' amplitudes, and they transform differently:

$$
P^A P^B \to e^{i(\alpha_A+\alpha_B)}P^AP^B \qquad(\text{sum phase}),
$$
$$
P^A (P^B)^{*} \to e^{i(\alpha_A-\alpha_B)}P^A(P^B)^{*} \qquad(\text{difference phase}).
$$

Paper_090's gauge law is the **difference** phase. That is a fingerprint. The product $P^AP^B$ is exactly Paper_063's factorizable joint amplitude $\Psi^{AB}_{KL}=P^A_K P^B_L$ (the bipartite *state*), and it carries the *sum* phase. So V5's coupling is **not** the joint-amplitude product. The object that carries the difference phase is the conjugated amplitude-pair moment $P^A(P^B)^{*}$ (unique up to complex conjugation: $(P^A)^{*}P^B$ carries $e^{-i(\alpha_A-\alpha_B)}$ and has the same real content), the substrate analog of a two-point correlation or propagator, which is what a correlation kernel between two chains should be. Writing it out,

$$
P^A(P^B)^{*} = \sqrt{b_A b_B}\;e^{\,i(\pi_A-\pi_B)}.
$$

Its modulus $\sqrt{b_A b_B}$ is manifestly gauge-invariant, and its phase $\Delta\pi=\pi_A-\pi_B$ is gauge-*covariant*: under the transformation it shifts by $\alpha_A-\alpha_B$. The gauge-invariant phase observable is therefore the P05-connection-dressed difference $\Delta\pi - \int_B^A\!\mathcal{A}$, in which the P05 connection $\mathcal{A}$ compensates the shift. This is the same P05 connection that transports the phase in the kernel, so P05 enters consistently in both roles. The gauge-invariant coherence content is then

$$
\mathrm{Coh}_{AB} = \sqrt{b_A b_B}\,\cos\!\Big(\Delta\pi - \int_B^A\!\mathcal{A}\Big),
$$

which reduces to $\sqrt{b_A b_B}\cos\Delta\pi$ in a fixed gauge or trivial-connection limit, and is the interference part of the superposition, $|P^A+P^B|^2-b_A-b_B$ up to the factor 2. This is the same functional that *Phase Coherence in ED* operationalizes for P12 and build-verifies as finite-reach, and it is exactly the connection-dressed coherence the build of §4 uses, whose holonomy $A_{ij}$ is the discrete $\int\!\mathcal{A}$. So **V5's phase-dependent coupling is the coherence content of the two chains' superposition**, modulated by the real reach-and-memory envelope $F_{V5}$.

This identification is the gauge-consistent one, forced among content bilinear in the two chains' amplitudes, conditional on three commitments, none invented here:

1. **The gauge law** $e^{i(\alpha_A-\alpha_B)}$, stated verbatim in Paper_090 §4.3.
2. **Bilinear cross-chain content**: V5 "acts on pairs of chains" (§3.2), and Paper_063 §3 builds the bipartite content from the two amplitudes ($\Psi^{AB}_{KL}=P^A_KP^B_L+\Delta^{AB}_{KL}$, the product piece being P-Bipartite-Mapping). Among content bilinear in the two amplitudes, the conjugated-pair moment is the unique object with the difference-phase transformation.
3. **The like-chain correlation reading**. A formal alternative also produces a difference phase: two *opposite-charge* chains multiplied as a product, $P^A P^B$ with $P^B\to e^{-i\alpha_B}P^B$, giving $\cos(\pi_A+\pi_B)$. That alternative is physically excluded, because $\cos(\pi_A+\pi_B)$ is maximal at anti-alignment $\pi_A=-\pi_B$ and would make chains repel, contradicting V5's binding role. The like-chain correlation ($\cos\Delta\pi$, maximal at alignment) is selected by V5's function, not assumed at random.

This is a far cleaner dependency than the program's earlier attempt to characterize V5 through Tsirelson's theorem, which was retracted because it needed measurement operators, a commuting tensor split, and a Born bridge that Paper_063 never builds. The present argument uses only what Paper_063 actually builds (the bilinear joint content) plus Paper_090's gauge law and the standard correlation reading. No operator algebra is invented.

## 4. The build: the attractive sign emerges from P12's coherence

The identification says V5's coupling is the coherence content. The attractive sign then has a candidate source that is not V5-specific: P12's stability landscape is $\Sigma=\mathrm{Coh}-\mathrm{Str}-\mathrm{Grad}$, in which the coherence term enters with a *positive* coefficient by the definition of P12. The positive coefficient attaches to whatever functional plays the coherence role; that the phase-coherence content of §3 is that role, rather than the certified density-based $\mathrm{Coh}=-(\rho-\rho^{*})^2$, is the operationalization established in *Phase Coherence in ED*. If V5's coupling is that coherence content, its sign is P12's sign. We test this directly on the certified substrate, taking care to put in no attraction by hand.

**Setup.** Two clusters of four chains each run on parallel lanes. Each chain is a real certified 1D chain: the advance-versus-dwell decision at each step is the certified $\Sigma$ (`compute_sigma`) verbatim, and natural rates come from the established memory mechanism, more carried memory means more dwelling means a slower rate. The two clusters run at different mean rates with a genuine within-cluster spread, a time-dilation setup: two composites at different proper-time rates, each with real internal spread that would drift apart uncoupled. Each front carries a $U(1)$ gauge phase $\phi$ that advances with its forward proper-time progress, a proxy in which $\phi$ tracks accumulated forward commitments (P09; Paper_090 §4.3; flagged as a modeling choice). The only cross-chain coupling is P12's coherence gradient, entered into the certified selection with P12's own sign:

$$
\text{sig}_{\text{advance}} \mathrel{+}= k_{c5}\sum_{j} w_{ij}\,\sin\!\big(\phi_j^{\text{ret}} + A_{ij} - \phi_i\big),
$$

where $w_{ij}=e^{-|\Delta y_{ij}|/\ell_{V5}}$ is the transverse reach weight (times a longitudinal window), $A_{ij}$ is a quenched P05 connection carrying substrate disorder, and $\phi_j^{\text{ret}}$ is the retarded (previous-step) neighbor phase (Paper_090 §4.6, P11). The $\sin$ is the gradient of $\cos$-coherence with respect to $\phi_i$: advancing moves a front up the coherence gradient, toward its neighbors' phase. The sign $k_{c5}>0$ is P12's reward-coherence sign. **No attractive term is put in.** Whether $+k_{c5}$ synchronizes is read off.

**A note on method, kept honest.** The first construction compared each choice's coherence against the neighbors' frozen last-step phase. At the aligned start that rewards phase *stasis*, holding still, rather than rate-matching, and the result was sign-independent: a null. The correct entry of "reward coherence" into a rate decision is the coherence *gradient* above, which is the exact derivative of the coherence and points a phase-behind front toward advancing. The reported runs use the gradient form. We record the null and the fix because ruling out the stasis artifact is part of trusting the result.

**Results** (three seeds, 2500 steps, cluster separation $D=20$). The uncoupled baseline has within-cluster rate spread $0.084$ and cross-cluster gap $0.243$.

| condition | reach $\ell_{V5}$ | within-spread (ratio to baseline) | cross-gap | reading |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **P12 sign $(+)$** | $2$ to $30$ ($<D$) | $0.022$ to $0.025$ (**0.27 to 0.30**) | $0.21$ to $0.24$ (**preserved**) | **local lock, time dilation intact** |
| **P12 sign $(+)$** | $100$ ($\ge D$) | $0.027$ (0.32) | **$0.036$ (collapsed)** | universal tick |
| **sign-flipped $(-)$** | all | $0.062$ to $0.065$ (0.74 to 0.78) | $\sim 0.21$ | **no lock at any reach** |
| **$(+)$, quenched disorder** | $2$ to $30$ | $0.023$ to $0.025$ (0.27 to 0.30) | preserved | local lock, disorder-robust |

Three readings. First, with P12's sign and a finite reach smaller than the separation, the within-cluster spread collapses by about 73 percent: nearby chains lock to a shared rate, a local proper time, while the two clusters keep their different rates, time dilation preserved. When the reach reaches the separation, everything locks and the gap collapses to a single universal tick, the wrong regime, confirming that the *finiteness* of the reach is load-bearing for time dilation. This is V5's characterized signature, emerging from $+k_{c5}$ alone. Second, flipping to the anti-coherence sign removes the local lock at every reach: the lock is specifically P12's positive coherence sign, not the coupling machinery or the reach structure. Third, adding the substrate's own quenched disorder to the P05 connection does not spoil the local lock; if anything it protects against the universal-tick collapse at long reach.

The small sign-independent within-spread reduction (ratio around 0.72) that appears in the flipped and short-reach runs is a uniform perturbation from the coupling breaking some ties. It is not synchronization: it has no reach dependence and does not preserve the gap structure. The genuine lock (ratio to 0.27 with the gap preserved and a sharp transition at $\ell_{V5}\approx D$) is only present for the positive sign at finite reach.

## 7. Falsifiers

- If Paper_090's gauge law were the *sum* phase $e^{i(\alpha_A+\alpha_B)}$ rather than the difference, the conjugated-moment identification would break and V5's coupling would be the joint-amplitude product, not the coherence content. It is the difference (§4.3).
- If the sign-flipped build had synchronized, the local lock would be an artifact of the coupling machinery and not of P12's sign. It did not; the flip destroys binding.
- If a substrate build showed the coherence coupling, under the like-chain reading, anti-binds or fails to give local-without-global synchronization, the derivation fails. It gives exactly local-without-global.
- Empirical falsifiers are inherited from the companion papers (the clock-binding and MOND-interference observational content); this paper adds none.
