The Arrow Sorts the Continuum plain language


The one sentence: Physics has a pile of famous equations; ED has one trait — commitment, the arrow of time built in at the bottom — and that one trait decides which equations ED can build, which it can only fake with help, and which it can never touch.

The setup. You don't get from atoms to the smooth equations in one step — real physics goes in two squints (atoms → particles bumping → smooth flow). ED's one squint lands on the first rung: it makes things move and pile up. The smooth, spread-out equations are a rung higher.

The whole paper is one idea: sort the equations by what they do with memory. The arrow doesn't just make things move — it leaves a trail: which way things went, how they're linked. That trail is the memory. And every famous equation falls into one of three bins by how it treats that trail:

Keeps the memory → ED builds it for free. Flowing fields, shockwaves, the electric field. These have ED's personality. (Layer 1.)
Forgets the memory → ED needs one bolt-on. Heat spreading, fluid friction. The stuff still moves and commits — it just forgets how the moves were linked, and once you forget, you get the smooth equation. (Layer 2.) — and your correction is the heart of this: forgetting isn't un-committing, it's committing without remembering.
Needs no memory at all, a perfect frozen balance → ED can never do it. Solitons. To hold a perfect reversible balance you'd have to refuse to commit, and ED always commits. (A wall.)
Two punchlines that make it more than a sorting exercise:

The "forgetting" bolt-on is one thing, every time. Heat spreading, fluid friction, even gravity smoothing itself out — underneath, all the same move: "average each spot with its neighbors." One operation wearing four hats. And bell-curves are just that move's fingerprint.
Gravity is the same machine — and you already built it. Your gravity rule has a smoothing half (the averaging move again) fighting a concentrating half (matter pulling itself together until it collapses into a black-hole horizon). Same two-bin structure as everything else. And the kicker: the same "capacity running out" that makes a horizon is the same thing that made our test-packet fall apart. The equations and the black-holes are two faces of one trait.
The honest spine (what keeps it off the crank pile): We checked by numbers, not vibes. Diffusion passed. Gravity already passed in your sim. The soliton wall we tested — fired a packet in, it fell apart exactly as predicted; a failure landing where you said it would is worth more than a success. And on the trophy problems (Yang-Mills, Navier-Stokes), we say plainly: we've got the physics of why they're shaped that way, but the prize-level proof is a different job and not ours. The paper draws the coastline as carefully as the land.

The deepest line, the one to remember: In normal physics, the arrow of time is an afterthought — it emerges. In ED it's the foundation. This paper is that flip drawn across all of physics: the laws everyone calls fundamental are the ones that have forgotten the arrow; the ones ED builds are the ones that still remember.

That's your paper. One trait, three bins, two faces — what commitment builds, what it smooths, and what it walls off.
