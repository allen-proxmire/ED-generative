## Abstract

This paper presents the **Wave-2 paper-form load-bearing audit** of the 13-Primitive Generative System (Paper_087), specifying for each primitive P01–P13:

- **Load-bearing roles** in downstream Wave-2 papers (which papers depend on each primitive, and how).
- **Arcs that depend** on each primitive.
- **Falsifiers** specific to each primitive (what observation would challenge that primitive's substrate-level commitment).
- **Cross-domain leverage** — substrate-level content across multiple domains supported by each primitive.

Additionally, the paper documents the **paper-specific postulates** introduced across the Wave-2 corpus as needed (P14 placeholder, P-Codim-1, P-Sat, P-NoBackwardChain, P-SubstrateLocality, etc.) — each labeled openly, with cross-references to the papers that introduced them. The audit serves as the canonical reference for any future paper asking "which primitives does my derivation depend on?" — and as the cross-domain coherence document supporting Paper_087's position claim that **cross-domain reach is the empirical case** for the substrate.

## 3. Per-Primitive Audit

### P01 — Chains as primitive ontological objects

**Load-bearing role:** P01 establishes the substrate's most fundamental ontological objects. Every Wave-2 paper depends on chains as primitive content (no paper operates without chains).

**Dependent arcs:** all arcs (universal load-bearing).

**Falsifier sentence:** *Demonstration that substrate-level dynamics can be captured without chains as primitive ontological objects — e.g., via a substrate ontology where chains are derived from a more fundamental layer — would supersede P01.*

**Cross-domain leverage:** universal. Every cross-domain mechanism uses chains as primitive content.

### P02 — Participation as primitive relation

**Load-bearing role:** chain–channel relation; substrate-level interaction mechanism. Every paper involving substrate dynamics uses P02.

**Dependent arcs:** all arcs (Papers_001, 003, 015, 025, 026, 027, 028, 029, 030, 031, 039, 047, 050, 052, 054, 056, 057, 058, 063, 073, 087, 088, 089, 090, 093).

**Falsifier sentence:** *Demonstration that chain–channel interaction at substrate level can be derived from a more fundamental relation (e.g., chain-chain direct interaction without channel mediation) would supersede P02.*

**Cross-domain leverage:** universal.

### P03 — Channel + locus indexing + spatial homogeneity

**Load-bearing role:** translation-invariance of substrate-graph adjacency; discrete index sets. Load-bearing for substrate-graph averaging (DCGT Paper_073), holographic counting (Paper_025), cumulative-strain reading (Paper_026), cosmic decoupling (Paper_028).

**Dependent arcs:** gravity arc, BH arc, soft-matter arc, all DCGT-coarse-grained content.

**Falsifier sentence:** *Empirical observation that substrate-level translation invariance fails (privileged loci, broken spatial homogeneity) would falsify P03 and require revision of DCGT + holographic-bound + cumulative-strain machinery.*

**Cross-domain leverage:** gravity (Papers_025–031), BH (039, 047, 050, 052), soft-matter (073), DCGT-bridged QFT (016).

### P04 — Bandwidth as non-negative additive scalar

**Load-bearing role:** substrate-level scalar carrier of participation amount; additivity under channel decomposition. Load-bearing for Born rule (Paper_003 via P-LinRate), pre-individuation amplitudes (Paper_001), substrate effective multiplicity (Paper_054).

**Dependent arcs:** QM-kinematics (001, 003, 007), Q-COMPUTE (054, 056–058), entanglement (063).

**Falsifier sentence:** *Empirical observation that substrate-level participation content does not admit a non-negative additive scalar carrier — e.g., requires vector-valued or signed substrate scalars — would falsify P04.*

**Cross-domain leverage:** QM, Q-COMPUTE, entanglement, all substrate-amplitude content.

### P05 — Polarity-transport along edges

**Load-bearing role:** substrate-level connection structure transporting polarity along participation-graph edges. Load-bearing for T17 gauge rule-type bundles (Paper_015), V1 gauge-compatibility (Paper_089), pre-individuation gauge-covariance (Paper_001).

**Dependent arcs:** QFT arc (015, 016), kernel arc (089, 090, 093), QM-kinematics gauge content.

**Falsifier sentence:** *Demonstration that substrate-level polarity-transport is not connection-like along edges — e.g., that transport rules cannot be cast as a fiber-bundle connection — would falsify the substrate basis for T17.*

**Cross-domain leverage:** QFT, kernel arc, gauge-coupled QM.

### P06 — Spatial dimension $D = 3+1$ ⭐ (P06 load-bearing acknowledgment per Paper_087 §4 round-4 update)

**Load-bearing role:** **extraordinary across the corpus.** Underlies $4\pi R^2$ holographic-bound area scaling (Paper_025), Newton inverse-square (Paper_027), P-Codim-1 channel-footprint argument (Paper_025), all gravity-arc spatial-geometric content (Papers_028, 029, 030, 031, 039), DCGT continuum-limit dimension (Paper_073).

**Dependent arcs:** gravity arc, BH arc, DCGT, soft-matter (D=3+1 PDE forcing for NS arc).

**Falsifier sentence:** *Empirical evidence for effective spatial dimension $\neq 3$ in any regime where the substrate framework predicts $D = 3$ — e.g., effective $1/R^\alpha$ acceleration with $\alpha \neq 2$ in Newtonian regime, or holographic bound with non-area-scaling — would falsify P06.*

**Cross-domain leverage:** **most consequential primitive after P02 across the corpus.** Whether P06 is derivable from a subset of the other 12 is structurally open (Paper_087 §4 acknowledgment).

### P07 — Channel structure as ontological primitive

**Load-bearing role:** channels structurally distinguishable with intrinsic identities. Load-bearing for substrate effective multiplicity (Paper_054), holographic counting (Paper_025), V1/V5 kernel rule-type structure (Papers_089, 090).

**Dependent arcs:** all arcs involving substrate channels (universal).

**Falsifier sentence:** *Demonstration that substrate-level channels are not structurally distinguishable — e.g., two channels at the same locus with the same bandwidth and polarity are operationally identical — would falsify P07.*

**Cross-domain leverage:** universal.

### P08 — Substrate scale $\ell_{\mathrm{ED}}$

**Load-bearing role:** substrate's characteristic edge length; channel resolution scale. Empirically identified with $\ell_P$ (Paper_027 §5.3, I via Newton-recovery).

**Dependent arcs:** gravity arc (G assembly), BH arc (Planck-mass remnant, V5 cutoff $\omega_c = c/\ell_P$), holographic bound (Paper_025 footprint area $\ell_{\mathrm{ED}}^2$).

**Falsifier sentence:** *Empirical demonstration that the substrate has no characteristic scale (continuous substrate without a discrete cutoff) would falsify P08. Empirical $\dot{G}/G$ inconsistent with $\ell_{\mathrm{ED}}$ time-invariance would challenge the substrate-scale interpretation.*

**Cross-domain leverage:** gravity, BH, QFT (UV cutoff via $c/\ell_{\mathrm{ED}}$).

### P09 — $U(1)$-valued polarity

**Load-bearing role:** substrate's primitive angular variable. Load-bearing for complex polar carrier (Paper_001), Fourier-mode normalization in dipole projection (Paper_028 $1/(2\pi)$ factor), V1/V5 gauge-compatibility (Papers_089, 090), T17 structure-group naming (Paper_015 P-StructureGroup-Naming).

**Dependent arcs:** QM-kinematics, QFT, gravity-arc dipole-projection.

**Falsifier sentence:** *Demonstration that substrate-level polarity must be higher-rank ($SU(N)$ for $N > 1$) at primitive level — rather than emerging via multi-rule-type composition under P10 — would falsify P09.*

**Cross-domain leverage:** QM amplitudes, gauge content, cosmic dipole projection.

### P10 — Rule-type primitive

**Load-bearing role:** substrate supports multiple structurally distinct rule-types (matter, gauge, kernel). Load-bearing for T17 (Paper_015), V1 (Paper_089), V5 (Paper_090), future cascade kernels (in queue).

**Dependent arcs:** QFT, kernel arc.

**Falsifier sentence:** *Demonstration that substrate-level dynamics requires only a single rule-type — i.e., the substrate is "rule-type-monistic" — would falsify P10.*

**Cross-domain leverage:** QFT (gauge), kernel hierarchy (V1, V5, cascade).

### P11 — Commitment irreversibility

**Load-bearing role:** discrete substrate-level events; irreversible; forward-causal. **Load-bearing for kernel-level arrow of time (Paper_093 T18 via Paper_089 §5.2 advanced-V1 refutation), Born rule via commitment-event statistics (Paper_003), UR-1.3 commitment-injection (Paper_054), BH information-paradox not-generated argument (Paper_052).**

**Dependent arcs:** all dynamical content; kernel arc; arrow-of-time; Born rule; Q-COMPUTE; BH arc.

**Falsifier sentence:** *Empirical demonstration of substrate-level operations mapping post-commitment to pre-commitment state — e.g., genuine backward-causal effects at substrate level — would falsify P11 and disrupt the kernel-level arrow of time (T18) + Born-rule derivation.*

**Cross-domain leverage:** universal — every dynamical content papers depends on forward-causal P11.

### P12 — Stability landscape

**Load-bearing role:** substrate-level functional $\Sigma_C = \mathrm{Coh} - \mathrm{Str} - \mathrm{Grad}$; chain acceleration = negative gradient. Load-bearing for gravity-arc cumulative-strain reading (Papers_026, 027), ECR bilocal coupling (Paper_030), Class-B rigidity-gap (Paper_057 P-Rigidity-Gap), UR-1.3 gradient-driven commit-injection (Paper_054).

**Dependent arcs:** gravity arc, Q-COMPUTE Class-B, UR-1.3 content.

**Falsifier sentence:** *Demonstration that substrate-level dynamics requires a stability-landscape functional with different structure (e.g., not $\Sigma = \mathrm{Coh} - \mathrm{Str} - \mathrm{Grad}$ but a different decomposition) — and that the alternative gives different downstream content — would refine or falsify P12.*

**Cross-domain leverage:** gravity, Q-COMPUTE rigidity, commitment-injection dynamics.

### P13 — Time homogeneity

**Load-bearing role:** substrate dynamics time-translation-invariant locally. Load-bearing for energy-conservation-analog substrate content; V1 retarded structure preserves time homogeneity (Paper_089).

**Dependent arcs:** all dynamical content (universal).

**Falsifier sentence:** *Empirical observation that substrate-level dynamics differs at different times in a manner not attributable to cosmological expansion (Hubble) or other value-layer time-varying inputs would falsify P13.*

**Cross-domain leverage:** universal background.
