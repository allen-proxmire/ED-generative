## 1. What We Postulate (the 13 axioms, stated baldly)

The Event Density (ED) Generative Primitives System consists of the following 13 primitive structural commitments. They are **postulated**, not derived. The empirical case for them is the downstream reach developed in §§3–6.

### 1.1 Existence and participation primitives

- **P01 (Event-density layer existence).** A pre-quantum substrate exists as a primitive structural layer. The substrate is not a Hilbert space, not a smooth manifold, not a field theory — it is a discrete graph-like structure equipped with primitive scalar, relational, and angular quantities.

- **P02 (Participation as primitive relation).** Chains (persistent micro-event sequences) participate in channels as a primitive substrate-level relation. Participation is the substrate's mechanism for chain-channel interaction.

### 1.2 Channel and locus structure

- **P03 (Channel + locus indexing; spatial homogeneity).** The substrate supplies a discrete index set of channels $\mathcal{K}$ and a (discrete or continuous) index set of loci, with primitive translation-invariance: shifting all loci by a common displacement leaves the participation-graph adjacency structure intact.

- **P06 (Spatial dimension $D = 3+1$).** The substrate's spatial axis is $\mathbb{R}^3$, plus one time dimension.

- **P07 (Channel structure as ontological primitive).** Channels are structurally distinguishable carriers of substrate content, with identities intrinsic to the participation graph rather than derived from external organizational choice.

### 1.3 Bandwidth and polarity primitives

- **P04 (Bandwidth as non-negative additive scalar, with four-band partition).** Each channel $K$ at each locus $u$ carries a real-valued non-negative quantity $b_K(u) \geq 0$, additive under channel decomposition. Bandwidth further decomposes into four mutually orthogonal substrate-level bands: internal, adjacency, environmental, commitment-reserve (P04 §1.5).

- **P09 ($U(1)$-valued polarity).** Each channel carries an angular variable $\pi_K(u) \in U(1) \cong S^1$, the substrate's unique angular primitive.

### 1.4 Transport, scale, and dynamical primitives

- **P05 (Polarity-transport between adjacent loci).** The substrate-level connection structure — mechanism by which polarity at one locus is transported to a neighboring locus along edges of the participation graph.

- **P08 (Substrate scale $\ell_\mathrm{ED}$).** The substrate has a characteristic edge length $\ell_\mathrm{ED}$, identified empirically with the Planck length $\ell_P$ via Newton-recovery.

- **P10 (Rule-type primitive).** The substrate supports multiple structurally distinct rule-types $\tau_\bullet$, each with its own participation measure. Matter and gauge are *different rule-types*, not different states of the same one.

- **P11 (Commitment with environmental phase-randomization, irreversible).** Discrete substrate-level events at which a chain's multi-channel participation collapses to a single channel, with phase-randomization on a uniform $U(1)$ distribution. Commitment is irreversible — no substrate-level dynamics maps a post-commitment state back to its pre-commitment superposition.

- **P12 (Stability landscape).** Substrate-level functional $\Sigma = \mathrm{Coh} - \mathrm{Str} - \mathrm{Grad}$ whose extrema govern chain dynamics. Carries the substrate-level coherence, strain, and gradient content for gravitational physics.

- **P13 (Time homogeneity; primitive event-discreteness).** The substrate's dynamical primitive is invariant under time translation; the substrate has no preferred absolute time origin. Events on the substrate are primitively discrete (the manifold is not infinitely refinable).

## 2. Why This Set (parsimony, minimality, internal consistency)

The 13 primitives are not derived from a deeper layer. They are chosen by three criteria, none of which constitutes a derivation:

### 2.1 Parsimony

Each primitive corresponds to a distinct operational category — there is no redundancy. P04 (real non-negative scalar) is structurally distinct from P09 ($U(1)$-valued angular). P03 (spatial-translation symmetry) is distinct from P13 (time-translation symmetry). P07 (channel-structure) is distinct from P10 (rule-type capacity). The 13 are minimal in the sense that removing any one primitive breaks at least one downstream derivation (see §8 audit + the separate `PRIMITIVE_LOAD_BEARING_AUDIT.md`).

The 13 are *not* minimal in some absolute sense — there may be primitive sets with fewer elements that generate the same downstream content. We do not claim otherwise. The 13 are the working set found adequate for the program's current cross-domain reach.

### 2.2 Internal consistency

The 13 primitives compose without contradiction. P04 (bandwidth) composes with P09 (polarity) to produce the complex polar participation measure $P_K = \sqrt{b_K}\,e^{i\pi_K}$. P03 + P13 (spatial-temporal homogeneity) compose to support Stone's theorem on the participation manifold's unitary one-parameter groups. P05 (polarity-transport) + P07 (channel structure) compose to support gauge-field structure. P11 (commitment irreversibility) composes with P05 to produce the kernel-level arrow of time.

We have not found contradictions among the 13. We do not claim this proves their truth — it shows that the set is at least internally coherent, which is the minimum a primitive system must satisfy.

### 2.3 Cross-domain reach

The 13 primitives generate substantial structure across multiple domains: QM kinematics, gauge-field theory, gravitational dynamics, soft-matter rheology, black-hole architecture, and quantum-computing multiplicity walls. §§3–7 develop this reach. The cross-domain reach is the strongest empirical argument for the set: a primitive system that generates only one domain may be over-fitted; a primitive system that generates many domains is doing genuine structural work.

This is not derivation. It is the same kind of case Newton, Einstein, Schrödinger, Dirac, and the operational-reconstruction tradition make for their respective axiom sets. The judgment is downstream — on what the axioms generate — not upstream on where the axioms come from.

ED does not derive $H_0$ from substrate constants; $H_0$ is empirical input. The substrate prediction $a_0 = cH_0/(2\pi)$ ties the transition acceleration to $H_0$, but $H_0$ itself is not derived.

ED treats $D = 3+1$ as an empirical-necessity primitive (P06). Deeper structural forcing of the spatial dimension — via anthropic, holographic, or dynamical-stability arguments — is an open problem in physics generally, not within ED's current scope.

### 7.6 Clay-problem solutions

The substrate-level analysis produces *substrate-positive verdicts* (Intermediate Path C for NS smoothness; structural-positive for YM Clay-relevance) — these are partial-structural-positive results, not constructive proofs.

### 7.7 General relativity / Einstein equations

ED produces Newton's law + $a_0$ + BTFR at the substrate level (gravity arc); it does *not* produce the full Einstein equations or curved-spacetime gravity in the dynamical-geometric sense. ### 7.8 Anthropic, multiverse, or "fine-tuning" arguments

The 13-primitive Generative Primitives System is offered as an empirical hypothesis. Each of the following observations would falsify the framework at a specific load-bearing point:

### 8.3 Failure of slope-4 BTFR

High-precision measurement of BTFR slope inconsistent with $4.000$ at the deep-MOND asymptotic regime would falsify §4.3. Note: this is currently consistent with data but not yet decisively tested.

### 8.4 Failure of Class-A multiplicity wall

Matter-wave interferometry experiments at $\geq 250$ kDa that *succeed* in producing interference (inconsistent with the predicted substrate-level multiplicity cutoff) would falsify §6.4.

### 8.5 Zero-mass black-hole evaporation endpoint

Empirical evidence (e.g., gamma-ray-burst signatures inconsistent with Planck-mass remnant evaporation, or other observational tests) that black-hole evaporation completes to zero mass would falsify §6.5.

### 8.6 Empirical observation refuting a primitive

- Discovery of genuinely $\mathbb{Z}_n$-valued (non-continuous) phase at the substrate level would falsify P09.
- Discovery of non-additive bandwidth content would falsify P04.
- Discovery of reversible commitment events would falsify P11.

### 8.7 Internal-consistency failure

Discovery of a contradiction among the 13 primitives — e.g., a downstream structure that simultaneously requires and refutes the same primitive — would falsify the framework's internal consistency claim of §2.2.
