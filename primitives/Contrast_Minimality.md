# Contrast — Minimality Analysis (Open Question 4 resolution note)

**Documentation note. Not a corpus edit, not an ontology change, not a new primitive.**
Companion to the P11 content-decomposition clarification (`P11_commitment.md`). Records the resolved status of the Facts paper's Open Question 4.
**Source basis:** `position-paper/paper_ED_Contrast_First_Ontology.md` (§2 definition, §5 the two "teeth", Open Question 4); primitives P02, P04, P05, P06, P09, P11.

---

## Abstract

The Facts paper ("When Contrast Becomes Fact") names **contrast** as ED's ontological primitive and flags, in its own Open Question 4, whether contrast is genuinely a single primitive or "a working consolidation of distinct substrate notions (gradient, participation, polarity)." We apply the minimality test the paper itself fixes in §2 — *a primitive earns its place only by forbidding something* (a non-vacuous restriction not derivable from other primitives). Under that test, contrast introduces no forbidding the certified primitives do not already underwrite, and every operational guise of contrast is supplied by an existing primitive. **Verdict: contrast is a consolidation (an ontological heading), operationally derivative — not a fourteenth primitive.** This strengthens, rather than weakens, the contrast-first ontology.

## 1. Operational definition (corpus-grounded)

The Facts paper §2 defines contrast as *"the minimal unit of difference: a gradient between densities, a sign-flip across a boundary, a participation pattern that distinguishes one channel from another, a change of state from one moment to the next… a substrate-level quantity."* This yields four corpus-grounded guises:

- **gradient between densities** — signed event-density difference across adjacency;
- **polarity / sign difference** — orientation or phase difference across a boundary;
- **participation asymmetry** — a channel pattern distinguishing one channel from another;
- **temporal before/after contrast** — a state-change across a fixing event.

## 2. Each aspect is already supplied by a certified primitive

| Contrast aspect (Facts §2) | Operational content | Supplied by | New irreducible capacity? |
|---|---|---|---|
| gradient between densities | signed density difference; discrete edge form `(∇ρ)_uv = b_uv(ρ_v − ρ_u)` | **P06** (ED gradient ∇ρ), composing **P04** (bandwidth) + ρ scalar over **P03** adjacency | No — a *derived* edge quantity |
| polarity / sign difference | U(1) orientation/phase difference, transported across loci | **P09** (U(1) polarity) + **P05** (polarity-transport) | No |
| participation asymmetry | channel-to-channel distinction; bandwidth differences | **P02** (participation) + **P04** (bandwidth) | No |
| temporal before/after | state-change across a commitment | **P11** (commitment) | No |

Every guise is the difference-carrying content of a primitive ED already certifies. None introduces an operation the certified set lacks.

## 3. Minimality test — the two teeth are theorems, not new axioms

The paper's criterion (§2): a primitive must **forbid** something not already forbidden. The candidate forbiddings are the two "teeth" of §5. Applying the test:

| Tooth (what contrast forbids) | Entailed by | New axiom? |
|---|---|---|
| **T1** — no determinate micro-fact in isolation | **P02** (commitment is selection *among channels*, hence requires participation) + **P11** | **No** — §5 states T1 *"is already encoded in ED's machinery."* |
| **T2** — no determinate macro-value without global participation | global participation web (**P02**) + the holistic/global-relational structure (constants as web-equilibria; value-inherited methodology) | **No** — a structural consequence of the global architecture, not a local forbidding |

Both restrictions are genuine, but each is a **theorem about existing primitives**, not a prohibition that a "contrast" primitive must supply. By the paper's own test, contrast therefore fails the condition for primitivehood: it underwrites no forbidding the certified primitives do not already underwrite.

A stronger reduction — letting contrast *replace* P02/P04/P05/P06/P09 and lower the primitive count — also fails: these primitives are operationally distinct and non-interchangeable (e.g., P09's phase is selection-blind where P04's bandwidth is not), so collapsing them into one token would lose operational content. Contrast names their shared difference-aspect; it does not merge them.

## 4. Verdict

| Hypothesis | Result |
|---|---|
| **H1 — genuine primitive** (irreducible, forbids something new) | **Rejected** |
| **H2 — consolidation** (one ontological heading over P02/P04/P05/P06/P09/P11; adds no commitment; does not reduce the count) | **Accepted — best fit** |
| **H3 — derivative** (any contrast magnitude is computed from existing primitive state) | **True at the operational register** |

**Contrast is a consolidation — an ontological heading — operationally derivative, and not a primitive.** It neither adds an irreducible capacity nor reduces the certified count. This resolves Open Question 4 in the direction the paper anticipates.

## 5. Implications (no commitments — observations only)

- **The contrast-first ontology remains intact.** It is an ontological *re-description* of the existing thirteen primitives, not a fourteenth. Introducing no new commitment is precisely what keeps it disciplined.
- **The two teeth are stronger as theorems than as axioms.** Being entailed by {P02, P11, global architecture}, the prohibitions rest on derivation, not assertion.
- **Commitment (P11) is the load-bearing mechanism.** The single thing contrast-first genuinely adds over the relational tradition is the determinateness operation — and that is a *certified primitive*, not "contrast." Contrast names what ED's relational primitives already are; commitment turns that contrast into fact.
- **No new primitives are introduced.** The ontology stands on the certified thirteen.
