# P07 — Channel structure as ontological primitive

**Canonical primitive of the ED Generative System.**
**Position paper reference:** `position-paper/paper_ED_Framework_13_Primitive_Generative_System.md` §1.2.

---

## Canonical statement

Channels are structurally distinguishable carriers of substrate content, with identities intrinsic to the participation graph rather than derived from external organizational choice.

P07 is the **ontological-priority** commitment that channels are not labels imposed on a structureless substrate; they have substrate-level structural identity prior to (and independent of) any observer or coordinate choice.

## Relationship to P03

P03 supplies the *indexing* (channels and loci as index sets, with translation invariance); P07 supplies the *ontological status* (channels as substrate-primitive carriers, not derived labels). The two are conceptually distinct: a substrate could in principle have indexable structure without that structure being ontologically primitive. P07 is the commitment that channel structure is *both* indexable *and* ontologically irreducible.
