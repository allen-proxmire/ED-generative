## §5 Falsification Criteria

- **F1: $a_0$ independent of $H_0$.** If empirical detection shows that $a_0$ does **not** vary with $H_0$ (including no co-variation under Hubble-tension-class $H_0$ shifts between local-universe and early-universe), the azimuthal-Fourier projection of $R_H$ to $a_0$ (§3.2) fails. This refutes the SCBU-boundary identification for $a_0$.

- **F2: BTFR slope ≠ 4.** If empirical galactic-rotation-curve data show the BTFR slope to deviate significantly from 4 in the deep-MOND regime, P-BTFR-Slope-4 (Paper_031) fails, and the IR-regime scaling-law content (§3.3) collapses. The MOND IR-regime ↔ SCBU mapping then fails at the scaling-law level.

- **F3: Deep-MOND limit fails.** If empirical evidence shows that the $\mu(x) \to x$ asymptotic (P-Deep-MOND-Limit, Paper_034) fails in the $|\nabla\Phi| \ll a_0$ regime — e.g., a sharp cutoff at some intermediate acceleration scale, or a different asymptotic form — the IR-regime mapping fails.

- **F4: SCBU postulate refuted.** If P-Substrate-Cosmology-Unified is refuted by an independent substrate-level derivation (the universal failure mode for the ED-SC 4.x arc), this paper's structural correspondence loses its anchor.

- **F5: IR-regime scaling inconsistent with kernel cascade.** If a substrate-level derivation of the kernel cascade (Paper_091) shows the IR regime to be incompatible with the deep-MOND IR-galactic projection (§3.1), the cascade-continuity step fails. Specifically: if Paper_091's kernel cascade structure precludes a smooth transition from V5-modulated hydrodynamic regime to deep-MOND galactic-acceleration regime, the SCBU-IR projection collapses.

Refutation of any of F1–F5 falsifies this paper's structural correspondence without necessarily refuting the constituent papers (Papers_029–034 remain individually intact at their own verdict levels; only the SCBU-IR projection-extension is refuted).
