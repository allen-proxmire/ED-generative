## §5 Falsification Criteria

- **F1: Any SCBU projection fails to occupy its predicted regime.** If empirical / structural evidence shows that one of the six projections lives in a different RG regime than assigned (e.g., $Q \approx 3.5$ inherits anchoring from substrate UV-scale rather than IR-V5-saturation, contradicting §3.3 placement), the unified four-regime structure fails for that projection. Multiple-projection failures of this kind refute the unified model.

- **F2: $\xi_{\mathrm{canonical}}$, $a_0$, $\mathcal{M}_{\mathrm{crit}}$, $Q$, $r_H/R_H$ fail synchronized co-variation.** The load-bearing testable prediction of the SCBU framework (IR-1 in §3.4) is that all six projections co-vary together under $H_0$ shifts (Hubble-tension-class). Empirical detection of asynchrony — e.g., $a_0$ shifts with $H_0$ but $Q$ does not, or $\mathcal{M}_{\mathrm{crit}}$ remains constant while $\xi_{\mathrm{canonical}}$ shifts — refutes the unified-boundary anchoring claim for the asynchronous projection.

- **F3: Kernel cascade discontinuity at cosmological regime.** Continuity 3 (§3.5) is load-bearing: the IR-to-cosmological transition must be smooth under P-Cascade-Continuity. A substrate-level discontinuity at this transition (kernel-content jump as $r \to R_H$) refutes the cascade-continuity assumption and the SCBU framework's structural integration.

- **F4: SCBU postulate refuted.** Universal failure mode for the ED-SC 4.x arc: if P-Substrate-Cosmology-Unified is refuted by an independent substrate-level derivation, the unified model loses its central postulate and the whole four-regime structure must be re-derived.

- **F5: Independent cross-scale invariance model contradicts this one.** If a substrate-level analysis produces a cross-scale invariance model that places the six projections in **different** RG-regime cells than §3.3 specifies, then either this paper's placement is wrong, or the alternative model is wrong, or both rest on inconsistent substrate-content assumptions. Either way, the unified four-regime model would need revision.

Refutation of F1–F5 falsifies the unified ED-SC 4.x synthesis while leaving the individual extension papers (Papers_ED_SC_4.1–4.5) potentially intact at their own M3 verdict levels — only the **integration** is refuted, not necessarily each individual projection.
