By I-030, the substrate-level composition of accelerations under substrate-gravity is the geometric mean $a = \sqrt{a_N \cdot a_0}$, where $a_0$ enters as the substrate-level scale fixing the geometric-mean partner.

By I-031, the BTFR slope-4 relation $v^4 = G M a_0$ (deep-MOND regime) requires $a_0$ to appear as a fixed proportionality scale in the substrate-gravity composition. By I-037 (P-H0-Cosmological-Invariant, inherited), substrate-level cosmological-rate parameters are not subject to local coarse-graining rescaling. Applied to $a_0$ via I-033 + I-034 substrate-MOND machinery: $a_0$ is a substrate-level invariant acceleration scale.

The structural role of $a_0$ — substrate-invariant acceleration scale entering ECR + BTFR + MOND-interpolation — is FORCED by the composition of upstream content.

### 3.5 Value INHERITED (I-row)

The numerical magnitude $a_0 \approx 1.2 \times 10^{-10}\,\mathrm{m/s^2}$ is fixed by the cosmological-rate identification $a_0 = c H_0 / (2\pi)$ (Paper_029) combined with empirical Hubble-tension-range matching to galactic-rotation observations.
